## Benefits

- **Monitor legal exposure continuously instead of reacting after the fact.** Sentinel watches configured sources for decisions, enforcement activity, legislation, guidance, and legal developments that could affect tracked matters or portfolios.

- **Filter signal through actual client and matter context.** Attorneys see alerts tied to jurisdictions, entities, practice areas, and tracked holdings rather than a generic stream of legal news.

- **Standardize triage.** Each alert includes severity, urgency, reasoning, and a recommended action so teams can decide quickly whether to investigate, notify, or dismiss.

- **Manage sources in the same place you review alerts.** The monitoring workspace includes source setup, portfolio mapping, and alert handling in one interface.

- **Keep sensitive portfolio context private.** Client names, matter mappings, and alert logic stay inside your own environment.

## Features

- Monitoring workspace with Alerts, Signals, Portfolio, and Sources tabs
- Source registry supporting RSS feeds, webpages, and APIs
- Practice-area, jurisdiction, and entity matching
- Alert scoring with severity, urgency, and recommended action
- Signal and alert detail views for deeper review
- Portfolio registry with matter-level context
- Manual polling and sync controls from the UI
- Status workflow for new, acknowledged, dismissed, and actioned alerts

## When to use it

- You need ongoing monitoring for regulatory, litigation, or enforcement exposure
- You want external developments tied to a tracked client or matter portfolio
- You need a shared queue for alert triage and follow-up
- You want sources, matching logic, and alerts managed in one place

## How it works

1. Open **Sources** and register the feeds, webpages, or APIs you want monitored
2. Define the tracked portfolio with matter, entity, and jurisdiction context
3. Let Sentinel ingest signals or trigger a manual poll
4. Review matched alerts with reasoning and recommended next steps
5. Acknowledge, dismiss, or action alerts as the team works the queue
