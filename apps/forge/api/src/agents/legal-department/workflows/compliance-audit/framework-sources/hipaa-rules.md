# HIPAA — Key Compliance Requirements

Paraphrased requirement summaries for RAG ingestion. Each section represents
a distinct compliance area chunked at rule/standard boundaries for retrieval quality.

---

## Privacy Rule — Use and Disclosure Standards

Covered entities may use or disclose protected health information (PHI) only as permitted or required by the Privacy Rule. PHI may be used or disclosed for treatment, payment, and health care operations without individual authorization. Most other uses and disclosures require written authorization from the individual. The minimum necessary standard requires that covered entities make reasonable efforts to limit PHI to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose of the use, disclosure, or request.

Key requirements:
- Identify all uses and disclosures of PHI
- Obtain written authorization for uses and disclosures not otherwise permitted
- Apply minimum necessary standard to all uses and disclosures
- Implement policies and procedures for minimum necessary determinations
- Document routine and recurring disclosures and limit to minimum necessary

## Privacy Rule — Individual Rights

Individuals have the right to access their PHI maintained in a designated record set, with limited exceptions. Covered entities must act on access requests within 30 days. Individuals have the right to request amendments to their PHI. Individuals have the right to receive an accounting of disclosures of their PHI made in the six years prior to the request. Individuals have the right to request restrictions on uses and disclosures of their PHI. Individuals have the right to request confidential communications by alternative means or at alternative locations.

Key requirements:
- Process for handling access requests within 30 days (one 30-day extension permitted)
- Ability to provide PHI in the form and format requested if readily producible
- Process for handling amendment requests and documenting denials
- Ability to provide accounting of disclosures for the prior six years
- Process for evaluating restriction requests
- Ability to accommodate reasonable requests for confidential communications

## Privacy Rule — Notice of Privacy Practices

Covered entities must provide individuals with a notice of their privacy practices. The notice must describe how the entity may use and disclose PHI, the individual's rights regarding their PHI, the entity's legal duties with respect to PHI, and who to contact for further information or to file a complaint. The notice must be provided at the first service delivery and must be made available at the entity's premises and on its website.

Key requirements:
- Notice of Privacy Practices includes all required elements
- Notice provided to individuals at first point of service
- Notice posted at physical premises
- Notice posted on entity's website
- Good faith effort to obtain written acknowledgment of receipt
- Notice updated when privacy practices change

## Privacy Rule — Administrative Requirements

Covered entities must designate a privacy official responsible for developing and implementing privacy policies and procedures. They must designate a contact person for receiving complaints. They must train all workforce members on privacy policies and procedures. They must have appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect PHI. They must have a process for individuals to file complaints. They must not intimidate or retaliate against individuals who exercise their rights or file complaints.

Key requirements:
- Designated privacy official with defined responsibilities
- Designated contact person for complaints
- Privacy training for all workforce members
- Training provided within reasonable time of joining
- Sanctions policy for workforce members who violate privacy policies
- Documentation of all privacy policies and procedures
- Retention of documentation for six years from date of creation or last effective date

## Security Rule — Administrative Safeguards

Covered entities must implement administrative actions, policies, and procedures to manage the selection, development, implementation, and maintenance of security measures to protect electronic PHI (ePHI). This includes conducting a risk analysis to identify threats and vulnerabilities, implementing a risk management program, implementing a sanction policy for workforce members, implementing procedures to regularly review information system activity, assigning security responsibility to a specific individual, implementing workforce access management procedures, implementing security awareness and training programs, and developing contingency plans for responding to emergencies.

Key requirements:
- Comprehensive risk analysis identifying threats and vulnerabilities to ePHI
- Risk management program to reduce risks to reasonable and appropriate levels
- Sanction policy for security violations
- Regular review of information system activity (audit logs, access reports)
- Designated security official
- Workforce access management based on role and function
- Security awareness training for all workforce members
- Contingency plan including data backup, disaster recovery, and emergency mode operations
- Periodic evaluation of security policies and procedures

---

## Security Rule — Physical Safeguards

Covered entities must implement physical measures, policies, and procedures to protect electronic information systems and related buildings and equipment from natural and environmental hazards and unauthorized intrusion. This includes facility access controls to limit physical access to electronic information systems, workstation use policies defining proper functions and physical attributes of workstations, workstation security measures to restrict access to authorized users, and device and media controls governing the receipt and removal of hardware and electronic media.

Key requirements:
- Facility access controls limiting physical access to ePHI systems
- Facility security plan documenting safeguards
- Access control and validation procedures for facilities
- Maintenance records for physical security measures
- Workstation use policies defining appropriate use
- Workstation security preventing unauthorized physical access
- Device and media disposal procedures ensuring ePHI is removed
- Media re-use procedures ensuring ePHI is removed before re-use
- Accountability records for hardware and media movement
- Data backup before moving equipment

## Security Rule — Technical Safeguards

Covered entities must implement technical policies and procedures for electronic information systems that maintain ePHI to allow access only to authorized persons or software programs. This includes unique user identification, emergency access procedures, automatic logoff, and encryption and decryption. Audit controls must be implemented to record and examine activity in information systems containing ePHI. Integrity controls must protect ePHI from improper alteration or destruction. Authentication measures must verify that a person or entity seeking access is who they claim to be. Transmission security measures must guard against unauthorized access to ePHI being transmitted over electronic communications networks.

Key requirements:
- Unique user identification for all system users
- Emergency access procedures for ePHI in emergencies
- Automatic logoff after period of inactivity
- Encryption of ePHI at rest and in transit
- Audit controls recording access and activity in ePHI systems
- Mechanism to authenticate ePHI integrity
- Person or entity authentication before granting access
- Transmission security including encryption for ePHI in transit

## Security Rule — Organizational Requirements

Covered entities must ensure that any business associate that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits ePHI on behalf of the covered entity enters into a business associate agreement. The agreement must establish the permitted and required uses and disclosures of PHI, require the business associate to implement appropriate safeguards, require reporting of security incidents, and ensure that the business associate's subcontractors agree to the same restrictions and conditions.

Key requirements:
- Business associate agreements with all entities handling ePHI
- Agreements include all required provisions
- Business associate agreements require appropriate safeguards
- Business associate agreements require breach reporting
- Agreements address subcontractor requirements
- Process for monitoring business associate compliance

## Breach Notification Rule — Notification Requirements

A covered entity must notify affected individuals following the discovery of a breach of unsecured PHI. Notification must be provided without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 days from discovery. If a breach affects 500 or more individuals, the covered entity must also notify the Secretary of Health and Human Services and prominent media outlets serving the affected area. If a breach affects fewer than 500 individuals, the entity must maintain a log and submit it annually to the Secretary. Business associates must notify covered entities of breaches without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 days from discovery.

Key requirements:
- Breach detection and investigation procedures
- Risk assessment to determine if breach notification is required
- Individual notification within 60 days of discovery
- Notification includes required content (description, types of information, protective steps, entity actions, contact procedures)
- Notification to HHS for breaches affecting 500 or more individuals
- Notification to media for breaches affecting 500 or more individuals in a jurisdiction
- Annual log submission for breaches affecting fewer than 500 individuals
- Business associate notification to covered entity within 60 days
- Documentation of all breach assessments and notifications

## Breach Notification Rule — Unsecured PHI

Unsecured PHI is PHI that is not rendered unusable, unreadable, or indecipherable to unauthorized persons through the use of a technology or methodology specified by the Secretary. The specified technologies and methodologies are encryption of ePHI consistent with NIST standards and destruction of PHI such that it cannot be reconstructed. PHI that has been properly encrypted or destroyed is not considered unsecured PHI and is not subject to breach notification requirements.

Key requirements:
- Encryption of ePHI using NIST-approved methods to avoid breach notification
- Secure destruction of PHI rendering it unreadable and not reconstructable
- Documentation of encryption and destruction methods used
- Assessment of whether PHI involved in an incident was secured or unsecured

## Enforcement Rule — Penalties and Compliance

Civil monetary penalties for HIPAA violations are structured in tiers based on the level of culpability. Tier 1 covers violations where the entity did not know and could not reasonably have known. Tier 2 covers violations due to reasonable cause and not willful neglect. Tier 3 covers violations due to willful neglect that are corrected within 30 days. Tier 4 covers violations due to willful neglect that are not corrected within 30 days. Criminal penalties may also apply for knowingly obtaining or disclosing PHI in violation of the rules.

Key requirements:
- Understanding of penalty tiers and potential financial exposure
- Compliance program to demonstrate reasonable diligence
- Prompt correction of identified violations (within 30 days)
- Documentation of compliance efforts and corrective actions
- Cooperation with HHS investigations and compliance reviews
