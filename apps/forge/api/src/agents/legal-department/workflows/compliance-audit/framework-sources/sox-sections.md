# SOX — Key Compliance Requirements

Paraphrased requirement summaries for RAG ingestion. Each section represents
a distinct compliance area chunked at section boundaries for retrieval quality.

---

## Section 302 — Corporate Responsibility for Financial Reports

The principal executive officer and principal financial officer of each issuer must certify in each annual or quarterly report that: they have reviewed the report; based on their knowledge, the report does not contain any untrue statement of material fact or omit a material fact necessary to make the statements not misleading; based on their knowledge, the financial statements fairly present in all material respects the financial condition and results of operations; the signing officers are responsible for establishing and maintaining internal controls; they have designed internal controls to ensure material information is made known to them; they have evaluated the effectiveness of internal controls within 90 days prior to the report; and they have presented their conclusions about effectiveness of internal controls in the report.

Key requirements:
- CEO and CFO personal certification of financial reports
- Certification that report contains no material misstatements or omissions
- Certification that financial statements fairly present financial condition
- Responsibility for establishing and maintaining internal controls
- Internal controls designed to surface material information to signing officers
- Evaluation of internal control effectiveness within 90 days of report
- Disclosure of significant deficiencies and material weaknesses to auditors and audit committee
- Disclosure of any fraud involving management or employees with significant internal control roles

## Section 404 — Management Assessment of Internal Controls

Management must include in each annual report an internal control report that states management's responsibility for establishing and maintaining an adequate internal control structure and procedures for financial reporting, and contains an assessment of the effectiveness of the internal control structure and procedures as of the end of the most recent fiscal year. The registered public accounting firm that prepares or issues the audit report must attest to and report on the assessment made by management.

Key requirements:
- Documented internal control framework (typically COSO)
- Management assessment of internal control effectiveness annually
- Documentation of all significant processes, risks, and controls
- Testing of key controls for operating effectiveness
- Identification and classification of control deficiencies
- Remediation plans for identified deficiencies
- External auditor attestation of management's assessment
- Documentation sufficient for external auditor to evaluate

---

## Section 404(b) — External Auditor Attestation

The external auditor must evaluate management's assessment of internal controls and issue an independent opinion on the effectiveness of internal controls over financial reporting. The auditor must identify significant deficiencies and material weaknesses. A material weakness is a deficiency or combination of deficiencies such that there is a reasonable possibility that a material misstatement of the annual or interim financial statements will not be prevented or detected on a timely basis.

Key requirements:
- External auditor independently evaluates internal controls
- Auditor tests controls in addition to reviewing management's assessment
- Auditor identifies significant deficiencies and material weaknesses
- Adverse opinion issued if material weakness exists
- Auditor's report publicly filed with SEC
- Communication of all identified deficiencies to management and audit committee

## Section 409 — Real-Time Issuer Disclosures

Each issuer must disclose to the public on a rapid and current basis such additional information concerning material changes in the financial condition or operations of the issuer as the Commission determines is necessary or useful for the protection of investors and in the public interest. Such disclosures must be in plain English and may include trend and qualitative information and graphic presentations.

Key requirements:
- Process for identifying material changes requiring rapid disclosure
- Disclosure controls and procedures for timely public reporting
- Plain English presentation of material changes
- Monitoring systems for detecting material events
- Defined escalation procedures for potential material events
- Documentation of disclosure decisions and timing

## Section 802 — Criminal Penalties for Document Destruction

It is unlawful to knowingly alter, destroy, mutilate, conceal, cover up, falsify, or make a false entry in any record, document, or tangible object with the intent to impede, obstruct, or influence the investigation or proper administration of any matter within the jurisdiction of any department or agency of the United States. Any accountant who conducts an audit of an issuer must maintain all audit or review workpapers for a period of five years from the end of the fiscal period in which the audit or review was concluded.

Key requirements:
- Document retention policies covering financial records and audit workpapers
- Five-year minimum retention for audit and review workpapers
- Seven-year retention recommended as best practice
- Policies prohibiting destruction of documents relevant to investigations
- Litigation hold procedures when investigations are anticipated
- Electronic records management including email retention
- Training on document retention obligations
- Sanctions for unauthorized destruction of records

## Section 906 — Corporate Responsibility for Financial Reports (Criminal)

The chief executive officer and chief financial officer of each issuer must certify that the periodic report containing financial statements fully complies with the requirements of the Securities Exchange Act and that information contained in the periodic report fairly presents in all material respects the financial condition and results of operations of the issuer. Knowing violations carry penalties of up to one million dollars in fines and up to ten years imprisonment. Willful violations carry penalties of up to five million dollars in fines and up to twenty years imprisonment.

Key requirements:
- CEO and CFO criminal certification of financial reports
- Certification of full compliance with Securities Exchange Act requirements
- Certification that financial information fairly presents financial condition
- Understanding of criminal penalty exposure for false certifications
- Processes to ensure accuracy of certified financial information
- Documentation supporting the basis for certifications

## Section 301 — Public Company Audit Committees

Each member of the audit committee must be an independent member of the board of directors. The audit committee must be directly responsible for the appointment, compensation, and oversight of the work of any registered public accounting firm employed by the issuer. The audit committee must establish procedures for receiving, retaining, and addressing complaints regarding accounting, internal accounting controls, or auditing matters, including procedures for confidential, anonymous submission by employees.

Key requirements:
- Audit committee composed entirely of independent directors
- At least one member qualifies as a financial expert
- Direct responsibility for external auditor appointment and oversight
- Procedures for receiving and investigating accounting complaints
- Whistleblower procedures including anonymous submission channels
- Authority to engage independent counsel and other advisors
- Adequate funding for audit committee activities
- Regular meetings with external and internal auditors

## Section 303 — Improper Influence on Conduct of Audits

It is unlawful for any officer or director of an issuer, or any other person acting under their direction, to take any action to fraudulently influence, coerce, manipulate, or mislead any independent public or certified accountant engaged in the performance of an audit of the financial statements of that issuer for the purpose of rendering such financial statements materially misleading.

Key requirements:
- Policies prohibiting improper influence on auditors
- Auditor independence protections
- Communication channels between auditors and audit committee
- Procedures for auditors to report attempts at improper influence
- Training for management on auditor independence requirements

---

## Section 806 — Whistleblower Protections

No publicly traded company may discharge, demote, suspend, threaten, harass, or in any other manner discriminate against an employee because the employee provided information or assisted in an investigation regarding conduct the employee reasonably believes constitutes a violation of federal securities laws, SEC rules and regulations, or any provision of federal law relating to fraud against shareholders. Protected activities include providing information to a federal regulatory or law enforcement agency, any member or committee of Congress, or a person with supervisory authority over the employee.

Key requirements:
- Whistleblower protection policy prohibiting retaliation
- Multiple reporting channels including anonymous options
- Investigation procedures for reported concerns
- Protection from discharge, demotion, suspension, threats, or harassment
- Training for management on whistleblower protections
- Documentation of all reports and investigation outcomes
- Remedies available to employees who experience retaliation

## Section 201 — Services Outside the Scope of Practice of Auditors

It is unlawful for a registered public accounting firm to provide certain non-audit services to an issuer contemporaneously with the audit. Prohibited services include bookkeeping, financial information systems design and implementation, appraisal or valuation services, actuarial services, internal audit outsourcing, management functions, human resources functions, broker-dealer services, and legal services unrelated to the audit. The audit committee may approve other non-audit services that are not prohibited.

Key requirements:
- Inventory of all services provided by the external audit firm
- Prohibition of specified non-audit services during audit engagement
- Audit committee pre-approval of permissible non-audit services
- Disclosure of fees paid to audit firm for audit and non-audit services
- Policies ensuring auditor independence
- Regular assessment of auditor independence

---

## Section 203 — Audit Partner Rotation

The lead audit partner and the reviewing audit partner must rotate off the audit engagement after five consecutive fiscal years. A rotated partner may not return to the engagement for five fiscal years following rotation.

Key requirements:
- Tracking of audit partner tenure on engagement
- Rotation of lead and reviewing partners after five years
- Five-year cooling-off period before partner may return
- Succession planning for audit partner transitions
- Consideration of mandatory firm rotation policies
