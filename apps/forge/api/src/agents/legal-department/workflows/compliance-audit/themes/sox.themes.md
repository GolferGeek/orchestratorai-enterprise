# SOX Compliance Themes

## Theme: CEO and CFO Certification
Sections: 302, 906
Questions:
- Do the CEO and CFO personally certify each annual and quarterly financial report?
- Do certifications confirm the report contains no material misstatements or omissions?
- Do certifications confirm financial statements fairly present financial condition?
- Are signing officers responsible for establishing and maintaining internal controls?
- Are internal controls designed to surface material information to signing officers?
- Are significant deficiencies and material weaknesses disclosed to auditors and the audit committee?
- Is any fraud involving management or employees with significant internal control roles disclosed?

## Theme: Internal Controls Over Financial Reporting
Sections: 404
Questions:
- Is there a documented internal control framework such as COSO?
- Does management assess internal control effectiveness annually?
- Are all significant processes, risks, and controls documented?
- Are key controls tested for operating effectiveness?
- Are control deficiencies identified, classified, and tracked?
- Are remediation plans in place for identified deficiencies?
- Is documentation sufficient for external auditor evaluation?

## Theme: External Auditor Attestation
Sections: 404(b)
Questions:
- Does the external auditor independently evaluate internal controls?
- Does the auditor test controls in addition to reviewing management's assessment?
- Does the auditor identify significant deficiencies and material weaknesses?
- Is the auditor's report publicly filed with the SEC?
- Are all identified deficiencies communicated to management and the audit committee?
- Is an adverse opinion issued when a material weakness exists?

## Theme: Real-Time Disclosure
Sections: 409
Questions:
- Is there a process for identifying material changes requiring rapid disclosure?
- Are disclosure controls and procedures in place for timely public reporting?
- Are material changes presented in plain English?
- Are monitoring systems in place for detecting material events?
- Are escalation procedures defined for potential material events?
- Are disclosure decisions and their timing documented?

## Theme: Document Retention
Sections: 802
Questions:
- Are document retention policies in place covering financial records and audit workpapers?
- Is the five-year minimum retention period for audit and review workpapers observed?
- Are policies in place prohibiting destruction of documents relevant to investigations?
- Are litigation hold procedures in place when investigations are anticipated?
- Does electronic records management include email retention requirements?
- Are employees trained on document retention obligations?
- Are sanctions in place for unauthorized destruction of records?

## Theme: Audit Committee Independence
Sections: 301
Questions:
- Is the audit committee composed entirely of independent directors?
- Does at least one member qualify as a financial expert?
- Is the audit committee directly responsible for external auditor appointment and oversight?
- Are procedures in place for receiving and investigating accounting complaints?
- Are whistleblower procedures in place including anonymous submission channels?
- Does the audit committee have authority to engage independent counsel and advisors?
- Is adequate funding provided for audit committee activities?
- Does the audit committee meet regularly with external and internal auditors?

## Theme: Auditor Independence
Sections: 201, 203, 303
Questions:
- Is there an inventory of all services provided by the external audit firm?
- Are prohibited non-audit services excluded during the audit engagement?
- Does the audit committee pre-approve all permissible non-audit services?
- Are fees paid to the audit firm for audit and non-audit services disclosed?
- Are lead and reviewing audit partners rotated after five consecutive years?
- Is the five-year cooling-off period observed before a rotated partner may return?
- Are policies in place prohibiting improper influence on auditors?
- Are communication channels maintained between auditors and the audit committee?

## Theme: Whistleblower Protection
Sections: 806
Questions:
- Is there a whistleblower protection policy prohibiting retaliation?
- Are multiple reporting channels available including anonymous options?
- Are investigation procedures in place for reported concerns?
- Are employees protected from discharge, demotion, suspension, threats, or harassment for reporting?
- Is management trained on whistleblower protection requirements?
- Are all reports and investigation outcomes documented?
- Are remedies available to employees who experience retaliation?

## Theme: Financial Reporting Accuracy
Sections: 302, 401, 409
Questions:
- Are financial statements prepared in accordance with applicable accounting standards?
- Are off-balance-sheet transactions and obligations properly disclosed?
- Are pro forma financial presentations reconciled to GAAP measures?
- Is there a formal process for reviewing financial statements before certification?
- Are accounting estimates and judgments documented and reviewed?
- Is there a process for identifying and correcting errors in financial reporting?

## Theme: Risk Assessment and Monitoring
Sections: 302, 404
Questions:
- Is there a formal risk assessment process for financial reporting risks?
- Are risks identified, evaluated, and prioritized?
- Are risk mitigation controls mapped to identified risks?
- Is the risk assessment updated when business conditions or operations change?
- Are emerging risks monitored and assessed on an ongoing basis?
- Is the risk assessment process documented and reviewed by management?

## Theme: IT General Controls
Sections: 404
Questions:
- Are access controls in place for financial reporting systems?
- Is there a formal change management process for financial systems?
- Are system development and acquisition controls documented?
- Are computer operations controls in place (job scheduling, backup, recovery)?
- Are IT general controls tested as part of the internal controls assessment?
- Is segregation of duties enforced in financial reporting systems?
- Are automated controls within financial applications identified and tested?

## Theme: Entity-Level Controls
Sections: 302, 404
Questions:
- Is there a tone at the top that emphasizes ethical behavior and compliance?
- Is there a formal code of conduct or ethics policy?
- Are entity-level controls assessed as part of the internal controls evaluation?
- Is the control environment evaluated including integrity, ethical values, and competence?
- Is there an internal audit function or equivalent monitoring activity?
- Are communication channels in place for reporting control issues to management?

## Theme: Anti-Fraud Controls
Sections: 302, 404, 802
Questions:
- Is there a formal fraud risk assessment process?
- Are anti-fraud controls designed and implemented based on identified fraud risks?
- Does management override testing cover journal entries, estimates, and business rationale for unusual transactions?
- Are fraud reporting mechanisms in place and communicated to employees?
- Is there a process for investigating allegations of fraud?
- Are the results of fraud investigations reported to the audit committee?

## Theme: Disclosure Controls and Procedures
Sections: 302
Questions:
- Are disclosure controls and procedures designed to ensure material information reaches decision-makers?
- Is there a disclosure committee or equivalent responsible for evaluating disclosure obligations?
- Are disclosure controls evaluated for effectiveness at least quarterly?
- Are the results of disclosure control evaluations reported to the CEO and CFO?
- Is there a process for capturing and reporting subsequent events?
- Are disclosure controls documented and tested?

## Theme: Remediation and Continuous Improvement
Sections: 302, 404
Questions:
- Is there a formal process for remediating identified control deficiencies?
- Are remediation timelines established and monitored?
- Are material weaknesses and significant deficiencies tracked through remediation?
- Is the effectiveness of remediation actions validated through re-testing?
- Are lessons learned from identified deficiencies incorporated into control design?
- Is there an ongoing process for monitoring and improving the internal control environment?
