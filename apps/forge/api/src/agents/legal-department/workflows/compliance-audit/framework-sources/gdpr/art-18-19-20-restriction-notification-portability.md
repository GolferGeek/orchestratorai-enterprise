# GDPR Articles 18-20 — Restriction, Notification, and Portability

## Article 18 — Right to Restriction of Processing

### Article 18(1) — Grounds for Restriction

The data subject has the right to obtain from the controller restriction of processing where one of the following applies:

#### (a) Accuracy Contested
The accuracy of the personal data is contested by the data subject, for a period enabling the controller to verify the accuracy of the data.

#### (b) Unlawful Processing, Erasure Opposed
The processing is unlawful and the data subject opposes erasure and requests restriction instead.

#### (c) Controller No Longer Needs Data
The controller no longer needs the personal data for processing purposes, but the data subject requires it for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.

#### (d) Pending Objection Verification
The data subject has objected to processing under Article 21(1), pending verification of whether the controller's legitimate grounds override those of the data subject.

### Article 18(2) — Processing Under Restriction

Where processing has been restricted, such personal data shall, with the exception of storage, only be processed:
- With the data subject's consent
- For the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims
- For the protection of the rights of another natural or legal person
- For reasons of important public interest of the Union or a Member State

### Article 18(3) — Lifting Restriction

A data subject who has obtained restriction of processing must be informed by the controller before the restriction is lifted.

### Technical Implementation

Restriction may be implemented through:
- Temporarily moving data to another processing system
- Making data unavailable to users
- Temporarily removing published data from a website
- Flagging data in the system to indicate restricted status

## Article 19 — Notification Obligation Regarding Rectification, Erasure, or Restriction

The controller shall communicate any rectification or erasure of personal data or restriction of processing carried out in accordance with Articles 16, 17(1), and 18 to each recipient to whom the personal data has been disclosed, unless this proves impossible or involves disproportionate effort.

The controller shall inform the data subject about those recipients if the data subject requests it.

### Implementation Requirements
- Maintain records of all recipients to whom data has been disclosed
- Implement procedures for notifying recipients of rectification, erasure, or restriction
- Be prepared to provide the list of recipients to the data subject upon request
- Document cases where notification was not made due to impossibility or disproportionate effort

## Article 20 — Right to Data Portability

### Article 20(1) — Right to Receive Data

The data subject has the right to receive the personal data concerning them, which they have provided to a controller, in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and has the right to transmit that data to another controller without hindrance from the controller to which the data has been provided, where:

#### (a) Lawful Basis
The processing is based on consent under Article 6(1)(a) or Article 9(2)(a), or on a contract under Article 6(1)(b).

#### (b) Automated Means
The processing is carried out by automated means.

### Article 20(2) — Direct Transmission

In exercising their right to data portability, the data subject has the right to have the personal data transmitted directly from one controller to another, where technically feasible.

### Article 20(3) — Relationship to Erasure

The exercise of the right to portability shall be without prejudice to Article 17. The right to portability does not automatically trigger erasure.

### Article 20(4) — Rights of Others

The right to data portability shall not adversely affect the rights and freedoms of others.

### Scope of Portability

Data covered by the portability right includes:
- Data actively and knowingly provided by the data subject (e.g., form submissions, account information)
- Data generated by the data subject's activity (e.g., search history, location data, transaction records)

Data NOT covered:
- Data derived or inferred by the controller (e.g., credit scores, user profiles, analytics results)
- Data provided by third parties about the data subject

### Format Requirements

"Structured, commonly used, and machine-readable" formats include:
- CSV, JSON, XML for structured data
- Industry-standard formats where available (e.g., vCard for contacts, iCalendar for events)
- The controller is not required to adopt or maintain processing systems that are technically compatible with other controllers

## Enforcement

Violations of Articles 18-20 are subject to the higher tier of administrative fines under Article 83(5)(b): up to EUR 20,000,000 or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher.
