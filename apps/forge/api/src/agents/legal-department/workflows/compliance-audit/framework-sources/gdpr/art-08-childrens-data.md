# GDPR Article 8 — Conditions Applicable to Child's Consent in Relation to Information Society Services

## Article 8(1) — Age Threshold

Where Article 6(1)(a) applies (consent as lawful basis) in relation to the offer of information society services directly to a child, processing is lawful where the child is at least 16 years old. Where the child is below age 16, processing is lawful only if and to the extent that consent is given or authorized by the holder of parental responsibility over the child.

Member States may provide by law for a lower age for consent purposes, provided the age is not below 13 years. Member State age thresholds include:
- 16 years: Germany, Netherlands, Luxembourg, Croatia, Lithuania
- 15 years: Czech Republic, France, Greece, Slovenia
- 14 years: Austria, Bulgaria, Italy, Spain, Portugal
- 13 years: Belgium, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, Latvia, Malta, Poland, Sweden, United Kingdom (pre-Brexit)

"Information society service" means any service normally provided for remuneration, at a distance, by electronic means, and at the individual request of a recipient of services. This includes social media platforms, online gaming, streaming services, and e-commerce but excludes offline services.

## Article 8(2) — Verification Obligations

The controller shall make reasonable efforts to verify in such cases that consent is given or authorized by the holder of parental responsibility over the child, taking into consideration available technology.

Verification measures may include:
- Email verification to a parent or guardian
- Credit card verification (as a proxy for adult status)
- Digital identity verification services
- Video call verification
- Providing a consent form requiring parental signature

The standard of "reasonable efforts" is proportionate to the risks of the processing and the available technology. Higher-risk processing requires stronger verification measures.

## Article 8(3) — General Contract Law

Article 8(1) shall not affect the general contract law of Member States such as the rules on the validity, formation, or effect of a contract in relation to a child.

## Implementation Requirements

Organizations offering information society services that may be accessed by children must:

1. Determine the applicable age threshold based on the Member States where services are offered
2. Implement age verification mechanisms appropriate to the risk level
3. Obtain verifiable parental consent for users below the applicable age threshold
4. Adapt privacy notices to use language understandable by children
5. Minimize data collection from children to what is strictly necessary
6. Not rely on consent obtained from children below the threshold, even if the child claims to be older
7. Implement processes to handle cases where a child's age is initially misrepresented

## Enforcement

Violations of Article 8 are subject to the higher tier of administrative fines under Article 83(5)(a): up to EUR 20,000,000 or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher.
