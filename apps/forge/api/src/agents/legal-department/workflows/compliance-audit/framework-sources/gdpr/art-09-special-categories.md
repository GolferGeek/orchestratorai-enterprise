# GDPR Article 9 — Processing of Special Categories of Personal Data

## Article 9(1) — General Prohibition

Processing of the following categories of personal data is prohibited:
- Racial or ethnic origin
- Political opinions
- Religious or philosophical beliefs
- Trade union membership
- Genetic data (data relating to inherited or acquired genetic characteristics providing information about physiology or health, obtained from biological sample analysis)
- Biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person (facial images, fingerprints, iris scans)
- Data concerning health (physical or mental health, including provision of health care services)
- Data concerning sex life or sexual orientation

This prohibition applies to both direct and indirect processing. Data that can be used to infer special categories (e.g., dietary preferences inferring religion, medical app usage inferring health conditions) may also be covered.

## Article 9(2) — Exceptions to the Prohibition

The prohibition in paragraph 1 does not apply if one of the following conditions is met:

### (a) Explicit Consent
The data subject has given explicit consent to processing for one or more specified purposes, except where Union or Member State law provides that the prohibition cannot be lifted by consent. "Explicit" consent requires a clear, express statement of consent — not merely implied or inferred from conduct.

### (b) Employment, Social Security, and Social Protection Law
Processing is necessary for carrying out obligations and exercising specific rights of the controller or the data subject in the field of employment, social security, and social protection law, authorized by Union or Member State law or a collective agreement, providing appropriate safeguards.

### (c) Vital Interests
Processing is necessary to protect the vital interests of the data subject or another person where the data subject is physically or legally incapable of giving consent. This is limited to emergency situations.

### (d) Legitimate Activities of Certain Bodies
Processing is carried out by a foundation, association, or other not-for-profit body with a political, philosophical, religious, or trade union aim, provided processing relates solely to members or former members or those in regular contact, data is not disclosed outside the body without consent, and appropriate safeguards are in place.

### (e) Data Manifestly Made Public
Processing relates to personal data which is manifestly made public by the data subject. The data subject must have clearly and deliberately made the data public.

### (f) Legal Claims
Processing is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims or whenever courts are acting in their judicial capacity.

### (g) Substantial Public Interest
Processing is necessary for reasons of substantial public interest on the basis of Union or Member State law, proportionate to the aim pursued, respects the essence of the right to data protection, and provides suitable and specific measures to safeguard fundamental rights and interests.

### (h) Health or Social Care
Processing is necessary for preventive or occupational medicine, assessment of working capacity, medical diagnosis, provision of health or social care or treatment, or management of health or social care systems on the basis of Union or Member State law or a contract with a health professional, subject to the conditions and safeguards in Article 9(3).

### (i) Public Health
Processing is necessary for reasons of public interest in the area of public health, such as protecting against serious cross-border threats to health, ensuring high standards of quality and safety of health care and medicinal products or medical devices, on the basis of Union or Member State law providing suitable and specific measures.

### (j) Archiving, Research, and Statistics
Processing is necessary for archiving purposes in the public interest, scientific or historical research purposes, or statistical purposes under Article 89(1), on the basis of Union or Member State law, proportionate to the aim pursued, respecting the essence of the right to data protection, and providing suitable and specific safeguards.

## Article 9(3) — Health Professional Obligations

When processing under Article 9(2)(h) involves health data, it must be processed by or under the responsibility of a professional subject to obligation of professional secrecy under Union or Member State law or rules established by national competent bodies, or by another person also subject to a confidentiality obligation.

## Article 9(4) — Member State Conditions

Member States may maintain or introduce further conditions, including limitations, with regard to the processing of genetic data, biometric data, or data concerning health.

## Documentation Requirements

Organizations processing special category data must document:
- Which exception under Article 9(2) applies
- The specific purpose requiring special category data
- Why the processing is necessary (not merely useful)
- What safeguards are in place
- Retention periods specific to the special category data
- Access controls limiting who can access the data

## Enforcement

Violations of Article 9 are subject to the higher tier of administrative fines under Article 83(5)(a): up to EUR 20,000,000 or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher.
