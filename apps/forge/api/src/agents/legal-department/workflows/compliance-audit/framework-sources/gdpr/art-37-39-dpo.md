# GDPR Articles 37-39 — Data Protection Officer

## Article 37 — Designation of the Data Protection Officer

### Article 37(1) — Mandatory Designation

The controller and the processor shall designate a data protection officer in any case where:

**(a) Public Authority or Body**
The processing is carried out by a public authority or body, except for courts acting in their judicial capacity.

**(b) Core Activities Require Regular and Systematic Monitoring**
The core activities of the controller or the processor consist of processing operations which, by virtue of their nature, their scope, and/or their purposes, require regular and systematic monitoring of data subjects on a large scale.

"Regular and systematic monitoring" includes:
- Online behavioral advertising and tracking
- Loyalty programs and profiling for marketing
- CCTV surveillance
- Location tracking
- Connected devices collecting behavioral data
- Fitness and health tracking apps
- Network monitoring of internet usage

**(c) Core Activities Involve Large-Scale Special Category Processing**
The core activities of the controller or the processor consist of processing on a large scale of special categories of data pursuant to Article 9 or personal data relating to criminal convictions and offenses referred to in Article 10.

"Large scale" factors include:
- The number of data subjects concerned (either as a specific number or as a proportion of the relevant population)
- The volume of data and/or range of different data items being processed
- The duration or permanence of the data processing activity
- The geographical extent of the processing activity

### Article 37(2)-(3) — Group DPO and Contact Publishing

A group of undertakings may appoint a single DPO provided they are easily accessible from each establishment. The controller or processor shall publish the contact details of the DPO and communicate them to the supervisory authority.

### Article 37(5) — Qualifications

The DPO shall be designated on the basis of professional qualities and, in particular, expert knowledge of data protection law and practices and the ability to fulfil their tasks under Article 39.

Required competencies:
- Expertise in national and European data protection laws and practices
- Understanding of GDPR requirements
- Understanding of the processing operations carried out by the organization
- Understanding of information technologies and data security
- Knowledge of the relevant business sector
- Ability to promote a data protection culture within the organization

### Article 37(6) — Internal or External DPO

The DPO may be a staff member of the controller or processor, or fulfil the tasks on the basis of a service contract (external DPO). External DPOs must meet the same requirements and protections.

### Article 37(7) — Voluntary Designation

Where designation is not mandatory, the controller or processor or associations and other bodies representing categories of controllers or processors may designate a DPO on a voluntary basis. If voluntarily appointed, all requirements of Articles 37-39 apply as if the appointment were mandatory.

## Article 38 — Position of the Data Protection Officer

### Article 38(1) — Involvement

The controller and the processor shall ensure that the DPO is involved, properly and in a timely manner, in all issues which relate to the protection of personal data.

This means:
- The DPO must be consulted on all data protection matters from the earliest stage
- The DPO must be invited to attend senior management meetings where data protection matters are discussed
- The DPO's opinion must be given due weight in decision-making
- The DPO must be consulted when a data breach occurs
- The DPO must be consulted during DPIA processes

### Article 38(2) — Resources

The controller and the processor shall support the DPO in performing their tasks by providing:
- Resources necessary to carry out those tasks
- Access to personal data and processing operations
- Resources necessary to maintain their expert knowledge

This includes:
- Sufficient time to fulfil DPO duties (if the DPO has other roles)
- Financial resources for training and professional development
- Adequate staff support where the organization's size warrants it
- Access to IT systems and data inventories
- Budget for external legal or technical advice when needed

### Article 38(3) — Independence

The controller and the processor shall ensure that the DPO:
- Does not receive any instructions regarding the exercise of their tasks
- Is not dismissed or penalized for performing their tasks
- Reports directly to the highest management level of the controller or the processor

The DPO may fulfil other tasks and duties, but the controller or processor must ensure those tasks do not result in a conflict of interest. The DPO cannot hold a position that determines the purposes and means of processing (e.g., cannot simultaneously be Head of IT, HR Director, or Chief Financial Officer).

### Article 38(5)-(6) — Confidentiality and Conflicts

The DPO is bound by secrecy or confidentiality concerning the performance of their tasks. The DPO may fulfil other tasks but must have no conflict of interest.

## Article 39 — Tasks of the Data Protection Officer

### Article 39(1) — Minimum Tasks

The DPO shall have at least the following tasks:

**(a)** Inform and advise the controller or the processor and the employees who carry out processing of their obligations pursuant to the GDPR and other Union or Member State data protection provisions

**(b)** Monitor compliance with the GDPR, other Union or Member State data protection provisions, and the policies of the controller or processor, including assignment of responsibilities, awareness-raising, and training of staff involved in processing operations, and related audits

**(c)** Provide advice where requested regarding the DPIA and monitor its performance pursuant to Article 35

**(d)** Cooperate with the supervisory authority

**(e)** Act as the contact point for the supervisory authority on issues relating to processing, including prior consultation under Article 36, and to consult, where appropriate, with regard to any other matter

### Article 39(2) — Risk-Based Approach

The DPO shall in the performance of their tasks have due regard to the risk associated with processing operations, taking into account the nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing.

## Enforcement

Violations of Articles 37-39 are subject to the lower tier of administrative fines under Article 83(4)(a): up to EUR 10,000,000 or 2% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher.
