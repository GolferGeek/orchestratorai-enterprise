# Privacy Rule — Individual Rights

## Regulatory Basis
45 CFR 164.520 through 164.528 establish the rights of individuals with respect to their protected health information (PHI).

## Right of Access — 45 CFR 164.524

### Scope
An individual has the right to inspect and obtain a copy of PHI about the individual in a designated record set, including medical records, billing records, and enrollment/payment/claims records maintained by or for a covered entity.

### Timeline Requirements
- **30 calendar days** from receipt of request to provide access or written denial.
- **One-time 30-day extension** permitted if the covered entity provides written notice of the reasons for the delay and the date by which action will be completed.
- If PHI is maintained off-site, 60 days total (30 + 30 extension) from receipt of request.

### Fee Limitations
- Fees must be reasonable and cost-based, limited to:
  - Labor for copying (including electronic media)
  - Supplies for creating the copy (paper, electronic media)
  - Postage if mailed
- **Flat fee option:** $6.50 per request (inclusive of all costs) when providing electronic copies, as established by HHS guidance (2016).
- Fees may NOT include costs for searching, retrieving, or maintaining systems.
- No fee may be charged for inspection without copying.

### Format Requirements
- Must provide PHI in the form and format requested if readily producible.
- If not readily producible in requested format, provide in readable hard copy or other agreed-upon format.
- If PHI is maintained electronically, the individual has the right to obtain an electronic copy in a readily producible electronic form and format.
- The individual may direct a covered entity to transmit a copy directly to a designated third party (written, signed direction required per HITECH [HIPAA-HITECH]).

### Grounds for Denial
**Unreviewable denials (no appeal right):**
- Psychotherapy notes
- Information compiled in reasonable anticipation of litigation
- PHI maintained by certain research laboratories (CLIA-exempt)
- PHI obtained from someone other than a health care provider under a promise of confidentiality (if access would reveal source)
- Inmates when access would jeopardize health, safety, security, custody, or rehabilitation

**Reviewable denials (right to appeal to designated licensed health care professional):**
- Licensed health care professional determines access is reasonably likely to endanger the life or physical safety of the individual or another person
- PHI references another person (not a health care provider) and access is reasonably likely to cause substantial harm to that person
- Request made by a personal representative and access is reasonably likely to cause substantial harm to the individual or another person

### Denial Process
- Written denial must be in plain language, contain the basis for denial, a statement of review rights, and description of complaint process.
- If partial denial, covered entity must provide access to any non-denied portion.

## Right to Amend — 45 CFR 164.526

### Scope
An individual has the right to request amendment of PHI in a designated record set for as long as the covered entity maintains the information.

### Timeline
- **60 calendar days** from receipt of request to act on the request.
- **One-time 30-day extension** with written notice to the individual.

### Grounds for Denial
A covered entity may deny the request if the PHI:
- Was not created by the covered entity (unless the originator is no longer available)
- Is not part of the designated record set
- Would not be available for inspection under 45 CFR 164.524
- Is accurate and complete

### Amendment Process
- If accepted: Make the amendment, inform the individual, and make reasonable efforts to inform others (including business associates) who have the PHI and who may have relied on it.
- If denied: Provide written denial with basis, inform of right to submit a statement of disagreement, and include the request and denial (and any statement of disagreement) with future disclosures.

## Right to Accounting of Disclosures — 45 CFR 164.528

### Scope
An individual has the right to receive an accounting of disclosures of PHI made by a covered entity in the six years prior to the request (or since April 14, 2003, if shorter).

### Excluded Disclosures (NOT tracked)
- Disclosures for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations
- Disclosures to the individual
- Disclosures for the facility's directory or to persons involved in care
- Disclosures for national security or intelligence purposes
- Disclosures to correctional institutions or law enforcement with custody
- Disclosures part of a limited data set per 164.514(e)
- Disclosures that occurred prior to the compliance date
- Disclosures authorized by the individual

Note: HITECH expanded accounting rights to include disclosures through an electronic health record for TPO purposes for the three years prior to the request. See [HIPAA-HITECH].

### Required Content of Accounting
For each disclosure:
- Date of the disclosure
- Name and address (if known) of the entity or person who received the PHI
- Brief description of the PHI disclosed
- Brief statement of the purpose or a copy of the request

For repetitive disclosures (same recipient, same purpose), the accounting may include the first disclosure date, last disclosure date, frequency, and a summary.

### Timeline and Fees
- **60 calendar days** from receipt of request (one-time 30-day extension).
- First accounting in any 12-month period: **free of charge**.
- Subsequent requests within 12 months: reasonable, cost-based fee (must inform individual in advance and allow withdrawal of request).

## Right to Request Restrictions — 45 CFR 164.522(a)

### Scope
An individual may request restrictions on uses and disclosures of PHI for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations, as well as disclosures to family members or others involved in care.

### Covered Entity Obligations
- A covered entity is NOT required to agree to a requested restriction, with one exception:
- **Mandatory restriction (HITECH):** A covered entity MUST agree to restrict disclosure to a health plan if the individual pays out of pocket in full for the health care item or service and the disclosure is solely for payment or healthcare operations (not treatment). See [HIPAA-HITECH].
- If agreed to, the covered entity must comply with the restriction except in emergency treatment situations (and must request that the emergency provider not further disclose).
- A restriction may be terminated by agreement with the individual or by written/oral notice from either party (applies only to PHI created or received after the termination).

## Right to Confidential Communications — 45 CFR 164.522(b)

### Health Care Providers
Must accommodate reasonable requests to receive communications by alternative means or at alternative locations. No requirement that the individual explain the basis for the request.

### Health Plans
Must accommodate reasonable requests if the individual clearly states that disclosure of all or part of the information could endanger the individual. May condition accommodation on the individual providing an alternative address or method of contact and information on how payment will be handled.

## Right to Receive Breach Notification — 45 CFR 164.404
An individual has the right to receive notification of a breach of their unsecured PHI. See [HIPAA-BN-REQ] for detailed requirements including:
- Notification within 60 days of discovery
- Content requirements (description of breach, types of PHI, steps to protect, entity's mitigation actions, contact information)
- Substitute notice procedures when contact information is insufficient
