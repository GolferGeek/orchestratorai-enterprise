# GDPR Articles 16-17 — Right to Rectification and Right to Erasure

## Article 16 — Right to Rectification

The data subject has the right to obtain from the controller without undue delay the rectification of inaccurate personal data concerning them. Taking into account the purposes of the processing, the data subject has the right to have incomplete personal data completed, including by means of providing a supplementary statement.

### Implementation Requirements
- Establish clear procedures for receiving and processing rectification requests
- Verify the identity of the requester before making changes
- Rectify data without undue delay (within the one-month timeline under Article 12(3))
- Notify all recipients to whom the data has been disclosed of the rectification, unless impossible or involving disproportionate effort (Article 19)
- Inform the data subject about those recipients if the data subject requests it

## Article 17 — Right to Erasure ("Right to Be Forgotten")

### Article 17(1) — Grounds for Erasure

The data subject has the right to obtain from the controller the erasure of personal data concerning them without undue delay, and the controller has the obligation to erase personal data without undue delay where one of the following grounds applies:

#### (a) Purpose Fulfilled
The personal data is no longer necessary in relation to the purposes for which it was collected or otherwise processed.

#### (b) Consent Withdrawn
The data subject withdraws consent on which the processing is based according to Article 6(1)(a) or Article 9(2)(a), and there is no other legal ground for the processing.

#### (c) Objection to Processing
The data subject objects to the processing pursuant to Article 21(1) and there are no overriding legitimate grounds for the processing, or the data subject objects to direct marketing processing pursuant to Article 21(2).

#### (d) Unlawful Processing
The personal data has been unlawfully processed (no valid legal basis existed).

#### (e) Legal Obligation
The personal data must be erased for compliance with a legal obligation in Union or Member State law to which the controller is subject.

#### (f) Information Society Services to Children
The personal data was collected in relation to the offer of information society services to a child under Article 8(1).

### Article 17(2) — Public Dissemination

Where the controller has made the personal data public and is obliged to erase it, the controller shall, taking account of available technology and the cost of implementation, take reasonable steps, including technical measures, to inform controllers that are processing the data that the data subject has requested the erasure of any links to, or copies or replications of, that personal data.

### Article 17(3) — Exceptions to Erasure

The right to erasure does not apply to the extent that processing is necessary for:

#### (a) Freedom of Expression and Information
Exercising the right of freedom of expression and information.

#### (b) Legal Obligation
Compliance with a legal obligation which requires processing by Union or Member State law, or for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority.

#### (c) Public Health
Reasons of public interest in the area of public health in accordance with Article 9(2)(h) and (i).

#### (d) Archiving and Research
Archiving purposes in the public interest, scientific or historical research purposes, or statistical purposes under Article 89(1), insofar as the right to erasure is likely to render impossible or seriously impair the achievement of the objectives of that processing.

#### (e) Legal Claims
The establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.

### Implementation Requirements

Organizations must implement procedures for:
- Receiving and validating erasure requests
- Determining whether an exception under Article 17(3) applies
- Erasing data from all systems including backups (with documented timelines for backup erasure)
- Notifying processors under Article 28 to erase the data
- Notifying other controllers under Article 17(2) where data was made public
- Notifying recipients under Article 19
- Documenting the erasure action and justification
- Responding to the data subject within the Article 12(3) timeline

## Enforcement

Violations of Articles 16-17 are subject to the higher tier of administrative fines under Article 83(5)(b): up to EUR 20,000,000 or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher.
