# Section 409: Real-Time Issuer Disclosures

## Overview

Section 409 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act amends Section 13 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 to require each issuer reporting under Section 13(a) or 15(d) to disclose to the public on a **rapid and current basis** such additional information concerning material changes in the financial condition or operations of the issuer, in plain English, which may include trend and qualitative information and graphic presentations, as the SEC determines, by rule, is necessary or useful for the protection of investors and in the public interest.

## Core Requirement

### Rapid and Current Disclosure
Issuers must disclose material changes in financial condition or operations on a rapid and current basis. The SEC implemented this requirement primarily through amendments to **Form 8-K**, which serves as the vehicle for current reports of significant events.

### Plain English Requirement
All disclosures must be made in **plain English** — clear, concise, and understandable language. The SEC's plain English rules (Rule 421 of Regulation C) require:
- Short sentences
- Definite, concrete, everyday words
- Active voice
- Tabular presentation of complex information where appropriate
- No legal jargon or highly technical business terms without explanation

## Form 8-K Triggering Events and Filing Deadlines

### Section 1 — Registrant's Business and Operations

#### Item 1.01: Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of material contracts not made in the ordinary course of business, including terms, parties, and material conditions

#### Item 1.02: Termination of a Material Definitive Agreement
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of termination of a material contract, including circumstances and any material early termination penalties

#### Item 1.03: Bankruptcy or Receivership
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of entry into bankruptcy, receivership, or similar proceeding, including court identity, date, and appointed trustee/receiver

#### Item 1.04: Mine Safety — Reporting of Shutdowns and Patterns of Violations
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of mine closure orders, safety violations patterns, and related regulatory actions

### Section 2 — Financial Information

#### Item 2.01: Completion of Acquisition or Disposition of Assets
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of completed acquisitions or dispositions where the assets acquired or disposed of constitute a significant amount (generally 10%+ of total assets)

#### Item 2.02: Results of Operations and Financial Condition
- **Deadline:** 4 business days (but safe harbor applies)
- Disclosure of material non-public information regarding results of operations or financial condition, such as preliminary earnings announcements

#### Item 2.03: Creation of a Direct Financial Obligation or an Obligation under an Off-Balance Sheet Arrangement
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of material direct financial obligations (borrowings, guarantees) or off-balance sheet arrangements

#### Item 2.04: Triggering Events That Accelerate or Increase a Direct Financial Obligation
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of events that trigger acceleration of payments, increase in obligations, or cross-default provisions

#### Item 2.05: Costs Associated with Exit or Disposal Activities
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of commitments to exit plans, plant closings, or employee terminations involving material charges

#### Item 2.06: Material Impairments
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure when the board of directors, a committee thereof, or authorized officers conclude that a material charge for impairment is required under GAAP

### Section 3 — Securities and Trading Markets

#### Item 3.01: Notice of Delisting or Failure to Satisfy Listing Rule
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of receipt of notification from a national securities exchange that the issuer does not satisfy a listing rule

#### Item 3.02: Unregistered Sales of Equity Securities
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of sales of unregistered equity securities (unless reported in periodic reports)

#### Item 3.03: Material Modification to Rights of Security Holders
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of material limitations or qualifications of rights of registered security holders

### Section 4 — Matters Related to Accountants and Financial Statements

#### Item 4.01: Changes in Registrant's Certifying Accountant
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of auditor dismissal, resignation, or engagement of a new auditor, including any disagreements or reportable events

#### Item 4.02: Non-Reliance on Previously Issued Financial Statements
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure when the issuer's board, audit committee, or officers conclude that previously issued financial statements should no longer be relied upon due to error — critical intersection with [SOX-SEC-302] and [SOX-SEC-404]

### Section 5 — Corporate Governance and Management

#### Item 5.01: Changes in Control of Registrant
- **Deadline:** 4 business days

#### Item 5.02: Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers; Compensatory Arrangements
- **Deadline:** 4 business days
- Disclosure of departure or appointment of CEO, CFO, COO, principal accounting officer, or directors

#### Item 5.03: Amendments to Articles of Incorporation or Bylaws
- **Deadline:** 4 business days

#### Item 5.07: Submission of Matters to a Vote of Security Holders
- **Deadline:** 4 business days after the meeting

### Section 7 — Regulation FD

#### Item 7.01: Regulation FD Disclosure
- Filing simultaneous with or promptly after selective disclosure
- Material non-public information that has been selectively disclosed must be broadly disseminated

### Section 8 — Other Events

#### Item 8.01: Other Events
- Voluntary disclosure of any events the registrant deems of importance to security holders
- No mandatory deadline (voluntary reporting)

### Section 9 — Financial Statements and Exhibits

#### Item 9.01: Financial Statements and Exhibits
- Filed as part of any 8-K requiring financial statements or exhibits

## Material Events Requiring Immediate Attention

### Cybersecurity Incidents (Item 1.05 — effective December 2023)
- **Deadline:** 4 business days after determining the incident is material
- Disclosure of nature, scope, timing, and material impact or reasonably likely material impact
- May delay filing if U.S. Attorney General determines disclosure poses substantial risk to national security or public safety (up to 60 days, extendable to 120 days)

### Supply Chain Disruptions
- Material supply chain disruptions that affect financial condition or operations may trigger Item 2.05, 2.06, or 8.01 reporting

## Late Filing Consequences

### Loss of Form S-3 Eligibility
Issuers that fail to file timely 8-Ks may lose eligibility to use Form S-3 for registered offerings, significantly increasing the cost and time required for capital raising.

### SEC Enforcement
- SEC may bring enforcement actions for failure to file required current reports
- Civil money penalties under Section 21(d) of the Exchange Act
- Late filings are tracked and publicly available on EDGAR

### No Private Right of Action
Form 8-K filings are generally considered "furnished" rather than "filed" for purposes of Section 18 liability, providing some protection against private securities fraud lawsuits (except for Items 2.02 and 7.01, which are furnished, and most other items, which are filed).

## Relationship to Other SOX Provisions

### Section 302 [SOX-SEC-302]
CEO/CFO certifications cover disclosure controls designed to ensure material information is communicated on a timely basis. Failure to file timely 8-Ks may indicate deficiencies in disclosure controls.

### Section 404 [SOX-SEC-404]
Internal controls over financial reporting should include controls to identify and escalate triggering events for Form 8-K reporting.

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Establish a disclosure committee responsible for identifying 8-K triggering events
- [ ] Maintain a current list of all Form 8-K triggering events and deadlines
- [ ] Implement escalation procedures for material events requiring rapid disclosure
- [ ] Ensure disclosure controls capture material changes across all business units and subsidiaries
- [ ] Train management and key personnel on 8-K triggering events and reporting obligations
- [ ] Monitor all material contracts for triggering events (defaults, terminations, amendments)
- [ ] Coordinate with legal counsel on materiality determinations
- [ ] Maintain templates and pre-approved language for common 8-K filings
- [ ] Track filing deadlines using a compliance calendar
- [ ] Conduct post-filing review to ensure accuracy and completeness
- [ ] Establish cybersecurity incident response procedures that integrate with 8-K disclosure requirements
