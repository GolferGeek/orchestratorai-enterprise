# GDPR Article 5 — Principles Relating to Processing of Personal Data

## Article 5(1) — Core Processing Principles

All processing of personal data must comply with six binding principles:

### (a) Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency
Personal data must be processed lawfully, fairly, and in a transparent manner in relation to the data subject. Lawfulness requires a valid legal basis under Article 6. Fairness means processing must not be detrimental, unexpected, or misleading. Transparency requires clear, accessible information about processing activities provided to data subjects.

### (b) Purpose Limitation
Personal data must be collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes. Further processing for archiving purposes in the public interest, scientific or historical research purposes, or statistical purposes is not considered incompatible with the initial purposes, provided appropriate safeguards under Article 89(1) are in place.

Incompatibility assessment factors include:
- Any link between the original purpose and the proposed further processing purpose
- The context in which data was collected, particularly the data subject's relationship with the controller
- The nature of the personal data, especially whether special categories under Article 9 are involved
- Possible consequences of the intended further processing for data subjects
- Existence of appropriate safeguards such as encryption or pseudonymization

### (c) Data Minimization
Personal data must be adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which it is processed. Organizations must not collect data "just in case" or for unspecified future use. Each data element collected must be justified against the stated processing purpose.

### (d) Accuracy
Personal data must be accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date. Every reasonable step must be taken to ensure that personal data that is inaccurate, having regard to the purposes for which it is processed, is erased or rectified without delay. Organizations must implement processes to verify data accuracy at collection and periodically thereafter.

### (e) Storage Limitation
Personal data must be kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which the personal data is processed. Organizations must define and document retention periods for each category of personal data. Data may be stored for longer periods solely for archiving purposes in the public interest, scientific or historical research, or statistical purposes under Article 89(1), subject to appropriate technical and organizational safeguards.

### (f) Integrity and Confidentiality
Personal data must be processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security, including protection against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage, using appropriate technical and organizational measures. This principle directly connects to the detailed security requirements in Article 32.

## Article 5(2) — Accountability

The controller shall be responsible for, and be able to demonstrate compliance with, all principles in Article 5(1). This accountability principle requires organizations to:

- Maintain documentation of compliance measures
- Implement data protection policies
- Conduct regular audits and reviews
- Adopt data protection by design and by default measures under Article 25
- Maintain records of processing activities under Article 30
- Carry out data protection impact assessments where required under Article 35
- Designate a Data Protection Officer where required under Article 37

The burden of proof lies with the controller. It is not sufficient to claim compliance — the controller must be able to actively demonstrate it through documented evidence.

## Enforcement

Violations of Article 5 principles are subject to the higher tier of administrative fines under Article 83(5)(a): up to EUR 20,000,000 or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover of the preceding financial year, whichever is higher.
