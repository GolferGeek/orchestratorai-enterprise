# Section 201: Services Outside the Scope of Practice of Auditors

## Overview

Section 201 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act amends Section 10A of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 to prohibit registered public accounting firms from providing certain non-audit services to audit clients contemporaneously with an audit engagement. This section is foundational to auditor independence and directly addresses conflicts of interest that contributed to major corporate accounting scandals.

## Nine Prohibited Non-Audit Services

A registered public accounting firm that performs an audit for an issuer SHALL NOT provide the following non-audit services to that issuer contemporaneously with the audit:

### 1. Bookkeeping Services (Section 201(a)(1))
Bookkeeping or other services related to the accounting records or financial statements of the audit client. This includes maintaining or preparing the audit client's accounting records, preparing the audit client's financial statements that are filed with the SEC, or preparing or originating source data underlying the audit client's financial statements.

### 2. Financial Information Systems Design and Implementation (Section 201(a)(2))
Design or implementation of financial information systems. This covers hardware or software systems that aggregate source data underlying the financial statements or that generate information significant to the audit client's financial statements or other financial information systems taken as a whole.

### 3. Appraisal or Valuation Services (Section 201(a)(3))
Appraisal services, valuation services, fairness opinions, or contribution-in-kind reports where it is reasonably likely that the results of any valuation or appraisal would be material to the financial statements, or where the audit firm would audit the results.

### 4. Actuarial Services (Section 201(a)(4))
Actuarial-oriented advisory services involving the determination of amounts recorded in the financial statements and related accounts for the audit client. This includes insurance company policy reserves, ## pension and post-retirement benefit obligations, and similar actuarial calculations.

### 5. Internal Audit Outsourcing Services (Section 201(a)(5))
Internal audit outsourcing services relating to the internal accounting controls, financial systems, or financial statements of the audit client. The external auditor cannot serve as the de facto internal audit function.

### 6. Management Functions (Section 201(a)(6))
Acting, temporarily or permanently, as a director, officer, or employee of the audit client, or performing any decision-making, supervisory, or ongoing monitoring function for the audit client.

### 7. Human Resources (Section 201(a)(7))
Searching for or seeking out prospective candidates for managerial, executive, or director positions. Engaging in psychological testing, background checks, or providing formal written or oral reference checks for prospective candidates for managerial, executive, or director positions. Acting as a negotiator on the audit client's behalf in determining position, status, compensation, or other conditions of employment.

### 8. Broker-Dealer, Investment Adviser, or Investment Banking Services (Section 201(a)(8))
Serving as a broker or dealer, promoter, or underwriter, on behalf of an audit client, making investment decisions on behalf of the audit client, or otherwise having discretionary authority over an audit client's investments, executing a transaction to buy or sell an audit client's investment, or having custody of assets of the audit client.

### 9. Legal Services and Expert Services (Section 201(a)(9))
Providing any service or product to an audit client for the purpose of being used as an expert, or providing legal services to the audit client. This includes providing expert opinions or other expert services for the purpose of advocating the audit client's interests in litigation or in a regulatory or administrative proceeding or investigation.

## Pre-Approval Requirements for Permitted Non-Audit Services

### Audit Committee Pre-Approval (Section 201(h))
All non-audit services not expressly prohibited under Section 201 must be pre-approved by the audit committee of the issuer. The audit committee must be informed of each non-audit service, and approval must be obtained before the service begins.

### Pre-Approval Policies and Procedures
- The audit committee may establish pre-approval policies and procedures for the engagement of non-audit services
- Such policies must be detailed as to the particular service
- The audit committee must be informed of each service approved under the policy
- Policies must not include delegation of audit committee responsibilities to management

### De Minimis Exception (Section 201(i)(1)(B))
Pre-approval is not required if:
- The aggregate amount of all non-audit services provided constitutes not more than **5 percent** of the total revenues paid by the issuer to the auditor during the fiscal year in which the services are provided
- Such services were not recognized as non-audit services by the issuer at the time of the engagement
- Such services are promptly brought to the attention of the audit committee and approved prior to completion of the audit by the audit committee or by one or more members designated by the audit committee

## Penalties for Violations

Violations of Section 201 can result in:
- Sanctions imposed by the PCAOB under Section 105, including censure, temporary or permanent suspension, temporary or permanent revocation of registration, and civil money penalties up to $750,000 for individuals and $15,000,000 for firms per violation
- SEC enforcement actions including cease-and-desist orders, disgorgement, and civil penalties
- Potential criminal referral under Section 802 [SOX-SEC-802] for willful violations

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Maintain a list of all services provided by the external auditor to the issuer
- [ ] Verify none of the nine prohibited services are being provided contemporaneously with the audit
- [ ] Ensure all permitted non-audit services have been pre-approved by the audit committee
- [ ] Monitor aggregate non-audit service fees against the 5% de minimis threshold
- [ ] Document all pre-approval decisions, including rationale and any conditions
- [ ] Disclose non-audit service fees in the annual proxy statement per SEC rules
- [ ] Establish written pre-approval policies and procedures
- [ ] Review and update pre-approval policies annually
