# Section 301: Public Company Audit Committees

## Overview

Section 301 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act directs the SEC to adopt rules requiring national securities exchanges and national securities associations to prohibit the listing of any security of an issuer that does not comply with the audit committee requirements set forth in this section. This section establishes the audit committee as the primary governance mechanism for financial reporting oversight.

## Independence Requirements

### Member Independence (Section 301(m)(3)(A))
Each member of the audit committee of the issuer shall be a member of the board of directors of the issuer, and shall otherwise be **independent**. For purposes of this section, a member of an audit committee is considered independent if the member:

1. **Does not accept any compensatory fee** from the issuer other than in the member's capacity as a member of the board of directors, a committee of the board of directors, or a fixed amount of compensation under a retirement plan (including deferred compensation) for prior service with the issuer, provided that such compensation is not contingent on continued service
2. **Is not an affiliated person** of the issuer or any subsidiary thereof, other than in the member's capacity as a member of the board of directors or any committee of the board

### Prohibited Relationships
An audit committee member may NOT:
- Be an officer or employee of the issuer or any parent or subsidiary
- Accept consulting, advisory, or other compensatory fees from the issuer (other than director fees)
- Own more than 10% of the issuer's voting securities (safe harbor — SEC Rule 10A-3)
- Be a partner, controlling shareholder, or executive officer of any entity that has a material business relationship with the issuer

### Exemptions
- Foreign private issuers: modified independence standards under SEC Rule 10A-3(b)(1)(iv)
- Listed closed-end investment companies: modified requirements under SEC Rule 10A-3(b)(2)
- Smaller reporting companies: no exemption from audit committee independence requirements

## Responsibilities for External Auditor Oversight

### Direct Responsibility (Section 301(m)(2))
The audit committee of each issuer shall be **directly responsible** for:

1. **Appointment** of the registered public accounting firm to serve as the external auditor
2. **Compensation** of the external auditor — negotiation and approval of audit fees
3. **Oversight** of the work of the external auditor, including resolution of disagreements between management and the auditor regarding financial reporting
4. **Retention** decisions — authority to engage, terminate, or replace the external auditor at any time

### Pre-Approval of Services
The audit committee must pre-approve all audit and permissible non-audit services provided by the external auditor [SOX-SEC-201]. This includes:
- Annual audit engagement letter and scope
- Quarterly review services
- Any permitted non-audit services not prohibited by Section 201
- Tax services (subject to specific audit committee pre-approval per PCAOB Rule 3524)

### Reporting Relationship
The external auditor reports **directly to the audit committee**, not to management. This reporting line is fundamental to auditor independence and ensures that the auditor is not subject to management pressure regarding the scope, timing, or findings of the audit.

## Authority to Engage Advisors

### Independent Counsel and Advisors (Section 301(m)(5))
The audit committee shall have the authority to **engage independent counsel and other advisors** as it determines necessary to carry out its duties. This includes:
- Legal counsel independent from the issuer's general counsel
- Forensic accountants
- Compensation consultants (for auditor fee benchmarking)
- Information technology specialists
- Other subject matter experts

### Funding (Section 301(m)(6))
Each issuer shall provide **appropriate funding**, as determined by the audit committee in its capacity as a committee of the board of directors, for:
- Payment of compensation to the registered public accounting firm employed by the issuer
- Payment of compensation to any advisors employed by the audit committee under Section 301(m)(5)
- Ordinary administrative expenses of the audit committee that are necessary or appropriate in carrying out its duties

The issuer may NOT limit or override the audit committee's funding determinations.

## Whistleblower Complaint Procedures

### Establishment of Procedures (Section 301(m)(4))
Each audit committee shall establish procedures for:

#### Receipt of Complaints
- Procedures for the **receipt, retention, and treatment** of complaints received by the issuer regarding:
  - Accounting matters
  - Internal accounting controls
  - Auditing matters
  - Potential fraud or financial misreporting

#### Confidential Anonymous Submission
- Procedures for the **confidential, anonymous submission** by employees of the issuer of concerns regarding:
  - Questionable accounting or auditing matters
  - Potential violations of the issuer's code of ethics
  - Internal control weaknesses or failures

### Implementation Requirements
- Establish a dedicated hotline or web-based portal for anonymous submissions
- Ensure complaints are directed to the audit committee (or its designee) without passing through management
- Maintain logs of all complaints received, their nature, and disposition
- Protect the identity of complainants from retaliation [SOX-SEC-806]
- Periodically report to the full board on complaint activity and trends
- Retain complaint records for a minimum of 7 years

## Financial Expert Requirement

### SEC Rule 407 of Regulation S-K
While Section 301 establishes the independence framework, SEC rules further require:
- Disclosure of whether at least one member of the audit committee is a **financial expert**
- If no financial expert serves on the committee, the issuer must disclose why not
- A financial expert must have: (1) understanding of GAAP and financial statements, (2) experience preparing, auditing, analyzing, or evaluating financial statements of comparable complexity, (3) understanding of internal controls and procedures for financial reporting, (4) understanding of audit committee functions

## NYSE and NASDAQ Listing Standards

### NYSE Listed Company Manual Section 303A.07
- Minimum three members on the audit committee
- All members must be financially literate (or become so within a reasonable period)
- At least one member must have accounting or financial management expertise
- Audit committee charter must be adopted and reviewed annually

### NASDAQ Rule 5605(c)
- Minimum three members
- All members must be able to read and understand fundamental financial statements
- At least one member must have past employment experience in finance or accounting

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

- Delisting of the issuer's securities from the national securities exchange
- SEC enforcement action for failure to maintain required audit committee structure
- Potential personal liability for directors who fail to comply with independence requirements
- Shareholder derivative suits alleging breach of fiduciary duty

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] All audit committee members meet independence requirements
- [ ] No member accepts compensatory fees other than director/committee fees
- [ ] No member is an affiliated person of the issuer
- [ ] Audit committee has sole authority to appoint, compensate, and oversee external auditor
- [ ] Pre-approval procedures for audit and non-audit services are established and documented
- [ ] Authority to engage independent counsel and advisors is established in the committee charter
- [ ] Adequate funding mechanism is in place and not subject to management override
- [ ] Whistleblower complaint procedures are established and operational
- [ ] Confidential anonymous submission channel is available to employees
- [ ] Complaint logs are maintained and periodically reviewed
- [ ] Financial expert disclosure is included in annual proxy statement
- [ ] Audit committee charter is reviewed and updated annually
