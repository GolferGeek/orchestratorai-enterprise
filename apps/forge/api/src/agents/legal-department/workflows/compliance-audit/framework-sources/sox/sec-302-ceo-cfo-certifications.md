# Section 302: Corporate Responsibility for Financial Reports — CEO/CFO Certifications

## Overview

Section 302 of the Sarbanes-Oxley Act requires the principal executive officer (CEO) and principal financial officer (CFO) of each issuer to personally certify the accuracy and completeness of periodic financial reports filed with the SEC. This section creates direct, personal accountability for the integrity of financial reporting, moving beyond the prior regime where certifications were largely a corporate formality.

## Certification Requirements

### Reports Subject to Certification
The certifying officers must sign certifications in connection with each report filed under:
- **Section 13(a)** of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 — periodic reports (10-K, 10-Q)
- **Section 15(d)** of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 — periodic reports by issuers with registered but unlisted securities

This applies to:
- Annual reports on Form 10-K
- Quarterly reports on Form 10-Q
- Any amendments to such reports (10-K/A, 10-Q/A)

### Certifying Officers
- **Principal Executive Officer** (CEO or equivalent)
- **Principal Financial Officer** (CFO or equivalent)
- Both must sign — there is no delegation or substitution

## Content of the Certification (SEC Rule 13a-14(a) / 15d-14(a))

### Statement 1: Review of the Report
The certifying officer must state that he or she has **reviewed the report** being filed. This imposes an affirmative duty to personally review the report, not merely sign it.

### Statement 2: No Material Misstatements or Omissions
Based on the certifying officer's knowledge, the report does not contain any **untrue statement of a material fact** or **omit to state a material fact** necessary to make the statements made, in light of the circumstances under which such statements were made, not misleading.

### Statement 3: Fair Presentation
Based on the certifying officer's knowledge, the financial statements and other financial information included in the report **fairly present in all material respects** the financial condition, results of operations, and cash flows of the issuer as of, and for, the periods presented in the report.

### Statement 4: Responsibility for Internal Controls
The certifying officer must acknowledge that he or she is **responsible for establishing and maintaining internal controls** (subsequently refined to "disclosure controls and procedures" and "internal control over financial reporting").

### Statement 5: Design of Disclosure Controls
The certifying officer must certify that he or she has:
- **Designed** such disclosure controls and procedures, or caused such disclosure controls and procedures to be designed under the officer's supervision, to ensure that material information relating to the issuer, including its consolidated subsidiaries, is made known to the certifying officers by others within those entities, **particularly during the period in which the periodic report is being prepared**

### Statement 6: Evaluation of Controls
The certifying officer must certify that he or she has:
- **Evaluated the effectiveness** of the issuer's disclosure controls and procedures as of a date **within 90 days** prior to the filing date of the report
- **Presented in the report** the certifying officers' conclusions about the effectiveness of the disclosure controls and procedures based on such evaluation

### Statement 7: Disclosure to Auditors and Audit Committee
The certifying officer must certify that he or she has **disclosed to the issuer's auditors and the audit committee** of the board of directors (or persons fulfilling the equivalent function):
- **All significant deficiencies** in the design or operation of internal controls which could adversely affect the issuer's ability to record, process, summarize, and report financial data, and has identified for the auditors any **material weaknesses** in internal controls
- **Any fraud**, whether or not material, that involves management or other employees who have a **significant role** in the issuer's internal controls

### Statement 8: Changes in Internal Controls
The certifying officer must certify whether or not there were **significant changes** in internal controls or in other factors that could significantly affect internal controls subsequent to the date of the evaluation, including any corrective actions with regard to significant deficiencies and material weaknesses.

## Disclosure Controls and Procedures (SEC Rule 13a-15)

### Definition
Disclosure controls and procedures are controls and other procedures of an issuer designed to ensure that information required to be disclosed by the issuer in reports filed under the Exchange Act is:
- **Recorded, processed, summarized, and reported** within the time periods specified in the SEC's rules and forms
- **Accumulated and communicated** to the issuer's management, including the certifying officers, as appropriate, to allow timely decisions regarding required disclosure

### Sub-Certification Process
To support the CEO/CFO certifications, issuers typically implement a **sub-certification** or **management representation** process whereby:
- Business unit leaders, controllers, and other key personnel provide representations to the CEO/CFO regarding the accuracy and completeness of financial information within their areas
- These sub-certifications cascade up through the organization to provide the CEO/CFO with a basis for their certifications
- Sub-certifications are an internal practice, not an SEC requirement, but are widely considered a best practice

## Relationship to Section 906 Criminal Certifications [SOX-SEC-906]

| Aspect | Section 302 | Section 906 |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Nature | Civil certification | Criminal certification |
| Filed with SEC | Yes (Exhibit 31) | Yes (Exhibit 32) |
| Standard | "Based on certifying officer's knowledge" | "Fully complies" and "fairly presents" |
| Penalty for knowing violation | SEC civil enforcement | Up to $1M fine, 10 years imprisonment |
| Penalty for willful violation | SEC civil enforcement | Up to $5M fine, 20 years imprisonment |

Both certifications must accompany each periodic report. They serve complementary but distinct purposes.

## Relationship to Section 404 [SOX-SEC-404]

Section 302 requires the CEO/CFO to certify the effectiveness of disclosure controls and procedures. Section 404 requires management's assessment of internal control over financial reporting and (for larger filers) an external auditor attestation. Together, they create a comprehensive framework:
- Section 302: quarterly evaluation and certification of disclosure controls
- Section 404: annual management assessment and auditor attestation of internal controls over financial reporting

## Penalties for Violations

### Civil Penalties
- SEC civil enforcement actions under Sections 13(a) and 15(d) of the Exchange Act
- Disgorgement of bonuses and profits under Section 304 (clawback) if the issuer is required to prepare an accounting restatement due to material noncompliance with financial reporting requirements as a result of misconduct
- Civil money penalties under Section 21(d) of the Exchange Act: up to $181,071 per violation for individuals (as adjusted for inflation), or the gross amount of pecuniary gain

### Clawback Provisions (Section 304)
If an issuer is required to prepare an accounting restatement due to material noncompliance as a result of misconduct, the CEO and CFO must reimburse the issuer for:
- Any **bonus or other incentive-based or equity-based compensation** received during the 12-month period following the first public issuance of the non-compliant financial document
- Any **profits realized from the sale of securities** of the issuer during that 12-month period

## Compliance Checklist

- [ ] CEO and CFO personally review each periodic report before filing
- [ ] Certification language conforms to SEC Rule 13a-14(a)/15d-14(a)
- [ ] Disclosure controls and procedures are formally designed and documented
- [ ] Effectiveness evaluation performed within 90 days of filing date
- [ ] Sub-certification process cascades through all business units and subsidiaries
- [ ] Significant deficiencies and material weaknesses disclosed to auditors and audit committee
- [ ] Any fraud involving management or key internal control personnel is disclosed
- [ ] Changes in internal controls since last evaluation are identified and reported
- [ ] Both Section 302 (Exhibit 31) and Section 906 (Exhibit 32) certifications accompany each filing
- [ ] Clawback policies are in place per Section 304
