# GDPR Article 32 — Security of Processing

## Article 32(1) — Risk-Based Security Measures

Taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, and the nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing as well as the risk of varying likelihood and severity for the rights and freedoms of natural persons, the controller and the processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including inter alia as appropriate:

### (a) Pseudonymization and Encryption
- Pseudonymization: Processing personal data in such a manner that it can no longer be attributed to a specific data subject without the use of additional information, provided that such additional information is kept separately and is subject to technical and organizational measures ensuring non-attribution
- Encryption: Of personal data at rest (stored data) and in transit (data being transmitted), using current industry-standard algorithms and key management practices

### (b) Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability, and Resilience
Ensuring ongoing:
- **Confidentiality**: Data is accessible only to authorized persons for authorized purposes
- **Integrity**: Data is accurate and has not been improperly modified
- **Availability**: Data and processing systems are accessible when needed
- **Resilience**: Systems can withstand and recover from disruptions

### (c) Restoration Capability
The ability to restore the availability and access to personal data in a timely manner in the event of a physical or technical incident. This requires:
- Regular, tested backup procedures
- Documented disaster recovery plans
- Defined recovery time objectives (RTOs) and recovery point objectives (RPOs)
- Backup storage that is geographically separate from primary systems

### (d) Testing and Evaluation
A process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organizational measures for ensuring the security of the processing. This includes:
- Vulnerability assessments
- Penetration testing
- Security audits (internal and external)
- Review of access controls and permissions
- Incident response drills
- Review of security policies and procedures

## Article 32(2) — Risk Assessment

In assessing the appropriate level of security, account shall be taken in particular of the risks that are presented by processing, in particular from:
- Accidental or unlawful destruction of personal data
- Loss of personal data
- Alteration of personal data
- Unauthorized disclosure of or access to personal data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed

Risk factors to consider:
- Volume and sensitivity of personal data
- Number of data subjects affected
- Severity of potential harm to data subjects
- Likelihood of the risk materializing
- Whether special category data or children's data is involved

## Article 32(3) — Codes of Conduct and Certification

Adherence to an approved code of conduct under Article 40 or an approved certification mechanism under Article 42 may be used as an element by which to demonstrate compliance with security requirements.

## Article 32(4) — Personnel Controls

The controller and processor shall take steps to ensure that any natural person acting under the authority of the controller or the processor who has access to personal data does not process them except on instructions from the controller, unless required to do so by Union or Member State law.

## Technical Measures Checklist

Organizations should implement, where appropriate:
- Network security (firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention, network segmentation)
- Access controls (multi-factor authentication, role-based access, principle of least privilege)
- Endpoint protection (anti-malware, device encryption, mobile device management)
- Application security (secure coding practices, input validation, security testing)
- Data loss prevention (DLP tools, email filtering, USB controls)
- Logging and monitoring (security information and event management, audit trails)
- Patch management (timely application of security updates)
- Physical security (access controls to facilities, secure disposal of hardware)

## Organizational Measures Checklist

- Information security policy documented and communicated
- Staff security awareness training (at onboarding and regularly thereafter)
- Acceptable use policies for systems and data
- Incident response plan with defined roles and procedures
- Vendor and third-party security assessment procedures
- Data classification scheme
- Clean desk and clear screen policies
- Background checks for staff handling sensitive data
- Confidentiality agreements with staff and contractors
- Regular review of user access rights

## Enforcement

Violations of Article 32 are subject to the lower tier of administrative fines under Article 83(4)(a): up to EUR 10,000,000 or 2% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher.
