# Privacy Rule — Notice of Privacy Practices

## Regulatory Basis
45 CFR 164.520 establishes the requirements for the Notice of Privacy Practices (NPP), the primary vehicle through which covered entities inform individuals about their privacy rights and the entity's privacy practices.

## Required Content — 45 CFR 164.520(b)

The NPP must contain the following 13 elements:

### Header
1. **Header statement:** "THIS NOTICE DESCRIBES HOW MEDICAL INFORMATION ABOUT YOU MAY BE USED AND DISCLOSED AND HOW YOU CAN GET ACCESS TO THIS INFORMATION. PLEASE REVIEW IT CAREFULLY." The exact wording is prescribed by regulation.

### Uses and Disclosures
2. **Description of permitted uses and disclosures** for treatment, payment, and health care operations (TPO), with at least one example for each per [HIPAA-PR-USE].
3. **Description of each purpose** for which the covered entity is permitted or required to use or disclose PHI without authorization. For each, include a description sufficient to place the individual on notice.
4. **Description of uses and disclosures requiring authorization** — statement that other uses and disclosures not described will be made only with the individual's written authorization, and that the individual may revoke such authorization at any time per 45 CFR 164.508.

### Specific Use Statements
5. **Separate statements for the following purposes (if applicable):**
   - Uses or disclosures of psychotherapy notes (if maintained)
   - Uses or disclosures for marketing purposes and whether the covered entity receives remuneration for such marketing
   - Uses or disclosures constituting a sale of PHI [HIPAA-HITECH]
   - Uses or disclosures for fundraising and the right to opt out

### Individual Rights
6. **Statement of individual rights** including:
   - Right to request restrictions on uses and disclosures (including mandatory restrictions for self-pay per HITECH) per [HIPAA-PR-RIGHTS]
   - Right to receive confidential communications
   - Right to inspect and copy PHI
   - Right to amend PHI
   - Right to receive an accounting of disclosures
   - Right to receive a paper copy of the NPP upon request
   - Right to be notified of a breach of unsecured PHI per [HIPAA-BN-REQ]

### Entity Obligations
7. **Statement of the covered entity's duties**, including:
   - Required to maintain the privacy of PHI and provide the NPP
   - Required to abide by the terms of the NPP currently in effect
   - Statement reserving the right to change the terms of the NPP and how changes will be made effective

### Complaints and Contact
8. **Complaints procedure** — description of how individuals may file complaints with the covered entity and with the Secretary of HHS, including contact information.
9. **Contact information** — name (or title) and telephone number of the person or office to contact for further information.
10. **Effective date** — the notice must contain an effective date no earlier than the date on which the notice is printed or otherwise published.

### Additional Required Statements (HITECH Amendments)
11. **Statement regarding fundraising** — if the entity intends to use PHI for fundraising, the NPP must state that the individual has a right to opt out of receiving fundraising communications.
12. **Statement regarding sale of PHI** — the NPP must state that most uses and disclosures of PHI for the purpose of selling PHI require authorization [HIPAA-HITECH].
13. **Statement regarding marketing** — the NPP must state that uses and disclosures for marketing that involve financial remuneration to the covered entity require authorization [HIPAA-HITECH].

## Distribution Requirements

### Health Care Providers with Direct Treatment Relationships — 45 CFR 164.520(c)(2)
- **Provide the NPP** no later than the date of the first service delivery (including electronic service delivery).
- **Post the NPP** at the service delivery site in a clear and prominent location where individuals seeking service can reasonably be expected to read it.
- **Make copies available** for individuals to take with them.
- **Post on website** — if the covered entity maintains a website that provides information about its customer services or benefits, prominently post the NPP on the website and make it available electronically through the website.
- **Good faith effort** to obtain written acknowledgment of receipt of the NPP from the individual. If not obtained, document the good faith effort and the reason it was not obtained.

### Health Plans — 45 CFR 164.520(c)(1)
- **Provide the NPP** at the time of enrollment.
- **Provide the NPP** within 60 days of a material revision.
- **Provide a reminder** to enrollees at least once every three years that the NPP is available and how to obtain it.
- For group health plans that provide benefits through insurance or HMO: the plan is not required to issue the NPP if the plan sponsor has provided the notice.

### Health Care Clearinghouses — 45 CFR 164.520(c)(2)(iv)
- Make the NPP available to any person who asks.
- Only required if the clearinghouse creates or receives PHI other than as a business associate.

### Electronic Notice
- A covered entity may provide the NPP electronically if the individual agrees to electronic notice.
- If the individual's request for electronic notice is not feasible or the individual revokes consent, a paper copy must be provided.
- An electronic NPP must be available on the entity's website (if one exists).

## Material Changes — 45 CFR 164.520(b)(3), (c)(1)(v)

### What Constitutes a Material Change
Any change that materially alters the uses or disclosures permitted, individual rights, the entity's legal duties, or other privacy practices stated in the NPP.

### Process for Material Changes
1. Revise the NPP with a new effective date.
2. Make the revised NPP available upon request on or after the effective date.
3. Post the revised NPP prominently at the service delivery site (providers).
4. Post the revised NPP prominently on the website (if maintained).
5. **Health plans:** distribute the revised NPP or information about the material change and how to obtain the revised NPP within 60 days of the material revision.
6. **Providers:** provide the revised NPP to any individual who requests it.

### Retroactivity
A covered entity may NOT apply a material change to PHI created or received before the change is effective, unless the individual is informed in advance and agrees.

## Acknowledgment of Receipt — 45 CFR 164.520(c)(2)(ii)

### Providers Only
- Must make a good faith effort to obtain written acknowledgment of receipt.
- If acknowledgment cannot be obtained, document the efforts made and the reason for failure.
- No specific form is required; the acknowledgment is NOT consent or authorization.
- Failure to obtain acknowledgment does NOT prevent the provider from treating or providing services.

### Health Plans
- No acknowledgment requirement.

## Language and Accessibility
- The NPP must be written in plain language.
- OCR guidance recommends that covered entities consider providing the NPP in the primary languages of populations served.
- NPP must be provided in accessible formats for individuals with disabilities upon request (consistent with Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and ADA obligations).

## Documentation and Retention — per [HIPAA-PR-ADMIN]
- The NPP must be retained for 6 years from the date of its creation or the date it was last in effect, whichever is later.
- Documentation of acknowledgment attempts must be retained for 6 years.
