# GDPR Articles 13-14 — Information to Be Provided to Data Subjects

## Article 13 — Information Where Data Is Collected from the Data Subject

When personal data is collected directly from the data subject, the controller must provide the following information at the time of collection:

### Article 13(1) — Always Required
- Identity and contact details of the controller (and representative, if applicable)
- Contact details of the Data Protection Officer, where appointed
- Purposes of processing and the legal basis under Article 6(1)
- Where processing is based on legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)), the legitimate interests pursued by the controller or third party
- Recipients or categories of recipients of the personal data
- Where applicable, the intention to transfer data to a third country or international organization, including the existence or absence of an adequacy decision, or reference to appropriate safeguards under Articles 46-47

### Article 13(2) — Additional Information for Fairness and Transparency
- The period for which data will be stored, or criteria used to determine that period
- The right to request access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, and portability
- Where processing is based on consent, the right to withdraw consent at any time without affecting lawfulness of prior processing
- The right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority
- Whether providing data is a statutory or contractual requirement, or necessary to enter into a contract, and the consequences of failing to provide the data
- The existence of automated decision-making, including profiling under Article 22, with meaningful information about the logic involved, the significance, and the envisaged consequences

### Article 13(3) — Compatible Further Processing
Where the controller intends to further process data for a purpose other than that for which it was collected, the controller must provide information about the new purpose and all relevant Article 13(2) information before that further processing.

### Article 13(4) — Exemption
Article 13(1)-(3) do not apply where and insofar as the data subject already has the information.

## Article 14 — Information Where Data Has Not Been Obtained from the Data Subject

When personal data is obtained from a source other than the data subject, the controller must provide:

### Article 14(1)-(2) — Required Information
All information listed in Article 13(1) and 13(2), plus:
- The categories of personal data concerned
- The source from which the personal data originates, and if applicable, whether it came from publicly accessible sources

### Article 14(3) — Timing
The controller must provide the information:
- Within a reasonable period after obtaining the data, but at the latest within one month
- If the data will be used for communication with the data subject, at the latest at the time of the first communication
- If disclosure to another recipient is envisaged, at the latest when the data is first disclosed

### Article 14(4) — Compatible Further Processing
Same obligation as Article 13(3) — notice before further processing for a new purpose.

### Article 14(5) — Exemptions
Article 14(1)-(4) do not apply where:
- The data subject already has the information
- Providing the information proves impossible or would involve disproportionate effort (particularly for archiving, research, or statistical purposes under Article 89(1)), subject to appropriate safeguards
- Obtaining or disclosure is expressly laid down by Union or Member State law providing appropriate measures to protect the data subject's legitimate interests
- The data must remain confidential subject to a professional secrecy obligation regulated by Union or Member State law

When relying on the disproportionate effort exemption, the controller must make the information publicly available (e.g., on a website).

## Privacy Notice Best Practices

An effective privacy notice should:
- Use a layered approach (short-form notice with links to full details)
- Be written in the language of the data subjects
- Be regularly reviewed and updated when processing changes
- Be version-controlled with dates
- Be accessible to persons with disabilities
- Not contain legal jargon unnecessarily
- Use visual aids where appropriate (icons under Article 12(7))

## Enforcement

Violations of Articles 13-14 are subject to the higher tier of administrative fines under Article 83(5)(b): up to EUR 20,000,000 or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher.
