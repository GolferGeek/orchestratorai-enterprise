## Benefits

- **Move through discovery volume faster without turning review into a black box.** The workflow handles batch coding and staged review while preserving attorney control over the calls that matter.

- **Treat privilege as a first-class review problem.** Potentially privileged material is surfaced early and reviewed separately before anything moves toward production.

- **Correct errors before they scale across the corpus.** Batch checkpoints let you fix coding drift early rather than discovering late that thousands of documents were tagged the wrong way.

- **Keep the production set defensible.** Relevance, privilege, hot-doc identification, and sample quality checks are all reviewed before final output is generated.

- **Keep sensitive litigation material inside your own environment.** Discovery content, coding decisions, and review judgments stay on your infrastructure.

## Features

- Batch-oriented coding for relevance, privilege, and production readiness
- Separate review gates for privilege, relevance, hot-doc triage, and sample QA
- Batch review interface with per-document coding visibility
- Produced-set generation after review completion
- Job status tracking across queue, processing, review, completion, and failure states
- Human-in-the-loop corrections before downstream production decisions

## When to use it

- You need a first-pass review on a large discovery population
- You want privilege review separated cleanly from general responsiveness coding
- You need to identify hot documents quickly for case strategy
- You want attorney-supervised automation rather than blind coding at scale

## How it works

1. Click **Start a Document Review** and upload the discovery set
2. The workflow batches documents and begins coding for privilege and relevance
3. Work through each review gate and correct any coding errors
4. Confirm hot documents and quality-control samples
5. Finalize the reviewed corpus and generate the production-ready set
