## Benefits

- **Analyze an entire deal room — not one document at a time.** Upload all your deal documents at once. The system classifies each one (contracts, financials, corporate records, IP assignments, employment agreements), routes them to the right specialists, and builds cross-document findings. You get a holistic picture, not isolated reviews.

- **12+ specialists working simultaneously across legal and financial domains.** Legal specialists cover contracts, IP, employment, real estate, compliance, corporate governance, and litigation. Financial specialists cover financial statements, working capital, cap tables, debt schedules, and revenue concentration. Each document gets the specialists it needs — a balance sheet goes to financial-statements and working-capital, not to the IP specialist.

- **Cross-document awareness catches what isolated reviews miss.** Each specialist sees findings from prior documents in its category. When the employment agreement contradicts the corporate governance policy, or the debt schedule reveals a change-of-control trigger that affects the contract terms, the system surfaces it.

- **Quantified risk matrix with deal-breaker flags.** Seven risk categories (contractual, IP, employment, regulatory, financial, corporate, environmental) scored across four severity levels. Deal-breaker flags highlight critical findings that could kill the deal — with reasoning and recommendations.

- **Two review gates give you control at the right moments.** After document analysis, review classifications and specialist findings — correct any misclassifications or flag missed issues before synthesis. After synthesis, review the risk matrix, deal-breaker flags, and cross-references before the final report.

- **Financial findings are grounded in the numbers.** Financial specialists validate numeric quotes against the source documents. Ungrounded findings (numbers the model invented) are dropped, not silently included.

## Features

- Automatic document classification: 19 document types (contracts, NDAs, balance sheets, P&L, cap tables, board decks, and more)
- Metadata extraction: parties, dates, signatures, document structure
- 7 legal specialists: contract, IP, employment, real estate, compliance, corporate, litigation
- 5 financial specialists: financial statements, working capital, cap table, debt schedule, revenue concentration
- Cross-document context: each specialist sees prior findings for its category
- Per-document risk scoring with specialist routing decisions
- Risk matrix: 7 categories x 4 severity levels with document references
- Deal-breaker flags with reasoning and recommendations
- Missing document detection: identifies referenced but absent documents
- Cross-reference map: inter-document relationship tracking
- Two HITL review gates: post-extraction and post-synthesis
- Incremental mode: add documents to an existing room without re-analyzing completed ones
- Supports PDF, DOCX, images (via vision/OCR), and plain text

## When to use it

- You're conducting M&A due diligence and need to analyze a deal room full of documents
- You want specialist-level analysis across both legal and financial domains
- You need a risk matrix and deal-breaker assessment, not just document summaries
- You're looking for cross-document issues that isolated reviews would miss
- You want to incrementally add documents as they become available during the deal

## How it works

1. Click **New Room** and enter the deal context (target company, transaction type, jurisdictions, focus areas)
2. Upload deal documents (contracts, financials, corporate records — any combination)
3. Watch classification assign document types and route to specialists
4. **Review Gate 1**: Review document classifications and specialist findings — correct misclassifications or flag issues
5. The system synthesizes all findings into a risk matrix with deal-breaker flags
6. **Review Gate 2**: Review the synthesis — approve, or send back for re-analysis
7. Receive your due diligence report with risk matrix, deal-breaker flags, and cross-references
