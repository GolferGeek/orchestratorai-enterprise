## Benefits

- **Analyze the deal room as a system, not a stack of unrelated files.** Upload contracts, financials, governance records, employment materials, and IP documents together and get a cross-document view of the transaction.

- **Run legal and financial specialists in parallel.** Each document is routed to the right reviewers so the system can assess issues across contracts, corporate structure, debt, working capital, cap tables, employment exposure, and more at the same time.

- **Catch conflicts that isolated review misses.** The workflow correlates findings across the room, surfacing contradictions, missing materials, and cross-document risk patterns that do not appear when files are reviewed one by one.

- **Move faster toward the real negotiation issues.** Instead of manually synthesizing specialist findings, you get a risk matrix, deal-breaker flags, and supporting context ready for partner review.

- **Keep attorney review in the right places.** Human checkpoints let you correct routing, review findings, and approve synthesis before the room is finalized.

## Features

- Automatic classification across common diligence document types
- Metadata extraction for parties, dates, signatures, and document structure
- Parallel legal and financial specialist routing
- Cross-document context sharing within specialist categories
- Per-document and room-level risk scoring
- Risk matrix with severity and category breakdowns
- Deal-breaker flagging with reasoning and recommendations
- Missing-document detection and cross-reference mapping
- Human review gates after analysis and synthesis
- Incremental room support for adding documents over time

## When to use it

- You are running M&A or investment diligence on a live deal room
- You need legal and financial issue spotting in one workflow
- You want a risk matrix and deal-breaker view, not just document summaries
- You expect documents to arrive over time and want the room to grow incrementally

## How it works

1. Click **New Room** and define the transaction context
2. Upload the available deal documents
3. Let the workflow classify files and route them to the proper specialists
4. Review the first gate to correct classifications or findings
5. Review the synthesized risk matrix and deal-breaker analysis
6. Approve the room output or send it back for refinement
7. Use the final room report to support negotiation, diligence summaries, and deal decisions
