## Benefits

- **Get a research memo that behaves more like a strong associate than a chatbot.** The workflow breaks a legal question into sub-questions, follows each thread recursively, and builds a structured answer instead of returning a single-shot response.

- **See which citations are grounded and which need checking.** Every citation is labeled as verified or unverified, so attorneys can immediately distinguish traceable support from sources that require independent validation.

- **Use your own firm's knowledge, not just model memory.** The research process can search ingested policies, templates, playbooks, and prior work product so the answer reflects how your organization actually operates.

- **Control depth, scope, and cost.** You set the recursion depth, research breadth, and budget constraints before the run starts.

- **Keep sensitive research in your own environment.** For privileged or sealed matters, the workflow can run without pushing strategy or source material into consumer-grade tools.

## Features

- Recursive sub-question generation and depth-first research
- RAG-grounded retrieval from your knowledge base
- Verified and unverified citation labeling
- Configurable depth limits, branch counts, token budgets, and time budgets
- Structured legal memorandum with issue-by-issue analysis
- Research-tree visualization with confidence indicators
- Human review actions to approve, deepen, or redirect the memo
- Scope visibility so you know what document set informed the result

## When to use it

- You need a structured research memorandum for a legal question
- You want research informed by the firm's own knowledge base
- You need clearer source confidence than a generic chat response provides
- You are handling privileged or sealed research that should stay in-house
- You want to deepen only selected branches instead of rerunning everything

## How it works

1. Click **New** and enter the legal question, jurisdiction, practice area, and key facts
2. Set the research controls for depth, branching, and budget
3. Watch the workflow build and investigate sub-questions against your knowledge base
4. Review the memo, citations, and research tree
5. Approve the result, deepen selected branches, or redirect the workflow
6. Finalize the research memorandum when the analysis is where you want it
