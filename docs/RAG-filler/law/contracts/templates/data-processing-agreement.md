## RECITALS

## SECTION 1. DEFINITIONS

1.1. **"Applicable Data Protection Laws"** means all laws, regulations, and binding guidance relating to the processing of Personal Data applicable to the Parties, including but not limited to: (a) GDPR and its implementing legislation; (b) the CCPA/CPRA (Cal. Civ. Code Section 1798.100 et seq.); (c) MCDPA (Minn. Stat. Section 325O); (d) the Colorado Privacy Act; (e) the Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act; and (f) any other applicable federal, state, or international data protection law.

1.2. **"Controller"** means the Party that determines the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data (also referred to as "Business" under CCPA/CPRA).

1.3. **"Data Breach"** means any accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise Processed by Processor.

1.4. **"Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates (also referred to as "Consumer" under CCPA/CPRA).

1.5. **"EEA"** means the European Economic Area.

1.6. **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that is Processed by Processor on behalf of Controller in connection with the Principal Agreement (also referred to as "Personal Information" under CCPA/CPRA).

1.7. **"Processing"** (and its cognates "Process," "Processed") means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation, alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination, combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

1.8. **"Processor"** means the Party that Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller (also referred to as "Service Provider" under CCPA/CPRA).

1.9. **"Standard Contractual Clauses"** or **"SCCs"** means the standard contractual clauses approved by the European Commission for international transfers of Personal Data, as set forth in Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914.

1.10. **"Sub-processor"** means any third party engaged by Processor to Process Personal Data on behalf of Controller.

1.11. **"Supervisory Authority"** means an independent public authority responsible for monitoring the application of Data Protection Laws.

1.12. **"Technical and Organizational Measures"** or **"TOMs"** means the security measures described in Annex II that Processor implements to protect Personal Data.

## SECTION 2. SCOPE AND PURPOSE OF PROCESSING

2.1. **Scope.** This DPA applies to the Processing of Personal Data by Processor on behalf of Controller in connection with the services provided under the Principal Agreement.

2.2. **Details of Processing.** The details of the Processing are described in Annex I, including:

(a) **Subject Matter:** Processing necessary to provide the OrchestratorAI platform and associated services.

(b) **Duration:** For the term of the Principal Agreement plus the data retention period specified herein.

(c) **Nature and Purpose:** Hosting, storage, AI processing, analysis, and retrieval of data as directed by Controller through the Platform.

(d) **Categories of Data Subjects:** Controller's employees, customers, clients, partners, and other individuals whose data Controller processes through the Platform.

(e) **Categories of Personal Data:** As specified in Annex I, which may include names, contact information, professional information, financial data, and other categories as determined by Controller's use of the Platform.

(f) **Special Categories of Data:** Processing of special categories (as defined in GDPR Article 9) shall only occur if explicitly identified in Annex I and subject to additional safeguards.

2.3. **Controller's Instructions.** Processor shall Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller, including with regard to international transfers, unless required by Applicable Data Protection Laws to which Processor is subject. The Principal Agreement, this DPA, and Controller's configuration of the Platform constitute Controller's documented instructions.

2.4. **Processor Obligations Under CCPA/CPRA.** To the extent the CCPA/CPRA applies, Processor certifies that it: (a) shall not sell or share Personal Data; (b) shall not retain, use, or disclose Personal Data for any purpose other than the business purpose specified in the Principal Agreement; (c) shall not retain, use, or disclose Personal Data outside the direct business relationship between the Parties; and (d) shall comply with the CCPA/CPRA and grant Controller the same level of protection as required by the CCPA/CPRA.

---

## SECTION 3. DATA LOCALITY AND LOCAL DEPLOYMENT

3.1. **Local Deployment Model.** The OrchestratorAI platform is designed for local deployment on Controller's hardware. In a local deployment: (a) all Personal Data is stored and Processed entirely on Controller's hardware at Controller's designated facility; (b) Processor does not have persistent access to Personal Data; (c) no Personal Data is transmitted to Processor's systems except as described in Section 3.3; and (d) Controller maintains physical and logical control over all infrastructure.

3.2. **Controller Responsibility.** In a local deployment, Controller is primarily responsible for the security of the Deployment Environment, including: (a) physical security of the hardware; (b) network security and access controls; (c) encryption at rest and in transit; (d) backup and disaster recovery; and (e) access logging and monitoring.

3.3. **Limited Remote Access.** Processor may access Controller's Deployment Environment only: (a) for pre-authorized remote support sessions initiated by Controller; (b) for license validation communications (which transmit only license identifiers, not Personal Data); and (c) for aggregated, anonymized usage analytics (if enabled by Controller). All remote access sessions shall be logged, time-limited, and conducted over encrypted connections.

3.4. **Cloud and Hybrid Deployments.** If the Principal Agreement specifies a cloud or hybrid deployment model, the additional data processing provisions in Annex III shall apply, including specific data center locations, hosting provider commitments, and encryption requirements.

---

## SECTION 4. TECHNICAL AND ORGANIZATIONAL MEASURES

4.1. **Security Measures.** Processor shall implement and maintain appropriate Technical and Organizational Measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, as described in Annex II. Such measures shall include, at a minimum:

(a) **Access Controls.** Role-based access controls, multi-factor authentication for administrative access, principle of least privilege, and regular access reviews.

(b) **Encryption.** Encryption of Personal Data at rest (AES-256 or equivalent) and in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher).

(c) **Network Security.** Firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems, network segmentation, and VPN for remote access.

(d) **Application Security.** Secure software development lifecycle, regular security testing, dependency scanning, and vulnerability management.

(e) **Data Minimization.** Processing only the minimum Personal Data necessary for the specified purpose.

(f) **Pseudonymization and Anonymization.** Where feasible, pseudonymization of Personal Data to reduce risk.

(g) **Logging and Monitoring.** Comprehensive audit logging, real-time monitoring, and anomaly detection.

(h) **Physical Security.** For Processor's own facilities: locked access, surveillance, environmental controls, and visitor management.

(i) **Personnel Security.** Background checks for personnel with access to Personal Data, confidentiality agreements, and regular security training.

(j) **Business Continuity.** Incident response procedures, backup and recovery capabilities, and disaster recovery planning.

4.2. **Security Assessments.** Processor shall conduct regular security assessments of its systems and practices, including: (a) annual penetration testing by qualified third parties; (b) quarterly vulnerability scans; and (c) periodic internal security audits.

4.3. **Platform Security Features.** The OrchestratorAI Platform includes the following security features that Controller may configure: (a) granular role-based access control for all users; (b) audit logging of all system actions; (c) data encryption at rest and in transit; (d) API authentication and rate limiting; (e) session management and timeout controls; and (f) configurable data retention policies.

## SECTION 5. SUB-PROCESSORS

5.1. **Authorized Sub-processors.** Controller hereby grants general written authorization for Processor to engage Sub-processors to Process Personal Data, subject to the requirements of this Section 5. The current list of Sub-processors is set forth in Annex IV.

5.2. **Sub-processor Requirements.** Before engaging any Sub-processor, Processor shall: (a) conduct due diligence on the Sub-processor's data protection practices; (b) enter into a written agreement with the Sub-processor that imposes data protection obligations no less protective than those in this DPA; and (c) remain fully liable to Controller for the acts and omissions of its Sub-processors.

5.3. **Notice of Changes.** Processor shall notify Controller at least thirty (30) days before engaging any new Sub-processor or replacing an existing Sub-processor. The notice shall include the Sub-processor's name, location, and description of Processing activities.

5.4. **Objection Right.** Controller may object to a new Sub-processor by notifying Processor in writing within fifteen (15) days of receiving notice. If Controller objects, the Parties shall discuss the objection in good faith. If the Parties cannot resolve the objection within thirty (30) days, Controller may terminate the affected services under the Principal Agreement without penalty.

---

## SECTION 6. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

6.1. **Assistance.** Processor shall assist Controller in fulfilling its obligations to respond to Data Subject requests to exercise their rights under Applicable Data Protection Laws, including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection.

6.2. **Notification.** If Processor receives a request directly from a Data Subject, Processor shall promptly forward the request to Controller and shall not respond to the Data Subject directly unless instructed by Controller.

6.3. **Technical Capabilities.** The OrchestratorAI Platform provides the following capabilities to support Data Subject rights: (a) data export in machine-readable formats; (b) data deletion tools; (c) data search and retrieval by Data Subject identifier; and (d) audit trail of data processing activities.

---

## SECTION 7. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION

7.1. **Notification Timeline.** Processor shall notify Controller of any Data Breach without undue delay, and in any event within forty-eight (48) hours of becoming aware of the breach, via email to [CONTROLLER SECURITY CONTACT] and by telephone to [CONTROLLER EMERGENCY NUMBER].

7.2. **Breach Notification Content.** The notification shall include, to the extent known: (a) the nature of the breach, including categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records affected; (b) the likely consequences; (c) the measures taken or proposed to address the breach; (d) the name and contact details of Processor's data protection officer or contact point; and (e) a description of the categories of Personal Data involved.

7.3. **Cooperation.** Processor shall cooperate with Controller in investigating and remediating the breach, including: (a) preserving evidence; (b) providing additional information as it becomes available; (c) assisting with notifications to Supervisory Authorities and Data Subjects; and (d) implementing corrective measures.

7.4. **No Admission.** Notification of a Data Breach shall not be construed as an admission of fault or liability by either Party.

---

## SECTION 8. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

8.1. **Transfer Restrictions.** Processor shall not transfer Personal Data outside of the country or region where Controller's Deployment Environment is located without Controller's prior written consent and an appropriate transfer mechanism.

8.2. **Transfer Mechanisms.** For transfers of Personal Data from the EEA to countries not deemed adequate by the European Commission, the Parties agree to use the Standard Contractual Clauses (Module Two: Controller to Processor), attached as Annex V.

8.3. **Local Deployment Advantage.** The Parties acknowledge that the OrchestratorAI platform's local deployment model minimizes the need for international data transfers, as all Personal Data Processing occurs on Controller's locally-hosted infrastructure.

8.4. **U.S. State Law Compliance.** For transfers subject to U.S. state privacy laws, Processor shall comply with the applicable requirements, including data protection assessments and consumer rights protections.

---

## SECTION 9. AUDITS

9.1. **Audit Rights.** Controller shall have the right to audit Processor's compliance with this DPA, subject to the following conditions: (a) audits shall be conducted no more than once per year (except in the event of a Data Breach); (b) Controller shall provide thirty (30) days' prior written notice; (c) audits shall be conducted during normal business hours; (d) Controller shall bear the cost of audits; and (e) Controller's auditors shall be bound by confidentiality obligations.

9.2. **Third-Party Audits.** Processor may satisfy audit requests by providing: (a) SOC 2 Type II audit reports (or equivalent) covering the relevant period; (b) results of penetration testing; or (c) certifications (ISO 27001 or equivalent). If such reports do not adequately address Controller's concerns, Controller may conduct an on-site audit subject to the conditions above.

9.3. **Cooperation.** Processor shall cooperate with audits and provide reasonable access to facilities, systems, records, and personnel relevant to the Processing of Personal Data.

---

## SECTION 10. DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

10.1. **Retention During Term.** During the term of the Principal Agreement, Processor shall retain Personal Data only as necessary to perform the services and in accordance with Controller's instructions.

10.2. **Upon Termination.** Upon termination or expiration of the Principal Agreement, Processor shall, at Controller's election: (a) return all Personal Data to Controller in a standard, machine-readable format; or (b) securely delete all Personal Data and certify such deletion in writing. Controller shall make this election within thirty (30) days of termination.

10.3. **Deletion Timeline.** Processor shall complete return or deletion within sixty (60) days of Controller's election. Processor may retain copies only to the extent required by Applicable Data Protection Laws, and such retained data shall remain subject to the confidentiality and security provisions of this DPA.

10.4. **Local Deployment.** In a local deployment, Personal Data resides on Controller's hardware. Upon termination, Processor shall: (a) delete any remote access credentials; (b) delete any copies of Personal Data in Processor's possession (e.g., from support sessions); and (c) provide instructions for Controller to securely decommission the Platform.

---

## SECTION 11. GENERAL PROVISIONS

11.1. **Precedence.** In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the Principal Agreement, this DPA shall prevail with respect to the Processing of Personal Data.

11.2. **Governing Law.** This DPA shall be governed by the same law that governs the Principal Agreement, except that: (a) for Processing subject to the GDPR, this DPA shall be interpreted in accordance with the GDPR; and (b) for Processing subject to the CCPA/CPRA, the relevant provisions shall be interpreted in accordance with the CCPA/CPRA.

11.3. **Term.** This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the Principal Agreement plus the data retention period specified herein.

11.4. **Amendment.** This DPA may only be amended by written instrument signed by both Parties.

11.5. **Severability.** Invalid provisions severed; remainder continues in force.

11.6. **Entire DPA.** This DPA, together with its Annexes, constitutes the complete data processing agreement between the Parties.
