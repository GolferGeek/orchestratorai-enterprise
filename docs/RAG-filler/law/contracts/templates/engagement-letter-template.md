#### OPTION C: CONTINGENCY FEE ENGAGEMENT

##### 3.1.C.1 Contingency Fee Percentage

Our fee will be a percentage of the Gross Recovery obtained on your behalf, as follows:

| Stage | Percentage |
|-------|------------|
| If settled before filing suit | [XX]% |
| If settled after filing but before formal discovery | [XX]% |
| If settled during or after discovery but before trial | [XX]% |
| If case goes to trial, arbitration, or administrative hearing | [XX]% |
| If appeal is necessary | [XX]% |

##### 3.1.C.2 Definition of Gross Recovery

"Gross Recovery" means all amounts recovered on your behalf, including but not limited to:
- Settlement payments
- Jury verdicts or judgments
- Arbitration awards
- Statutory damages
- Punitive damages
- Pre-judgment and post-judgment interest

"Gross Recovery" does NOT include:
- Amounts specifically designated for medical liens
- Amounts specifically designated for attorney's fees awarded by the court (if separate)
- Costs advanced and reimbursed

##### 3.1.C.3 Calculation and Distribution

The contingency fee will be calculated on the Gross Recovery BEFORE deduction of costs and expenses. Distribution will occur as follows:

1. Contingency fee percentage to Firm
2. Reimbursement of advanced costs to Firm
3. Payment of any medical or other liens
4. Remainder to Client

##### 3.1.C.4 No Recovery

If no recovery is obtained, you will not owe any fee. However, you remain responsible for costs and expenses incurred.

##### 3.1.C.5 Fee if Engagement Terminates Early

If this engagement terminates before conclusion:
- If terminated by Client without cause: Firm entitled to quantum meruit fee based on work performed
- If terminated by Firm without cause: Firm entitled to contingency fee if case later resolves successfully
- If terminated by either party for cause: Rights determined by applicable law and ethics rules

## ARTICLE VI: CONFIDENTIALITY AND PRIVILEGE

### 6.1 Attorney-Client Privilege

Communications between you and the Firm for the purpose of obtaining legal advice are protected by the attorney-client privilege. This privilege belongs to you and can only be waived by you.

### 6.2 Scope of Confidentiality

We will maintain the confidentiality of all information relating to your representation, including:
- Communications with us
- Documents and information you provide
- Our work product
- The fact of representation itself (unless publicly known)

### 6.3 Exceptions to Confidentiality

We may disclose confidential information:
- With your informed consent
- As required by law, court order, or ethics rules
- To prevent reasonably certain death or substantial bodily harm
- To prevent you from committing a crime or fraud that would result in substantial financial harm to another (and in which our services were used)
- To establish a claim or defense in a dispute between us
- To comply with other law or court order

### 6.4 Preservation of Privilege

To preserve privilege, you should:
- Not share our communications with third parties
- Mark our communications as "Privileged and Confidential" when forwarding internally
- Consult us before disclosing any privileged information

### 6.5 Work Product

Our work product (legal research, analysis, strategy documents) is protected by the work product doctrine. This protection belongs to you.

## ARTICLE VIII: TERMINATION OF ENGAGEMENT

### 8.1 Termination by Client

You may terminate this engagement at any time by written notice to us. Upon termination:
- You remain responsible for fees and costs incurred through the date of termination
- We will take reasonable steps to protect your interests
- We will promptly release your file to you or successor counsel upon request

### 8.2 Termination by Firm

We may withdraw from representation upon reasonable notice if:
- You fail to pay fees or costs when due
- You fail to cooperate or follow our advice on significant matters
- Continued representation would violate ethics rules
- You engage in conduct that makes representation unreasonably difficult
- Other good cause exists

### 8.3 Mandatory Withdrawal

We must withdraw from representation if:
- Continued representation would violate ethics rules
- Our physical or mental condition materially impairs our ability to represent you
- We are discharged by you

### 8.4 Court Approval

If litigation is pending, our withdrawal may require court approval. We will seek such approval if necessary and will not abandon your matter.

### 8.5 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:
- All unpaid fees and costs become immediately due
- We will provide a final accounting
- We will cooperate in the orderly transfer of the matter
- Confidentiality obligations continue indefinitely
