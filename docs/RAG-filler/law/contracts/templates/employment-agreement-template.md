## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company is engaged in the business of developing, deploying, and maintaining artificial intelligence software platforms, including the OrchestratorAI platform (orchestratorai.io) and the Divinr.ai market prediction SaaS application;

WHEREAS, the Company desires to engage Contractor as an independent contractor to perform certain development, engineering, and/or related services in connection with the Company's products and technology;

WHEREAS, Contractor possesses the skills, qualifications, and experience necessary to perform such services and desires to be engaged on the terms and conditions set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

## SECTION 1. DEFINITIONS

1.1. **"Confidential Information"** means all non-public information disclosed by the Company to Contractor, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or by inspection, including but not limited to: (a) source code, algorithms, model architectures, training data, and technical specifications of the OrchestratorAI platform and Divinr.ai; (b) business plans, financial information, pricing strategies, customer lists, and marketing plans; (c) trade secrets as defined under the Florida Uniform Trade Secrets Act (Fla. Stat. Section 688.002); (d) proprietary methodologies, deployment configurations, and infrastructure designs; and (e) any information designated as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

1.2. **"Deliverables"** means all work product, code, documentation, designs, reports, and other materials created by Contractor in the performance of the Services under this Agreement.

1.3. **"Intellectual Property"** or **"IP"** means all patents, patent applications, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, know-how, inventions, discoveries, improvements, designs, algorithms, software code, documentation, and all other intellectual property rights, whether registered or unregistered.

1.4. **"OrchestratorAI Platform"** means the Company's artificial intelligence orchestration platform, including all associated software, APIs, agent frameworks, user interfaces, and related systems available at orchestratorai.io.

1.5. **"Divinr.ai"** means the Company's market prediction SaaS application and all associated software, models, and infrastructure.

1.6. **"Services"** means the development, engineering, consulting, and/or related services to be performed by Contractor as described in one or more Statements of Work executed pursuant to this Agreement.

1.7. **"Statement of Work"** or **"SOW"** means a written document executed by both Parties that describes the specific Services to be performed, Deliverables to be produced, timelines, and compensation terms, substantially in the form attached hereto as Exhibit A.

1.8. **"Term"** has the meaning set forth in Section 8.1.

## SECTION 2. ENGAGEMENT AND SERVICES

2.1. **Engagement.** The Company hereby engages Contractor, and Contractor hereby accepts such engagement, to perform the Services described in one or more Statements of Work executed by the Parties from time to time during the Term of this Agreement.

2.2. **Scope of Services.** The specific nature, scope, and schedule of Services shall be set forth in each SOW. Contractor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards for software development and AI engineering.

2.3. **Standard of Performance.** Contractor shall: (a) perform all Services with the degree of skill, care, and diligence normally exercised by qualified professionals performing similar services; (b) comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards; (c) comply with the Company's coding standards, security policies, and development practices as communicated in writing; and (d) promptly notify the Company of any circumstances that may materially affect the timely or satisfactory completion of Services.

2.4. **Tools and Equipment.** Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, Contractor shall provide their own computer hardware, software tools, development environment, and internet connectivity necessary to perform the Services. The Company may, at its sole discretion, provide Contractor with access to Company systems, repositories, and development infrastructure.

2.5. **No Subcontracting.** Contractor shall not subcontract, delegate, or assign any portion of the Services to any third party without the prior written consent of the Company. Any approved subcontractor must execute a confidentiality and IP assignment agreement no less protective than the terms of this Agreement.

## SECTION 3. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

3.1. **Independent Contractor Relationship.** Contractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employer-employee relationship between the Parties.

3.2. **No Employment Benefits.** Contractor acknowledges and agrees that Contractor is not entitled to any benefits that the Company may provide to its employees or members, including but not limited to: health insurance, retirement benefits, paid vacation, sick leave, workers' compensation, or unemployment insurance.

3.3. **Taxes.** Contractor shall be solely responsible for the payment of all federal, state, and local taxes arising from the compensation paid under this Agreement, including but not limited to self-employment taxes, income taxes, and any applicable sales or use taxes. The Company shall report compensation paid to Contractor on IRS Form 1099-NEC in accordance with applicable law.

3.4. **Tax Identification.** Contractor shall provide the Company with a completed IRS Form W-9 (Request for Taxpayer Identification Number and Certification) prior to the commencement of Services.

3.5. **Control and Discretion.** Contractor retains the right to determine the manner, method, and means of performing the Services, subject to the specifications and deadlines set forth in the applicable SOW. The Company may specify the desired results but shall not control the specific manner in which Contractor achieves those results.

3.6. **Work Schedule.** Contractor shall set their own work hours and work schedule, provided that Contractor meets all deadlines and milestones specified in the applicable SOW and makes themselves reasonably available for scheduled meetings and communications during normal business hours (Central Time Zone).

3.7. **Indemnification for Misclassification.** Contractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company from and against any and all claims, liabilities, damages, penalties, interest, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from or related to any determination by any governmental authority that Contractor is or was an employee of the Company.

## SECTION 4. COMPENSATION AND PAYMENT

4.1. **Compensation.** The Company shall compensate Contractor for Services performed at the rates and in the manner specified in each applicable SOW. Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, the following default terms shall apply:

(a) **Hourly Rate:** $[RATE] per hour (typical range: $40.00 to $75.00 per hour, depending on experience and specialization).

(b) **Intern Rate:** For contractors participating in university partnership or internship programs, a reduced rate of $[INTERN_RATE] per hour (typical range: $20.00 to $35.00 per hour) may apply as specified in the applicable SOW.

(c) **Project-Based Compensation:** For discrete projects, a fixed fee may be negotiated and specified in the applicable SOW.

4.2. **Invoicing.** Contractor shall submit detailed invoices to the Company on a bi-weekly basis (every two weeks), or as otherwise specified in the applicable SOW. Each invoice shall include: (a) the invoice date and number; (b) the applicable SOW reference; (c) a detailed description of Services performed; (d) the number of hours worked per day; (e) the applicable rate; and (f) the total amount due.

4.3. **Payment Terms.** The Company shall pay undisputed invoices within fifteen (15) calendar days of receipt. Payment shall be made via direct deposit (ACH transfer) or check, at the Company's election.

4.4. **Disputed Invoices.** If the Company disputes any portion of an invoice, the Company shall: (a) pay the undisputed portion in accordance with Section 4.3; (b) notify Contractor in writing of the disputed amount and the basis for the dispute within ten (10) business days of receipt; and (c) work in good faith with Contractor to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) calendar days.

4.5. **Expenses.** The Company shall not reimburse Contractor for any expenses unless expressly pre-approved in writing by the Company. Any approved expenses must be accompanied by receipts and shall be invoiced separately from Services.

4.6. **No Additional Compensation.** Except as expressly set forth in an SOW, Contractor shall not be entitled to any bonuses, equity, profit-sharing, or other forms of compensation.

## SECTION 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1. **Work Made for Hire.** To the maximum extent permitted by law, all Deliverables and all Intellectual Property embodied therein shall be deemed "works made for hire" as defined under Section 101 of the United States Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. Section 101), with the Company as the author thereof.

5.2. **Assignment of Intellectual Property.** To the extent that any Deliverable or Intellectual Property created by Contractor in the performance of Services is not deemed a work made for hire, Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Deliverable and Intellectual Property, including all copyrights, patent rights, trade secret rights, and all other intellectual property rights therein, throughout the world, in perpetuity.

5.3. **Moral Rights Waiver.** To the extent permitted by applicable law, Contractor hereby irrevocably waives and agrees never to assert any and all moral rights that Contractor may have in or to any Deliverable, including any right of attribution, integrity, disclosure, or withdrawal.

5.4. **Further Assurances.** Contractor agrees to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by the Company to perfect, evidence, or enforce the Company's rights in the Deliverables and Intellectual Property assigned hereunder, including assignments, declarations, and powers of attorney. Contractor hereby irrevocably appoints the Company as Contractor's attorney-in-fact, coupled with an interest, to execute such documents on Contractor's behalf if Contractor fails or refuses to do so within ten (10) business days of the Company's written request.

5.5. **Prior Inventions.** Contractor shall disclose in writing on Exhibit B attached hereto any pre-existing inventions, code, or intellectual property that Contractor proposes to incorporate into any Deliverable ("Prior Inventions"). Contractor hereby grants the Company a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable license (with the right to sublicense) to use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and display any Prior Inventions incorporated into any Deliverable.

5.6. **Open Source Compliance.** Contractor shall not incorporate any open source software into any Deliverable without prior written approval from the Company. For each proposed open source component, Contractor shall identify: (a) the component name and version; (b) the applicable license; and (c) any copyleft or reciprocal licensing obligations. Contractor shall not incorporate any software licensed under the GNU General Public License (GPL), GNU Affero General Public License (AGPL), or any other copyleft license without express written approval from the Company.

5.7. **No Third-Party IP.** Contractor represents and warrants that the Services and Deliverables will not infringe, misappropriate, or violate any third party's Intellectual Property rights. Contractor shall not incorporate into any Deliverable any code, content, or materials owned by any third party unless expressly authorized in writing by the Company.

## SECTION 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1. **Obligation of Confidentiality.** Contractor shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not, without the prior written consent of the Company: (a) disclose any Confidential Information to any third party; or (b) use any Confidential Information for any purpose other than the performance of Services under this Agreement.

6.2. **Standard of Care.** Contractor shall protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care that Contractor uses to protect their own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.

6.3. **Permitted Disclosures.** Contractor may disclose Confidential Information: (a) to the extent required by applicable law, regulation, or court order, provided that Contractor gives the Company prompt written notice of such requirement (to the extent legally permissible) and cooperates with the Company in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy; or (b) to Contractor's legal or tax advisors who have a need to know and are bound by professional obligations of confidentiality.

6.4. **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault or action of Contractor; (b) was lawfully in Contractor's possession before disclosure by the Company, as evidenced by written records; (c) is independently developed by Contractor without use of or reference to Confidential Information; or (d) is lawfully obtained by Contractor from a third party not under an obligation of confidentiality to the Company.

6.5. **Return of Materials.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon the Company's written request, Contractor shall promptly: (a) return or destroy all Confidential Information in Contractor's possession or control, including all copies, extracts, and derivatives; (b) delete all Confidential Information from Contractor's computer systems, devices, and cloud storage; and (c) certify in writing that Contractor has complied with this Section 6.5.

6.6. **Duration.** The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this Section 6 shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement and continue for a period of five (5) years from the date of disclosure; provided, however, that with respect to trade secrets, such obligations shall continue for so long as the information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law.

## SECTION 7. RESTRICTIVE COVENANTS

7.1. **Non-Solicitation.** During the Term and for a period of twelve (12) months following the termination or expiration of this Agreement, Contractor shall not, directly or indirectly: (a) solicit, recruit, or hire any employee, contractor, or consultant of the Company; or (b) induce or encourage any employee, contractor, or consultant of the Company to terminate or reduce their engagement with the Company.

7.2. **Non-Competition.** During the Term and for a period of six (6) months following the termination or expiration of this Agreement (the "Restricted Period"), Contractor shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in, own, manage, operate, or provide services to any business that directly competes with the Company's core products, specifically: (a) an AI orchestration platform that enables multi-agent deployment on client hardware; or (b) a market prediction SaaS platform substantially similar to Divinr.ai.

7.3. **Scope Limitation.** The Parties acknowledge that the non-competition restriction in Section 7.2 is limited in scope to the Company's specific product categories and does not restrict Contractor from: (a) general software development or AI/ML consulting; (b) employment with companies that use AI tools but do not develop competing orchestration platforms; or (c) developing AI applications in unrelated domains.

7.4. **Enforceability.** The Parties agree that the restrictive covenants in this Section 7 are reasonable in scope, duration, and geographic extent. In the event that any provision of this Section 7 is held to be unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, the Parties agree that such court may modify the provision to make it enforceable to the maximum extent permitted by law, consistent with the intent of the Parties. This Agreement shall be construed in accordance with the provisions of the Florida Uniform Trade Secrets Act and Minnesota Statutes Section 181.988 (to the extent applicable).

## SECTION 8. TERM AND TERMINATION

8.1. **Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial period of twelve (12) months (the "Initial Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Section 8. Thereafter, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive six (6) month periods (each, a "Renewal Term") unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days prior to the expiration of the then-current term. The Initial Term and any Renewal Terms are collectively referred to as the "Term."

8.2. **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time, with or without cause, upon fourteen (14) calendar days' prior written notice to the other Party.

8.3. **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if: (a) the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within ten (10) calendar days after receiving written notice of the breach; (b) the other Party becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or has a bankruptcy petition filed against it; or (c) the other Party engages in fraud, gross negligence, or willful misconduct.

8.4. **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement: (a) all outstanding SOWs shall immediately terminate unless otherwise agreed in writing; (b) the Company shall pay Contractor for all Services satisfactorily performed through the effective date of termination, within thirty (30) calendar days of such date; (c) Contractor shall promptly deliver to the Company all completed and in-progress Deliverables; (d) Contractor shall comply with the obligations set forth in Section 6.5; and (e) Contractor shall return all Company property, including access credentials, hardware, and documentation.

8.5. **Survival.** Sections 1, 3.7, 5, 6, 7, 9, 10, 11, 12, and 13 shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement.

## SECTION 11. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

11.1. **Limitation.** IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER PARTY FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO LOSS OF PROFITS, REVENUE, DATA, OR BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, REGARDLESS OF THE CAUSE OF ACTION OR THEORY OF LIABILITY, EVEN IF SUCH PARTY HAS BEEN ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

11.2. **Cap.** THE AGGREGATE LIABILITY OF EITHER PARTY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL COMPENSATION PAID OR PAYABLE TO CONTRACTOR UNDER THIS AGREEMENT DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTH PERIOD PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

11.3. **Exceptions.** The limitations in Sections 11.1 and 11.2 shall not apply to: (a) Contractor's breach of Section 5 (Intellectual Property) or Section 6 (Confidentiality); (b) Contractor's breach of Section 7 (Restrictive Covenants); or (c) either Party's indemnification obligations under Section 10.

## SECTION 12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

12.1. **Negotiation.** The Parties shall attempt to resolve any dispute arising under or relating to this Agreement through good-faith negotiation for a period of thirty (30) calendar days.

12.2. **Mediation.** If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation, the Parties shall submit the dispute to mediation administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") in Hennepin County, Minnesota, before a single mediator.

12.3. **Arbitration.** If mediation fails to resolve the dispute within sixty (60) calendar days of initiation, the dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration administered by the AAA under its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted in Hennepin County, Minnesota, before a single arbitrator. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding, and judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

12.4. **Injunctive Relief.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm, including but not limited to breaches of Sections 5, 6, or 7 of this Agreement.

12.5. **Attorneys' Fees.** The prevailing Party in any dispute under this Agreement shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing Party.
