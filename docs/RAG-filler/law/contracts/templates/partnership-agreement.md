1.4. **"Joint Activities"** means the collaborative activities described in Section 2 and any supplemental activity addenda.

1.7. **"Referral"** means an introduction by one Party to the other Party of a prospective client, customer, or business opportunity, as described in Section 4.

1.8. **"Referral Fee"** means the compensation payable for successful Referrals as specified in Section 4.

## SECTION 2. PARTNERSHIP SCOPE AND JOINT ACTIVITIES

2.1. **Partnership Categories.** This Agreement may encompass one or more of the following partnership categories, as indicated in the Partnership Schedule (Exhibit A):

(a) **University Intern Program Partnership.** Structured placement of Partner's students and graduates in development, AI engineering, and research roles at OrchestratorAI.

(b) **Referral Partnership.** Mutual referral of business opportunities between the Parties.

(c) **Co-Marketing Partnership.** Joint marketing activities, event participation, and content creation.

(d) **Technology Partnership.** Integration, interoperability, or co-development initiatives.

(e) **Training Partnership.** Delivery of AI training programs leveraging OrchestratorAI's curriculum and platform.

2.2. **Joint Activities.** The specific activities under each partnership category shall be described in the Partnership Schedule or in supplemental addenda executed by both Parties from time to time.

2.3. **Coordination.** Each Party shall designate a primary contact person ("Partnership Coordinator") responsible for day-to-day coordination of Joint Activities. The Parties shall hold regular coordination meetings at least [monthly / quarterly] to review progress, address issues, and plan upcoming activities.

2.4. **Independence.** This Agreement does not create a joint venture, legal partnership, agency, or employer-employee relationship. Each Party remains an independent entity. Neither Party has authority to bind or obligate the other.

## SECTION 3. UNIVERSITY INTERN PROGRAM

*[This Section applies if "University Intern Program Partnership" is selected in the Partnership Schedule.]*

3.1. **Program Structure.** The Intern Program shall provide structured work experience in AI software development and engineering. The program includes:

(a) **Duration.** Each intern placement shall be for a period of [12 / 16 / 24] weeks, as specified in the applicable Intern Placement Form (Exhibit B).

(b) **Hours.** Interns shall work approximately [15-20 / 20-30 / 30-40] hours per week, as specified in the Intern Placement Form.

(c) **Compensation.** Interns shall be engaged as independent contractors of OrchestratorAI and compensated at a rate of $[20-35] per hour, as specified in the Intern Placement Form. All interns shall execute OrchestratorAI's standard Independent Contractor Agreement prior to commencing work.

(d) **Mentorship.** OrchestratorAI shall assign a technical mentor to each intern to provide guidance, code review, and professional development support.

3.2. **Selection Process.** (a) Partner shall nominate intern candidates who meet the qualifications specified by OrchestratorAI; (b) OrchestratorAI shall review nominations and conduct technical interviews; (c) OrchestratorAI shall make final selection decisions at its sole discretion; (d) selected interns shall complete OrchestratorAI's onboarding process, including execution of the Independent Contractor Agreement, Non-Disclosure Agreement, and background check authorization.

3.3. **Academic Integration.** The Parties shall cooperate to ensure that the Intern Program satisfies Partner's academic requirements for internship credit, including: (a) providing work plans aligned with learning objectives; (b) conducting mid-term and final evaluations; and (c) completing any required documentation for academic credit.

3.4. **Work Product.** All work product created by interns during their placement shall be subject to the IP assignment provisions of OrchestratorAI's Independent Contractor Agreement. OrchestratorAI retains all right, title, and interest in such work product.

3.5. **Program Costs.** (a) OrchestratorAI shall bear the cost of intern compensation and mentorship. (b) Partner shall bear the cost of intern recruitment, screening, and academic program administration. (c) Neither Party shall charge the other a placement fee unless otherwise specified in the Partnership Schedule.

3.6. **Intern Status.** Interns are independent contractors of OrchestratorAI for the duration of their placement. Interns are not employees of either Party. Neither Party assumes responsibility for the other Party's obligations related to taxes, insurance, or benefits for interns.

3.7. **Program Capacity.** OrchestratorAI may accept up to [NUMBER] interns per academic term, subject to project availability and mentorship capacity. OrchestratorAI reserves the right to adjust capacity with thirty (30) days' notice.

## SECTION 4. REFERRAL PROGRAM

*[This Section applies if "Referral Partnership" is selected in the Partnership Schedule.]*

4.1. **Referral Process.** (a) The referring Party shall introduce the prospective client to the receiving Party by email or through the designated referral portal. (b) The referring Party shall provide the prospective client's name, contact information, and a brief description of the opportunity. (c) The receiving Party shall acknowledge receipt within five (5) business days. (d) A referral shall be deemed "qualified" if the receiving Party confirms that the prospective client meets its target criteria.

4.2. **Referral Fees.** The receiving Party shall pay the referring Party a Referral Fee as follows:

**For OrchestratorAI Platform Deployments:**
| Engagement Value | Referral Fee |
|------------------|--------------|
| Up to $25,000 | 10% of first-year revenue |
| $25,001 - $100,000 | 8% of first-year revenue |
| Over $100,000 | 6% of first-year revenue |

**For AI Training Engagements:**
| Engagement Value | Referral Fee |
|------------------|--------------|
| Any value | 10% of engagement revenue |

**For Divinr.ai Subscriptions:**
| Tier | Referral Fee |
|------|--------------|
| Essentials / Analyst | $50 per conversion |
| Professional | $150 per conversion |
| Enterprise | $500 per conversion |

4.3. **Payment of Referral Fees.** Referral Fees shall be paid within thirty (30) days after the receiving Party collects payment from the referred client. Referral Fees are payable only on the initial engagement or first year of subscription; no recurring commissions apply unless otherwise specified.

4.4. **Referral Validity.** A referral expires if the receiving Party does not close an engagement with the referred client within one hundred eighty (180) days of the referral date. Expired referrals may be re-submitted.

4.5. **Exclusions.** No Referral Fee is payable for: (a) clients who were already in active discussions with the receiving Party at the time of referral; (b) clients who contact the receiving Party independently within thirty (30) days before or after the referral; or (c) self-referrals or referrals of affiliated entities.

## SECTION 5. CO-MARKETING

*[This Section applies if "Co-Marketing Partnership" is selected in the Partnership Schedule.]*

5.1. **Co-Marketing Activities.** The Parties may engage in joint marketing activities including: (a) co-branded content (blog posts, whitepapers, case studies); (b) joint webinars and presentations; (c) co-sponsorship of industry events; (d) cross-promotion on social media and newsletters; and (e) joint press releases.

5.2. **Brand Guidelines.** Each Party shall provide the other with brand guidelines and shall approve all uses of its name, logo, and trademarks in co-marketing materials before publication. Neither Party may alter, modify, or use the other's marks except as expressly approved.

5.3. **Content Approval.** All co-branded content shall be submitted to both Parties for review and approval at least ten (10) business days before publication. Neither Party may publish co-branded content without the other Party's written approval.

5.4. **Costs.** Unless otherwise agreed in a co-marketing addendum, each Party shall bear its own costs for co-marketing activities.

## SECTION 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1. **Mutual Confidentiality.** Each Party shall hold the other's Confidential Information in strict confidence, using at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information, but not less than reasonable care.

6.2. **Use Restriction.** Confidential Information shall be used solely for the purposes of this Agreement.

6.3. **Permitted Disclosures.** A Party may disclose Confidential Information to employees, contractors, and advisors with a need to know who are bound by confidentiality obligations.

6.4. **Exclusions.** Standard exclusions apply: publicly available information, previously known information, independently developed information, and information received from third parties without restriction.

6.5. **Duration.** Confidentiality obligations survive termination for three (3) years; trade secrets protected indefinitely.

## SECTION 7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

7.1. **Ownership.** Each Party retains all right, title, and interest in its pre-existing Intellectual Property. This Agreement does not transfer any IP ownership between the Parties.

7.2. **Joint Works.** Any Intellectual Property jointly created by the Parties in connection with Joint Activities shall be jointly owned, with each Party having the right to use, license, and exploit such joint works without accounting to the other, unless otherwise specified in a supplemental addendum.

7.3. **License Grants.** Each Party grants the other a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license to use its name, logo, and trademarks solely for the purposes described in this Agreement and in accordance with the granting Party's brand guidelines.

## SECTION 10. TERM AND TERMINATION

10.1. **Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an initial period of two (2) years (the "Initial Term"). Thereafter, it automatically renews for successive one-year periods unless either Party provides ninety (90) days' written notice of non-renewal.

10.2. **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate upon sixty (60) days' written notice.

10.3. **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other Party: (a) materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of notice; (b) becomes insolvent; or (c) engages in conduct that materially damages the terminating Party's reputation.

10.4. **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination: (a) all Joint Activities shall wind down in an orderly manner; (b) active intern placements shall continue to their scheduled end dates unless otherwise agreed; (c) Referral Fees for referrals made prior to termination remain payable; (d) each Party shall return the other's Confidential Information; and (e) each Party shall cease using the other's trademarks within thirty (30) days.

10.5. **Survival.** Sections 6, 7, 8.2, 9, and 11 shall survive termination.
