## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose of Client Qualification

Client qualification is the disciplined process of evaluating whether a prospective client is a good fit for OrchestratorAI's products and services. Not every inquiry becomes a client, and not every client produces a successful engagement. Qualification exists to protect both OrchestratorAI and the prospect from investing time and resources in relationships that are unlikely to succeed.

Effective qualification:
- Focuses sales and delivery resources on prospects most likely to succeed
- Reduces churn by avoiding engagements that will fail due to preventable mismatches
- Protects the company's reputation by ensuring we deliver on our promises
- Identifies and addresses risks before they become problems
- Provides data for improving our product-market fit over time

### 1.2 Qualification vs. Selling

Qualification is not a sales activity. It is an evaluation activity. The goal is not to convince the prospect to buy — it is to determine whether buying would be good for both parties. This distinction is critical. An unqualified client who deploys OrchestratorAI and fails becomes a detractor. A properly qualified and declined prospect may become a future client when circumstances change, or a referral source who respects our honesty.

### 1.3 When Qualification Occurs

Qualification is not a single event. It occurs at three stages:

1. **Initial screening** (at first contact) — Is this worth a conversation?
2. **Formal qualification** (after intake questionnaire, before intake meeting) — Should we invest significant time?
3. **Final qualification** (after intake meeting, before engagement letter) — Should we commit to this engagement?

Each stage has progressively higher information requirements and stricter criteria.

### 2.2 Ideal Client Characteristics

Beyond industry vertical, ideal clients share these characteristics:

**Organizational:**
- Annual revenue of $500K or more (ability to afford deployment and subscription)
- At least one person in a technology-adjacent role (IT director, tech-savvy partner, developer)
- Leadership that is open to AI adoption and understands its potential
- Stable business (not in financial distress, bankruptcy, or active dissolution)
- Decision-making process that can be completed within 60 days
- Internal champion who will drive adoption and advocate for the platform

**Technical:**
- Existing IT infrastructure or willingness to invest in it
- Internet connectivity adequate for platform operation
- Ability to designate or procure hardware for on-premises deployment (Mac Studio, DGX Spark, or equivalent)
- Existing digital workflows (not entirely paper-based)
- At least basic security practices (MFA, endpoint protection, backup)

**Financial:**
- Budget for hardware ($3,000-$15,000 depending on tier)
- Budget for deployment services ($5,000-$50,000 depending on scope)
- Budget for monthly platform subscription
- Budget for training ($2,000-$10,000)
- Budget authority identified and accessible
- Willingness to view AI investment as a productivity multiplier, not a cost center

**Cultural:**
- Openness to AI-assisted workflows
- Willingness to invest time in training and adoption
- Collaborative approach to the deployment process
- Realistic expectations about AI capabilities and limitations
- Long-term perspective (AI adoption is a journey, not a switch)

### 2.3 Client Personas

Understanding the key personas within target client organizations helps qualification conversations:

**The Visionary Executive**
- Title: CEO, Managing Partner, President
- Motivation: Competitive advantage, innovation, growth
- Concern: ROI, implementation disruption, reputation risk
- Qualification signal: Asks about outcomes and strategic impact
- Engagement approach: Business case and vision, not technical details

**The Pragmatic Operator**
- Title: COO, Operations Manager, Office Administrator
- Motivation: Efficiency, cost reduction, workflow improvement
- Concern: Staff disruption, learning curve, reliability
- Qualification signal: Asks about day-to-day usage and workflow integration
- Engagement approach: Practical demonstrations, implementation timeline, training plan

**The Technical Evaluator**
- Title: CTO, IT Director, Head of Engineering
- Motivation: Technical elegance, security, scalability
- Concern: Integration complexity, data security, vendor lock-in
- Qualification signal: Asks about architecture, APIs, data handling
- Engagement approach: Technical deep-dive, architecture documentation, security review

**The Skeptical Professional**
- Title: Senior Attorney, Creative Director, Senior Analyst
- Motivation: Quality, professional standards, career protection
- Concern: AI replacing their role, quality of AI output, ethical implications
- Qualification signal: Questions about AI accuracy, oversight, and professional responsibility
- Engagement approach: Position AI as augmentation not replacement, show human-in-the-loop design

**The Budget Guardian**
- Title: CFO, Controller, Finance Director
- Motivation: Financial prudence, measurable returns
- Concern: Total cost of ownership, hidden costs, payback period
- Qualification signal: Asks detailed questions about pricing, implementation costs, ongoing fees
- Engagement approach: Detailed financial analysis, ROI projections, comparable client results

## 3. Qualification Scoring Framework

### 3.1 Scoring Dimensions

Qualification uses a structured scoring framework across seven dimensions. Each dimension is scored 1-5 based on the criteria below.

#### Dimension 1: Budget Fit

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Approved budget exceeds deployment cost. Budget authority is the primary contact. No procurement obstacles. |
| 4 | Budget approved and adequate. Minor procurement steps remain (PO, vendor registration). |
| 3 | Budget identified but not yet approved. Decision maker is engaged and supportive. Approval expected within 30 days. |
| 2 | Budget is vague or aspirational. Decision maker is not yet engaged. Approval timeline uncertain. |
| 1 | No budget identified. Prospect is "exploring" with no financial commitment in sight. Free tier expectations. |

#### Dimension 2: Technical Readiness

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Modern IT infrastructure. In-house IT team or sophisticated MSP. Hardware meets requirements or is ordered. Network is ready. |
| 4 | Adequate IT infrastructure. IT support available. Minor hardware or network upgrades needed. |
| 3 | Basic IT infrastructure. Outsourced IT support. Hardware procurement needed. Network assessment needed. |
| 2 | Minimal IT infrastructure. No dedicated IT support. Significant investment needed before deployment. |
| 1 | No IT infrastructure. Paper-based workflows. No technical capability. Would require complete technology build-out. |

#### Dimension 3: Use Case Fit

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Use case is a core OrchestratorAI strength. Clear, quantifiable value proposition. Multiple applicable products. Prospect's pain is acute. |
| 4 | Strong use case fit. Clear value proposition. Prospect has a defined problem that maps well to our capabilities. |
| 3 | Reasonable use case fit. Value proposition is credible but requires some customization or creative application. |
| 2 | Marginal use case fit. Prospect's need is partially met by our platform. Significant customization or workaround required. |
| 1 | Poor use case fit. Prospect's need does not align with our capabilities. Would require building something we do not have. |

#### Dimension 4: Decision Maker Engagement

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Economic buyer is the primary contact. Champion and decision maker are the same person. No committee approval needed. |
| 4 | Economic buyer is identified, engaged, and supportive. Champion has a direct relationship with decision maker. |
| 3 | Decision maker is identified but not yet directly engaged. Champion is working to secure decision maker involvement. |
| 2 | Decision maker is unclear or multiple decision makers create complexity. No clear champion. Committee process is opaque. |
| 1 | Cannot identify decision maker. Contact is a gatekeeper with no authority. Organizational politics are impeding progress. |

#### Dimension 5: Timeline Alignment

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Prospect has a clear, committed timeline with a defined go-live date. Timeline is realistic for the scope. Internal resources are allocated. |
| 4 | Clear timeline with reasonable flexibility. Go-live date is targeted but not hard. Resources are planned. |
| 3 | General timeline exists ("this quarter," "next quarter") but no specific dates. Resources not yet allocated. |
| 2 | Timeline is vague ("sometime this year"). Competing priorities may delay. No resource allocation. |
| 1 | No timeline. "Just exploring." No urgency. Indefinite evaluation period. |

#### Dimension 6: Security and Compliance Manageability

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Security requirements are standard and well-documented. OrchestratorAI's architecture exceeds requirements. No special compliance burden. |
| 4 | Security requirements are somewhat elevated but within our capability. Compliance requirements are manageable. |
| 3 | Security requirements are significant. Vendor assessment process is defined but lengthy. Compliance creates additional work. |
| 2 | Security requirements are demanding. Extended vendor assessment (60+ days). Multiple compliance frameworks. May require platform modifications. |
| 1 | Security requirements cannot be met without fundamental changes. Compliance requirements are prohibitive. Government clearance requirements we cannot satisfy. |

#### Dimension 7: Cultural Fit and Growth Potential

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Enthusiastic AI adopter. Leadership committed to digital transformation. Clear expansion path (more products, more users, more departments). Collaborative, respectful culture. |
| 4 | Open to AI adoption. Willing to invest in change management. Reasonable expansion potential. Good working relationship. |
| 3 | Cautiously open to AI. Some internal resistance but manageable. Limited expansion potential. Transactional relationship likely. |
| 2 | Skeptical of AI. Significant internal resistance. One-time engagement likely. Difficult working dynamic anticipated. |
| 1 | Hostile to AI adoption (exploring under duress). Highly adversarial culture. No growth potential. Would be a difficult client to serve. |

### 3.2 Scoring Process

1. **Initial Score** (after intake questionnaire, before meeting): Account Manager scores based on questionnaire responses. This is a preliminary assessment with expected uncertainty.

2. **Updated Score** (after intake meeting): Account Manager updates scores based on meeting discussion. All dimensions should now have adequate information for confident scoring.

3. **Final Score** (before engagement letter): Engagement Partner reviews and validates scores. Any score changes are documented with rationale.

### 3.3 Decision Matrix

| Total Score (out of 35) | Qualification | Action |
|--------------------------|---------------|--------|
| **30-35** | **Strong Qualify** | Fast-track to engagement. Priority resource allocation. Reduced approval requirements. |
| **25-29** | **Qualify** | Proceed with standard process. Normal resource allocation and approval. |
| **20-24** | **Conditional Qualify** | Proceed only if specific conditions are met. Document conditions and acceptance criteria. Review with Engagement Partner. |
| **15-19** | **Weak Qualify** | Escalate to Engagement Partner. Proceed only with explicit partner approval and documented risk acceptance. |
| **10-14** | **Defer** | Do not proceed now. Communicate that timing is not right. Set follow-up for 3-6 months. Offer smaller engagement (AI Coding Course, consulting session) if appropriate. |
| **7-9** | **Decline** | Politely decline. Offer referral to alternative resources. Document in CRM. |

### 3.4 Dimension Overrides

Certain dimension scores can override the total score:

**Automatic Disqualification (regardless of total score):**
- Budget Fit = 1 AND the prospect shows no path to funding within 90 days
- Use Case Fit = 1 (we simply cannot help them)
- Security/Compliance = 1 (we cannot meet their requirements)

**Automatic Escalation (requires Engagement Partner decision):**
- Any dimension scored at 1
- Decision Maker Engagement = 2 or lower
- Total score changed by more than 5 points between initial and updated scoring (indicates significant new information)

## 4. Red Flags and Deal Breakers

### 4.1 Red Flags (Investigate Further)

The following are warning signs that require investigation and may affect qualification:

**Financial Red Flags:**
- Prospect pushes back significantly on pricing before understanding value
- Budget discussions are vague or deflected repeatedly
- Prospect asks for extended free trial or proof-of-concept without commitment
- Company shows signs of financial distress (layoffs, downsizing, negative press)
- Prospect wants to negotiate payment terms beyond net-60

**Technical Red Flags:**
- No IT support whatsoever and no willingness to engage an MSP
- Current environment is entirely paper-based with no digital workflow history
- Prospect expects turnkey deployment with zero internal participation
- Unrealistic performance expectations ("we want AI to replace our entire team")
- Prospect has tried and abandoned multiple AI or technology deployments

**Organizational Red Flags:**
- Cannot identify or gain access to the decision maker after two attempts
- Multiple stakeholders with conflicting objectives
- Internal politics are visible during intake discussions
- Prospect is simultaneously evaluating five or more vendors with no clear criteria
- The "champion" has no organizational influence or credibility

**Behavioral Red Flags:**
- Prospect is disrespectful to sales or technical team members
- Repeated missed deadlines for questionnaire completion or document delivery
- Prospect shares confidential information about other vendors' proposals
- Prospect demands references before engaging in substantive discussion
- Prospect wants to restructure our engagement model ("we want to buy just the code")

### 4.2 Deal Breakers (Automatic Decline)

The following are non-negotiable grounds for declining an engagement:

- **Sanctions and legal prohibitions:** Prospect is on OFAC SDN list, BIS Entity List, or any other sanctions list. Prospect is in a jurisdiction we cannot legally serve.

- **Ethical violations:** Prospect intends to use the platform for fraud, deception, manipulation, generation of misleading content, surveillance of individuals without consent, or any purpose that violates our acceptable use policy.

- **Unresolvable conflicts of interest:** Conflict check reveals a direct, material conflict that cannot be waived or screened.

- **Intellectual property infringement:** Prospect wants to use the platform to infringe on third-party IP (copy competitors' content, reverse-engineer proprietary systems, circumvent DRM).

- **Known bad actors:** Prospect has a documented history of fraud, embezzlement, or criminal conduct relevant to the engagement.

- **Deceptive representation:** Prospect has materially misrepresented their company, authority, or financial situation during intake.

## 5. Go/No-Go Decision Process

### 5.1 Go/No-Go Meeting

For prospects scoring 20-29 (Qualify and Conditional Qualify), the Account Manager convenes a brief go/no-go meeting with the Engagement Partner. For prospects scoring 30+, the Account Manager may proceed without a meeting (notification to the Engagement Partner is sufficient).

**Go/No-Go Meeting Agenda (15-30 minutes):**

1. Account Manager presents:
   - Prospect summary (company, industry, size, primary contact)
   - Use case and engagement scope
   - Qualification scores with supporting rationale
   - Red flags identified (if any) and mitigation plan
   - Resource requirements (team, timeline, special skills)
   - Revenue estimate and engagement terms

2. Engagement Partner evaluates:
   - Strategic fit with current portfolio
   - Resource availability
   - Risk assessment
   - Pricing and terms appropriateness
   - Any additional conflict or reputational concerns

3. Decision:
   - **Go:** Proceed to engagement letter. Document any conditions.
   - **Conditional Go:** Proceed only if specified conditions are met within a defined timeframe.
   - **No-Go:** Decline or defer. Document reasons and next steps (referral, follow-up date).

### 5.2 Conditional Qualification

When a prospect is conditionally qualified, the conditions must be:
- Specific and measurable (not vague)
- Time-bound (deadline for meeting the condition)
- Documented in the CRM
- Communicated to the prospect (when appropriate)

**Common Conditions:**
- "Budget must be approved by [date]"
- "Decision maker must attend a follow-up meeting by [date]"
- "Prospect must complete vendor security assessment by [date]"
- "Prospect must procure deployment hardware by [date]"
- "Legal review of engagement terms must be completed by [date]"

If conditions are not met by the deadline, the prospect reverts to "Deferred" status.

### 5.3 Documenting the Decision

Every qualification decision is documented in the CRM with:
- Decision date
- Decision (Qualify / Conditional / Defer / Decline)
- Scoring summary (7 dimensions + total)
- Key factors in the decision
- Conditions (if conditional)
- Next steps
- Decision maker (Account Manager for 25+, Engagement Partner for under 25)

## 6. Pipeline Management

### 6.1 Pipeline Stage Definitions

Prospects move through defined pipeline stages. Each stage has entry criteria and exit criteria.

| Stage | Entry Criteria | Activities | Exit Criteria |
|-------|---------------|------------|---------------|
| **1. Inquiry** | Prospect has contacted us | Initial screening, questionnaire sent | Questionnaire received OR screened out |
| **2. Discovery** | Questionnaire received | Review questionnaire, preliminary qualification, schedule meeting | Intake meeting scheduled OR disqualified |
| **3. Evaluation** | Intake meeting completed | Formal qualification scoring, go/no-go decision | Go decision made OR deferred/declined |
| **4. Proposal** | Go decision made | Engagement letter prepared and sent | Engagement letter executed OR abandoned |
| **5. Onboarding** | Engagement letter executed | File setup, team assignment, kickoff | Deployment kickoff meeting completed |
| **6. Active** | Deployment in progress | Platform deployment, training, go-live | Go-live achieved |

**Special Stages:**
| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Deferred** | Prospect does not qualify now but may in the future. Scheduled follow-up. |
| **Declined** | Prospect does not fit and is unlikely to. Closed with documentation. |
| **Lost** | Prospect chose a competitor or abandoned the evaluation. Closed with documentation and loss reason. |

### 6.2 Stage Velocity Targets

| Stage Transition | Target Duration |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| Inquiry to Discovery | 7 business days |
| Discovery to Evaluation | 10 business days |
| Evaluation to Proposal | 5 business days |
| Proposal to Onboarding | 7 business days |
| Onboarding to Active | 20 business days |
| **Total Inquiry to Active** | **49 business days** |

Prospects that significantly exceed stage duration targets should be reviewed for pipeline health. Stalled deals consume resources and create false forecasting signals.

### 6.3 Pipeline Review

Weekly pipeline review meeting (30 minutes):
- Review all prospects in Discovery and Evaluation stages
- Review any Proposal stage prospects beyond 7 days
- Identify stalled deals and determine action (push, defer, or decline)
- Review lost deals from the past week (what can we learn?)
- Update forecast based on current pipeline health

## 7. Vertical-Specific Qualification Considerations

### 7.1 Legal Firms

**Additional qualification criteria:**
- Firm must have malpractice insurance that does not exclude AI tool usage
- At least one partner must champion the deployment
- Firm must be willing to develop an AI usage policy (if they do not have one)
- Practice area must have document-intensive workflows for meaningful ROI
- Ethics opinion review must be completed before deployment

**Unique disqualifiers:**
- Firm under active bar investigation or disciplinary proceedings
- Firm with outstanding malpractice claims that could create AI-related exposure
- Firm unwilling to implement attorney review requirements for AI output

### 7.2 Financial Services Firms

**Additional qualification criteria:**
- Firm must have a Chief Compliance Officer or outside compliance consultant
- Regulatory registration must be current and in good standing
- Data handling requirements must be compatible with our deployment model
- AI risk tolerance level must be explicitly discussed and documented
- Books and records retention capabilities must be confirmed

**Unique disqualifiers:**
- Firm under active SEC or FINRA enforcement action
- Firm with material regulatory deficiencies that AI deployment could exacerbate
- Firm wanting AI for trading decisions without appropriate governance framework

### 7.3 Marketing Agencies

**Additional qualification criteria:**
- Client roster competitive conflicts must be assessable (agency must be willing to disclose client industries at minimum)
- Data isolation requirements must be compatible with our architecture
- AI disclosure policy must be discussed (some agency clients prohibit AI)
- Creative asset licensing implications must be addressed

**Unique disqualifiers:**
- Agency producing content that violates FTC guidelines or advertising regulations
- Agency unwilling to implement per-client data isolation
- Agency with clients who have categorically prohibited AI on their accounts and the agency wants to use it anyway

### 7.4 Technology Companies

**Additional qualification criteria:**
- Existing development team should have capacity to participate in deployment
- IP implications of AI-generated code must be discussed
- Open-source license compliance must be current
- Export control considerations must be assessed

**Unique disqualifiers:**
- Company under active IP litigation that could be complicated by AI tool adoption
- Company developing weapons systems or surveillance technology without appropriate authorization
- Company with unresolved open-source license violations

## 8. Continuous Improvement

### 8.1 Qualification Accuracy Tracking

After 90 days of an engagement being active, review the original qualification against actual experience:

| Question | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|
| Was the budget fit score accurate? | Over / Under / Accurate |
| Was the technical readiness score accurate? | Over / Under / Accurate |
| Was the use case fit score accurate? | Over / Under / Accurate |
| Was the decision maker engagement score accurate? | Over / Under / Accurate |
| Was the timeline alignment score accurate? | Over / Under / Accurate |
| Were security/compliance requirements as expected? | Over / Under / Accurate |
| Was the cultural fit/growth assessment accurate? | Over / Under / Accurate |
| Were any red flags missed during qualification? | Yes (describe) / No |
| Would we take this client again knowing what we know now? | Yes / No / Conditional |

Aggregate this data quarterly to identify systematic scoring biases and adjust criteria accordingly.

### 8.2 Win/Loss Analysis

For every deal that reaches the Proposal stage and is either won or lost:

**Win Analysis:**
- What were the decisive factors in winning?
- What could we have done better in the process?
- What can we learn about our ideal client from this win?

**Loss Analysis:**
- Why did we lose? (price, features, timing, competitor, internal decision)
- At what stage did we lose momentum?
- Were there early warning signs we missed?
- What can we change to improve future outcomes?

### 8.3 Qualification Framework Updates

This guide should be reviewed and updated:
- Quarterly: review scoring criteria against actual outcomes
- Semi-annually: review ICP definition against closed deals
- Annually: comprehensive review of entire qualification framework
- Ad hoc: when significant market changes, new products, or strategic shifts occur

### Appendix C: Objection Handling for Common Qualification Blockers

| Objection | Response Framework |
|-----------|-------------------|

| "We need to see it working first" | Offer a structured pilot program with defined scope, timeline, and success criteria. Pilot should be paid (signals commitment) but at reduced investment. |
