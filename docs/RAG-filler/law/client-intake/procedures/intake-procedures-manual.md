## 1. Overview and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This manual establishes the standard operating procedures for the complete client intake process at Orchestrator AI LLC and for professional services firms utilizing the OrchestratorAI platform. The intake process is the gateway to every client relationship. Its purpose is to gather complete and accurate information, identify conflicts, qualify the engagement, set expectations, and establish the administrative and technical foundation for a successful client relationship.

A well-executed intake process:
- Protects the firm from conflicts of interest and malpractice exposure
- Ensures the engagement is properly scoped and adequately resourced
- Sets clear expectations with the client from day one
- Creates a complete record that supports the engagement throughout its lifecycle
- Identifies potential issues before they become problems
- Establishes the data foundation for OrchestratorAI platform configuration

### 1.2 Scope

This manual covers the intake process from first contact through file setup and team assignment. It applies to:
- OrchestratorAI platform client onboarding (technology companies, legal firms, financial services firms, marketing agencies, and general business clients)
- Legal firm client intake (for firms using OrchestratorAI to manage their own client relationships)
- Consulting engagement intake

The procedures described herein are mandatory. Deviations require written approval from the Managing Director or Managing Partner.

### 1.3 Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Intake Coordinator** | First point of contact, initial screening, questionnaire distribution, scheduling, document collection |
| **Account Manager / Relationship Manager** | Qualification assessment, intake meeting facilitation, engagement letter preparation, ongoing relationship management |
| **Solutions Architect / Technical Consultant** | Technical environment assessment, platform fit evaluation, deployment planning |
| **Platform Engineer** | Hardware assessment, network evaluation, deployment feasibility, infrastructure readiness |
| **Conflict Check Officer** | Conflict identification, analysis, clearance, and waiver management |
| **Engagement Partner / Managing Director** | Final qualification decision, engagement letter approval, team assignment, matter supervision |
| **Compliance / Ethics Officer** | Regulatory compliance review, ethics clearance (for legal firm intake), data handling policy review |
| **Finance / Billing** | Client account setup, billing preferences, retainer processing, credit assessment |

## 2. Initial Contact Handling

### 2.1 Inquiry Channels

Prospective client inquiries arrive through multiple channels. Each channel has specific handling requirements:

**Website Inquiry (orchestratorai.io contact form)**
- Auto-acknowledgment email sent immediately by the system
- Intake Coordinator reviews within 4 business hours
- Personal response from Intake Coordinator within 24 hours
- Information captured: name, company, email, phone, inquiry type, brief description

**Email Inquiry**
- Intake Coordinator monitors the general inquiry inbox
- Acknowledgment response within 4 business hours during business hours
- Template: "Thank you for your interest in OrchestratorAI. I've received your inquiry and will respond with more information within 24 hours."
- Forward to Account Manager if the inquiry references a specific product or use case

**Phone Inquiry**
- Receptionist or Intake Coordinator captures: caller name, company, phone number, email, brief description of need, how they heard about us
- Enter into CRM immediately
- Schedule callback with Intake Coordinator or Account Manager within 4 business hours
- If the caller is a referral, note the referring source

**Referral**
- Referral source receives acknowledgment within 24 hours (thank you and status update)
- Prospect receives outreach within 24 hours
- Referral source tracked in CRM for relationship management
- Referral fee arrangements documented if applicable

**Event / Conference Lead**
- Leads entered into CRM within 48 hours of event conclusion
- Personalized follow-up email within 3 business days referencing the event and conversation
- Tag with event source for marketing attribution

**Divinr.ai or AI Coding Course Upsell**
- Existing Divinr.ai or course customers expressing interest in the full platform
- Priority handling: these prospects have existing relationships and product familiarity
- Intake Coordinator routes directly to Account Manager with customer history

### 2.2 Initial Screening

Before investing significant time in the intake process, the Intake Coordinator performs an initial screening to determine basic viability. This screening takes 10-15 minutes and uses the following criteria:

**Screen-In Criteria (proceed to full intake):**
- The prospect represents a real business (verifiable company, website, or registration)
- The inquiry describes a legitimate business need that OrchestratorAI products could address
- The contact person appears to have appropriate authority or is connected to a decision maker
- The prospect's industry and size fall within our target market
- No immediately obvious conflicts of interest

**Screen-Out Criteria (polite decline or redirect):**
- Individual consumers seeking personal use (redirect to AI Coding Course if appropriate)
- Requests for products or services we do not offer
- Prospects in jurisdictions we cannot serve
- Obvious competitive intelligence gathering
- Inquiries that appear fraudulent or from sanctioned entities

**Screening Documentation:**
- Record screening decision and rationale in CRM
- If screened out: send polite decline email with alternative resources if possible
- If screened in: proceed to Step 2.3

### 2.3 Initial Response and Questionnaire Distribution

Within 24 hours of screening-in a prospect, the Intake Coordinator:

1. **Sends a personalized welcome email** that includes:
   - Thank you for the inquiry
   - Brief overview of OrchestratorAI and the relevant product area
   - Next steps in the intake process
   - Estimated timeline for the process
   - Link to or attachment of the appropriate intake questionnaire

2. **Selects the appropriate intake questionnaire** based on industry:
   - Technology company: `technology-company-intake-checklist.md`
   - Legal firm: `legal-firm-intake-checklist.md`
   - Financial services: `financial-services-intake-checklist.md`
   - Marketing agency: `marketing-agency-intake-checklist.md`
   - Other / general: `general-business-intake-checklist.md`
   - If the prospect spans multiple verticals, use the general checklist supplemented with relevant sections from vertical-specific checklists

3. **Includes NDA if appropriate:**
   - For prospects who will be sharing sensitive business information during intake
   - Standard mutual NDA template from the engagement library
   - Request execution before detailed technical or business discussions
   - NDA is strongly recommended for legal firms and financial services clients

4. **Sets follow-up cadence:**
   - Day 3: Check-in email if questionnaire not yet received
   - Day 7: Phone call follow-up if questionnaire not yet received
   - Day 14: Final follow-up; if no response, mark as "unresponsive" in CRM
   - Day 30: Close inquiry if no response; send "door is always open" email

## 3. Qualification Criteria

### 3.1 Qualification Framework

Once the intake questionnaire is received, the Account Manager performs a formal qualification assessment. The purpose of qualification is to determine whether the prospect is a good fit for OrchestratorAI and whether we can deliver value within the prospect's constraints.

Qualification evaluates seven dimensions:

**1. Budget Fit**
- Can the prospect afford the platform deployment and ongoing costs?
- OrchestratorAI platform deployments typically require:
  - Hardware: $3,000 - $15,000 (Mac Studio to DGX Spark)
  - Deployment services: $5,000 - $50,000 (depending on scope)
  - Monthly platform subscription: varies by product mix and usage
  - Training: $2,000 - $10,000 per cohort
- Does the prospect have budget authority and approved funding?
- Score: 1 (no budget) to 5 (approved budget exceeds requirements)

**2. Technical Readiness**
- Does the prospect have adequate IT infrastructure or willingness to invest?
- Is there an IT team or managed service provider who can support deployment?
- Is the network infrastructure adequate for platform operation?
- Score: 1 (no IT capability) to 5 (sophisticated IT team, modern infrastructure)

**3. Use Case Fit**
- Does OrchestratorAI solve a real problem the prospect has?
- Is the use case within the platform's capabilities?
- Can we demonstrate clear ROI for the proposed use case?
- Score: 1 (no clear use case) to 5 (perfect fit with quantifiable ROI)

**4. Decision Maker Engagement**
- Is the economic buyer identified and engaged in the process?
- Is there an internal champion who will drive adoption?
- Are there organizational politics or competing priorities that could stall the engagement?
- Score: 1 (no access to decision maker) to 5 (decision maker is the primary contact)

**5. Timeline Alignment**
- Is the prospect's timeline realistic for the scope of deployment?
- Are there hard deadlines that create unacceptable risk?
- Does the prospect have bandwidth to participate in the deployment process?
- Score: 1 (unrealistic timeline or indefinite) to 5 (clear, realistic, aligned timeline)

**6. Security and Compliance Manageability**
- Can we meet the prospect's security requirements?
- Are compliance requirements within our delivery capability?
- Is the vendor assessment process reasonable?
- Score: 1 (requirements we cannot meet) to 5 (standard requirements, easily met)

**7. Cultural Fit and Growth Potential**
- Is the prospect open to AI adoption and willing to invest in change management?
- Is there potential for the engagement to expand over time?
- Does the prospect align with our values and working style?
- Score: 1 (resistant to change, one-time engagement) to 5 (enthusiastic adopter, long-term partner)

### 3.2 Qualification Decision

Total score ranges and recommended actions:

| Score Range | Decision | Action |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| 32-35 | Strong Qualify | Fast-track to engagement. Prioritize scheduling. |
| 25-31 | Qualify | Proceed with standard intake process. |
| 18-24 | Conditional Qualify | Identify and address gaps before proceeding. Document conditions. |
| 11-17 | Weak Qualify | Escalate to Engagement Partner for review. Consider whether gaps are addressable. |
| 7-10 | Disqualify | Polite decline. Offer alternative resources or referral. |

**Disqualification Process:**
When a prospect does not qualify, the Account Manager:
1. Documents the specific reasons for disqualification in the CRM
2. Discusses with the Engagement Partner to confirm the decision
3. Sends a professional decline communication that:
   - Thanks the prospect for their interest
   - Explains (diplomatically) why it is not the right fit at this time
   - Offers specific alternative resources or referrals where possible
   - Leaves the door open for future engagement if circumstances change
4. Sets a 6-month follow-up reminder in case circumstances change

### 3.3 Red Flags

The following red flags during intake require immediate escalation to the Engagement Partner:

- Prospect is evasive about company ownership or control
- Prospect is under active regulatory investigation or enforcement action
- Prospect has unrealistic expectations that cannot be managed
- Prospect is pressuring for deployment without proper security review
- Prospect wants to use the platform for purposes that conflict with our acceptable use policy
- Prospect's business model raises ethical concerns
- Prospect has a history of litigation against technology vendors
- Prospect refuses to sign an NDA but wants to discuss sensitive deployment details
- Prospect's primary use case involves generating content to deceive or manipulate
- Prospect is on any sanctions list (OFAC, BIS Entity List, etc.)

## 4. Intake Meeting

### 4.1 Pre-Meeting Preparation

The Account Manager prepares for the intake meeting by:

1. **Reviewing the completed intake questionnaire** (minimum 48 hours before meeting)
2. **Distributing the questionnaire** to all team members attending the meeting
3. **Preparing the meeting agenda** using the template from the appropriate industry checklist
4. **Identifying gaps** in the questionnaire that need to be addressed during the meeting
5. **Preparing preliminary qualification assessment** to share with the team
6. **Conducting preliminary research** on the prospect:
   - Company website review
   - LinkedIn profiles of key contacts
   - News search for recent company developments
   - Industry context and trends
   - Competitive landscape
7. **Preparing a brief OrchestratorAI overview** tailored to the prospect's industry and use case
8. **Setting up the meeting environment:**
   - Conference room or video call link
   - Screen sharing capability for demos
   - Recording permission (if recording, obtain consent)
   - Note-taking responsibilities assigned

### 4.2 Meeting Conduct

**Opening (10 minutes)**
- Introductions: all attendees, roles, and what they bring to the discussion
- Confirm the purpose of the meeting: mutual evaluation to determine fit
- Set expectations: this is a collaborative discussion, not a sales pitch
- Confirm the agenda and time allocation
- Ask permission to take notes

**Company/Firm Profile Discussion (15-20 minutes)**
- Walk through the questionnaire responses, clarifying and expanding as needed
- Focus on understanding the prospect's business model, not just checking boxes
- Ask open-ended questions: "Tell me about..." and "Walk me through..."
- Listen for unstated needs, pain points, and organizational dynamics
- Document any corrections or additions to questionnaire responses

**Technical Assessment (15-20 minutes)**
- Platform Engineer or Solutions Architect leads this section
- Deep-dive into current technology environment
- Assess infrastructure readiness for OrchestratorAI deployment
- Identify technical prerequisites and potential obstacles
- Discuss deployment architecture options (local, cloud, hybrid)
- Present hardware recommendations based on the prospect's needs and scale

**Security and Compliance Discussion (10-15 minutes)**
- Review data sensitivity and classification requirements
- Discuss regulatory compliance obligations
- Explain OrchestratorAI's security architecture:
  - Local-first deployment: data stays on-premises
  - Sovereign mode: all AI inference runs locally
  - Supabase database runs locally on deployment hardware
  - No data transmitted to external services unless explicitly configured
- Identify any security review or vendor assessment requirements
- Discuss timeline implications of security review processes

**Use Case and Success Criteria Discussion (10-15 minutes)**
- Revisit and refine the problem statement
- Map specific OrchestratorAI capabilities to the prospect's needs
- Discuss realistic outcomes and timeline for value delivery
- Agree on measurable success criteria
- Identify potential quick wins for early value demonstration

**Budget and Timeline Discussion (10 minutes)**
- Confirm budget parameters
- Discuss pricing model and expected investment
- Align on deployment timeline
- Identify any budget approval steps remaining
- Discuss payment terms and billing preferences

**Next Steps (5-10 minutes)**
- Summarize key discussion points and decisions
- List action items with owners and deadlines
- Identify remaining documents to be collected
- Confirm the path to engagement letter / MSA
- Set the next meeting date

### 4.3 Post-Meeting Documentation

Within 24 hours of the intake meeting, the Account Manager:

1. **Distributes meeting notes** to all attendees (internal and external)
2. **Updates the CRM** with:
   - Meeting date and attendees
   - Key discussion points and decisions
   - Updated qualification score
   - Next steps and action items
   - Estimated deal value and close date
3. **Sends a thank-you email** to the prospect that includes:
   - Summary of key discussion points
   - Confirmed next steps and timeline
   - List of outstanding document requests
   - OrchestratorAI team contact information
4. **Initiates conflict check** (if not already completed)
5. **Briefs the Engagement Partner** on the meeting outcome and recommendation

## 5. Conflict Check Procedures

### 5.1 When to Perform Conflict Checks

A conflict check is mandatory:
- Before any substantive discussions about the prospect's business or legal matters
- Before signing an engagement letter or MSA
- Before accessing any confidential prospect information
- When a new matter is opened for an existing client
- When new parties are identified during an ongoing engagement

### 5.2 Information Required for Conflict Check

The Conflict Check Officer requires:
- Full legal name of the prospective client entity
- All d/b/a and trade names
- Names of all principals, officers, directors, and owners
- Names of all subsidiaries and affiliates
- Names of all known adverse parties or opponents
- Names of key customers, vendors, and partners (for potential indirect conflicts)
- Brief description of the engagement scope
- Industry and market segment

### 5.3 Conflict Check Process

**Step 1: Database Search (Same Day)**
- Search the firm's conflict check database / CRM for all names provided
- Search variations of names (abbreviations, former names, common misspellings)
- Search by individual names of all principals
- Document all potential matches

**Step 2: Analysis (1 Business Day)**
- For each potential match, determine:
  - Is there a direct conflict (current client with adverse interests)?
  - Is there a related party conflict (affiliated entity, subsidiary, or principal)?
  - Is there a former client conflict (substantially related matter)?
  - Is there a business conflict (not an ethical conflict but a relationship risk)?
- Document the analysis for each potential match

**Step 3: Resolution (1-2 Business Days)**
- **No conflict found:** Issue clearance memorandum. Proceed with engagement.
- **Potential conflict identified:** Escalate to Engagement Partner and Ethics Officer for review.
  - Can the conflict be resolved by ethical screening (information barriers)?
  - Is a conflict waiver appropriate and obtainable?
  - Must the engagement be declined?
- **Conflict confirmed, waiver possible:**
  - Draft informed consent / conflict waiver letter
  - Both the prospect and the existing client must consent (if current client conflict)
  - Consent must be in writing and document the specific conflict
  - Establish and document ethical screens if engagement proceeds
- **Conflict confirmed, no waiver possible:**
  - Decline the engagement
  - Document the reason
  - Do not disclose the identity of the conflicting party to the prospect

### 5.4 Conflict Check Documentation

All conflict checks must be documented with:
- Date of the conflict check
- Names searched
- Search results (including "no match" results)
- Analysis of any potential matches
- Resolution and clearance (or declination)
- Approval signature of Conflict Check Officer
- Approval signature of Engagement Partner (if potential conflict was identified)
- Conflict waiver (if applicable)

Conflict check records are retained permanently.

## 6. Engagement Letter Execution

### 6.1 Engagement Letter Preparation

After conflict clearance, the Account Manager prepares the engagement letter or Master Services Agreement (MSA) within 2 business days. The engagement document includes:

**Standard Terms:**
- Parties (full legal names)
- Scope of engagement (specific services to be provided)
- Excluded scope (what is NOT included)
- Fee structure and payment terms
- Retainer requirements (if applicable)
- Billing frequency and format
- Expense policy
- Term and termination provisions
- Confidentiality obligations (mutual)
- Data handling and privacy terms
- Intellectual property ownership
- Limitation of liability
- Dispute resolution mechanism
- Governing law and jurisdiction

**Platform-Specific Terms (for OrchestratorAI deployments):**
- Platform license scope and permitted users
- Deployment model (on-premises, cloud, hybrid)
- Data sovereignty provisions
- Service level commitments (if applicable)
- Support and maintenance terms
- Update and upgrade policy
- Data portability and exit provisions
- Acceptable use policy
- Security incident notification obligations

**Legal Firm-Specific Terms (for legal firm clients of legal firms):**
- Attorney-client relationship establishment
- Scope of representation
- Conflicts disclosure
- Fee arrangement (hourly, flat, contingency)
- Trust account provisions
- File retention policy
- Withdrawal provisions
- Client obligations (cooperation, truthfulness, timely payment)

### 6.2 Review and Approval

Before sending to the prospect:

1. **Account Manager reviews** for accuracy and completeness
2. **Engagement Partner reviews** and approves terms
3. **Finance reviews** fee structure and payment terms
4. **Legal/Compliance reviews** (for non-standard terms or high-value engagements)
5. **Final approval** by Engagement Partner (signature authorization)

### 6.3 Execution Process

1. Send the engagement letter to the prospect's authorized signer via secure delivery (encrypted email or secure document signing platform)
2. Allow 3-5 business days for review and execution
3. If the prospect requests changes:
   - Account Manager documents requested changes
   - Escalate material changes to Engagement Partner for approval
   - Issue revised engagement letter with tracked changes
   - Do not accept verbal modifications — all changes must be in writing
4. Upon execution:
   - Countersign on behalf of OrchestratorAI (Engagement Partner or authorized signer)
   - Distribute fully executed copy to:
     - Client
     - Account Manager
     - Platform Engineer (if deployment engagement)
     - Finance / Billing
     - File
5. If the prospect does not execute within 10 business days:
   - Account Manager follows up
   - If no response after 20 business days, the engagement offer expires
   - Prospect must restart intake if they return after expiration

## 7. File Setup and Administrative Onboarding

### 7.1 Client Record Creation

Within 1 business day of engagement letter execution:

1. **Create client record in CRM:**
   - Company name, address, contacts
   - Engagement type and scope
   - Billing information
   - Engagement team members
   - Key dates (engagement start, renewal, review milestones)

2. **Create matter/project in project management system:**
   - Matter name and number
   - Client linkage
   - Practice area / service line
   - Responsible partner/director
   - Originating partner/director (for credit tracking)
   - Billing arrangements (rates, caps, budgets)
   - Status: Active

3. **Set up billing:**
   - Client billing preferences (electronic, paper, format)
   - Payment method (ACH, check, credit card)
   - Invoice routing (who receives, who approves)
   - Billing frequency
   - Trust/retainer deposit processing
   - Rate schedule entry

4. **Create document repository:**
   - Client folder in document management system
   - Standard subfolder structure:
     - `00-Engagement` (engagement letter, NDA, conflict check)
     - `01-Intake` (questionnaire, meeting notes, documents collected)
     - `02-Planning` (deployment plan, project plan, architecture)
     - `03-Correspondence` (emails, letters, memos)
     - `04-Deliverables` (work product, reports, configurations)
     - `05-Billing` (invoices, payment records)
   - Set access permissions (team members only)

5. **Create secure communication channel:**
   - Client portal setup (if applicable)
   - Secure file sharing link
   - Encrypted email configuration (if required)

### 7.2 Document Filing

File all intake documents in the client repository:
- Executed engagement letter / MSA
- Executed NDA (if applicable)
- Completed intake questionnaire
- Intake meeting notes
- Conflict check clearance memorandum
- All documents collected during intake (corporate documents, policies, diagrams, etc.)
- Qualification assessment scoring
- Any correspondence from the intake process

### 7.3 Welcome Package

Within 2 business days of engagement execution, send the client a welcome package:

**For Platform Clients:**
- Welcome letter from the Engagement Partner
- Team introduction (names, roles, contact information)
- Deployment timeline overview
- Hardware procurement guide (if applicable)
- Pre-deployment preparation checklist
- Support contact information and hours
- Portal login credentials (if applicable)
- Calendar invitations for kickoff meeting and key milestones

**For Legal/Consulting Clients:**
- Welcome letter from the Engagement Partner
- Team introduction (names, roles, contact information)
- Engagement timeline and key milestones
- Client portal login credentials (if applicable)
- Document submission instructions
- Communication preferences confirmation
- Billing schedule and payment instructions

## 8. Team Assignment

### 8.1 Team Assignment Criteria

The Engagement Partner assigns the engagement team based on:

- **Expertise match:** Team members with relevant industry and technical experience
- **Capacity:** Current workload and availability of team members
- **Client fit:** Personality and communication style alignment
- **Continuity:** Preference for team members who participated in intake
- **Development:** Opportunity for junior team members to gain experience (with appropriate supervision)
- **Geographic proximity:** For on-site deployment work, prefer team members near the client

### 8.2 Standard Team Composition

**Platform Deployment Team:**
| Role | Responsibility | Typical Seniority |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Engagement Partner | Relationship oversight, escalation point | Senior |
| Account Manager | Day-to-day relationship management, project coordination | Mid-Senior |
| Solutions Architect | Technical design, architecture decisions | Senior |
| Platform Engineer | Deployment, configuration, testing | Mid |
| Training Specialist | User onboarding, training delivery | Mid |

**Legal/Consulting Engagement Team:**
| Role | Responsibility | Typical Seniority |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| Engagement Partner | Matter supervision, client relationship | Senior |
| Associate | Day-to-day matter work, research, drafting | Junior-Mid |
| Paralegal | Document management, research support, filings | Support |
| Administrative Assistant | Scheduling, correspondence, billing | Support |

### 8.3 Team Notification and Briefing

Within 1 business day of team assignment:

1. **Notify all team members** of the assignment
2. **Distribute the engagement file** (intake questionnaire, meeting notes, engagement letter, relevant documents)
3. **Schedule a team briefing meeting** (30 minutes):
   - Client overview and engagement scope
   - Team roles and responsibilities
   - Key contacts at the client
   - Timeline and milestones
   - Special considerations (confidentiality requirements, regulatory constraints, etc.)
   - Immediate next steps and assignments
4. **Update the CRM** with team assignments
5. **Set up team communication** (dedicated channel in messaging platform if warranted)

## 10. Special Procedures

### 10.1 Emergency / Expedited Intake

In rare cases, a prospect may require expedited intake (e.g., pending litigation deadline, regulatory filing deadline, competitive pressure). Expedited intake:

- Requires Engagement Partner approval
- Minimum requirements: conflict check, basic company information, verbal scope agreement
- Engagement letter may be executed under abbreviated terms with a commitment to complete full terms within 30 days
- Full intake documentation must be completed within 15 business days of engagement start
- Document the reason for expedited processing

### 10.2 Re-Engagement of Former Clients

When a former client returns:
- Retrieve and review the original intake file
- Perform a new conflict check (circumstances may have changed)
- Update all company information (the questionnaire serves as update vehicle)
- Review the reason the prior engagement ended
- If the prior engagement ended due to non-payment or other issues, require Engagement Partner approval
- Verify that prior engagement files have been properly retained or destroyed per policy

### 10.3 Referral to Other Firms or Resources

When OrchestratorAI is not the right fit for a prospect, consider referral to:
- Partner law firms (for legal matters outside our scope)
- Technology consultants in our network
- Other AI platform vendors (when our capabilities do not match the need)
- Industry associations or professional organizations
- Government resources (SBA, SBDC, etc.)

Document referrals in the CRM. Follow up at 30 days to check on the outcome.

### 10.4 Intake for Multi-Entity Engagements

When a prospect involves multiple related entities (parent and subsidiaries, affiliated companies):

- Perform conflict checks on ALL entities
- Determine which entity is the contracting party
- Document the relationship between entities
- Determine whether separate engagement letters are needed per entity
- Assess whether data isolation between entities is required on the platform
- Address potential future conflicts (e.g., subsidiary disputes with parent)
