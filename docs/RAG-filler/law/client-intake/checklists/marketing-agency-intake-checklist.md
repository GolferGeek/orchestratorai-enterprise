## Purpose

Marketing agencies operate in an environment where client confidentiality, creative IP ownership, and rapid content production intersect. This checklist addresses the unique intake requirements for marketing agencies, including client roster protection, content creation workflows, brand guideline management, campaign data handling, creative asset management, and assessment of existing AI tool usage.

OrchestratorAI's platform offers marketing agencies significant productivity gains through AI-assisted content creation, campaign analysis, and knowledge management — but deployment must account for the complex web of client NDAs, brand guidelines, and competitive separation that agencies navigate daily.

## Notes

- **Client roster confidentiality is paramount.** Many agencies consider their client list itself to be confidential information. Handle accordingly during intake.
- **Competitive separation is the top concern** for most agency deployments. OrchestratorAI's multi-tenant architecture with per-client data isolation directly addresses this, but the specifics must be documented and verified during deployment.
- **AI disclosure policies are evolving rapidly.** Some clients welcome AI assistance, others prohibit it entirely. The platform must support per-client AI usage policies enforced at the configuration level.
- **Creative asset licensing** is a hidden risk area. AI-generated content that incorporates or closely resembles licensed stock assets may create licensing compliance issues. Address this during the AI governance discussion.
- **Agency acquisitions and mergers** are common in this industry. If the agency is contemplating or undergoing a transaction, deployment timing and data migration should be discussed.
