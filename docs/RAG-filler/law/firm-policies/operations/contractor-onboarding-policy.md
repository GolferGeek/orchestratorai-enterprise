## 1. Purpose

This Independent Contractor Onboarding Policy ("Policy") establishes the procedures, requirements, and standards for engaging, onboarding, and integrating independent contractors and interns into Orchestrator AI LLC's ("the Company") operations. As a single-member LLC that operates exclusively with 1099 independent contractors and interns (no W-2 employees), the Company must ensure that all engagements are properly classified, documented, and compliant with applicable tax and labor laws, and that all personnel are equipped with the access, training, and information necessary to perform their roles effectively and securely.

The Company processes sensitive client data — legal documents, financial predictions, proprietary code — through the OrchestratorAI platform and Divinr.ai. Rigorous onboarding procedures are essential to ensure that every individual with access to Company systems understands their obligations regarding data security, client confidentiality, and intellectual property.

---

## 2. Scope

This Policy applies to:

(a) All new independent contractors (1099) engaged by the Company for any purpose, including development, design, marketing, sales, consulting, and administrative functions.

(b) All interns, including university interns (e.g., from the University of St. Thomas) and other internship arrangements.

(c) The Managing Member, in their capacity as the individual responsible for executing the onboarding process.

(d) Renewals and extensions of existing contractor engagements, where material changes in scope, access, or responsibilities occur.

---

## 3. Definitions

**Independent Contractor:** An individual or entity engaged to perform services for the Company under an Independent Contractor Agreement, classified as a non-employee for federal and state tax purposes, and compensated via IRS Form 1099-NEC.

**Intern:** An individual engaged in a structured learning experience with the Company, which may be paid or unpaid in accordance with applicable law.

**Engagement Agreement:** The written contract governing the terms of an independent contractor's or intern's engagement with the Company, including scope of work, compensation, duration, and applicable terms and conditions.

**1099 Classification:** The tax classification of an individual as an independent contractor rather than an employee, subject to IRS guidelines and applicable state law.

**IP Assignment:** The contractual transfer of intellectual property rights from the contractor to the Company for work product created in the course of the engagement.

**NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement):** A legally binding agreement prohibiting the disclosure of confidential information.

## 4. Pre-Engagement Requirements

### 4.1 1099 Classification Verification

Before engaging any individual as an independent contractor, the Managing Member shall verify that the proposed engagement meets the criteria for independent contractor classification under applicable law. The classification analysis shall consider the following factors:

#### 4.1.1 IRS Common-Law Test Factors

(a) **Behavioral Control:** The Company does not control how the contractor performs the work. The Company specifies deliverables, deadlines, and quality standards, but the contractor determines the methods, tools, and schedule.

(b) **Financial Control:** The contractor has the opportunity for profit or loss, provides their own tools and equipment (or is compensated for Company-provided tools), is paid per project/milestone or at an agreed rate (not a salary), and may work for other clients simultaneously.

(c) **Relationship Type:** The engagement is defined by a written contract specifying the independent contractor relationship. No benefits (health insurance, retirement, paid leave) are provided. The engagement is project-based or for a defined term, not indefinite.

#### 4.1.2 State-Specific Requirements

(a) **Florida:** Classification shall comply with Florida's independent contractor standards under Fla. Stat. Section 440.02 and applicable Department of Revenue guidance.

(b) **Minnesota:** Classification shall comply with Minnesota's independent contractor requirements under Minn. Stat. Section 181.723 (for specific industries) and general common-law analysis. Minnesota applies a stricter analysis for certain industries.

(c) If the contractor is located in another state, the Managing Member shall verify compliance with that state's contractor classification requirements.

#### 4.1.3 Documentation of Classification

(a) The Managing Member shall document the classification analysis, noting the specific factors supporting independent contractor status.

(b) The classification analysis shall be retained with the contractor's engagement file for a minimum of seven (7) years.

(c) If the classification analysis raises doubts about proper classification, the Managing Member shall consult with legal counsel or a tax professional before proceeding with the engagement.

### 4.2 Background Verification

(a) For contractors who will have access to client data, production systems, or sensitive Company infrastructure, the Company may conduct background verification, including:
  - Verification of professional references
  - Review of public professional profiles (LinkedIn, GitHub)
  - Verification of claimed qualifications and experience
  - Criminal background check (with contractor's written consent, where permitted by law)

(b) Background verification requirements shall be communicated to the prospective contractor before the engagement begins.

(c) For interns, the Company shall verify enrollment status with the applicable educational institution.

## 5. Agreement Execution

### 5.1 Required Agreements

Before any work begins or system access is granted, the following agreements must be fully executed:

#### 5.1.1 Independent Contractor Agreement (ICA)

The ICA shall include, at minimum:

(a) **Parties:** Full legal names of the Company and the contractor (or contractor's business entity).

(b) **Scope of Work:** Detailed description of services to be performed, deliverables, milestones, and acceptance criteria.

(c) **Compensation:** Rate or fee structure, payment terms (consistent with the Credit Approval and Collections Policy), and invoicing procedures.

(d) **Duration:** Start date, end date or conditions for termination, and renewal provisions.

(e) **Independent Contractor Status:** Clear statement that the individual is an independent contractor, not an employee, and is responsible for their own taxes, insurance, and benefits.

(f) **Termination:** Conditions under which either party may terminate the engagement, including notice periods and obligations upon termination.

(g) **Insurance:** Requirement that the contractor maintain appropriate insurance (general liability, professional liability/E&O) as applicable to the engagement.

(h) **Governing Law:** Florida law, with Minnesota venue for disputes arising from Minnesota operations, as appropriate.

#### 5.1.2 Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

The NDA shall cover:

(a) Definition of confidential information, including client data, Company proprietary information, trade secrets, platform architecture, and business strategies.

(b) Obligations of non-disclosure and non-use for any purpose other than performing the contracted services.

(c) Duration of confidentiality obligations (typically surviving the engagement by three (3) to five (5) years, with trade secret protections surviving indefinitely).

(d) Return or destruction of confidential information upon termination.

(e) Permitted disclosures (e.g., as required by law, with notice to the Company).

#### 5.1.3 Intellectual Property Assignment Agreement

The IP Assignment Agreement shall include:

(a) Assignment of all intellectual property rights in work product created in the course of the engagement to the Company.

(b) Definition of "work product" to include code, designs, documentation, inventions, discoveries, and any other materials created in performance of the contracted services.

(c) Waiver of moral rights to the extent permitted by law.

(d) Contractor's representation that the work product will be original and will not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights.

(e) **Pre-Existing IP Exclusion:** Identification of any pre-existing intellectual property that the contractor will use in performing services, with a license grant to the Company for such pre-existing IP as needed.

(f) Cooperation clause requiring the contractor to execute additional documents necessary to perfect the Company's ownership of the work product.

#### 5.1.4 Intern Agreement (for Interns)

In addition to or in place of the ICA, interns shall execute an Intern Agreement that includes:

(a) Educational objectives of the internship.

(b) Duration and schedule.

(c) Compensation (if paid) or acknowledgment of unpaid status (if unpaid, with compliance with DOL and applicable state guidelines).

(d) Supervision arrangements.

(e) NDA and IP Assignment provisions (which may be incorporated or executed separately).

(f) University credit arrangements (if applicable).

### 5.2 Tax Documentation

(a) **IRS Form W-9:** Each U.S.-based contractor shall provide a completed W-9 (Request for Taxpayer Identification Number and Certification) before any payment is issued.

(b) **IRS Form W-8BEN/W-8BEN-E:** International contractors shall provide the applicable W-8 form.

(c) **Form 1099-NEC:** The Company shall issue Form 1099-NEC to each contractor paid $600 or more in a calendar year, in accordance with IRS deadlines (January 31 of the following year).

(d) Tax documentation shall be retained for a minimum of seven (7) years.

## 6. Tool Access Provisioning

### 6.1 Access Provisioning Sequence

After all agreements are fully executed and tax documentation is received, access shall be provisioned in the following order:

#### Step 1: Identity and Authentication

(a) Create user account in the OrchestratorAI Auth service (port 6100/7100).

(b) Assign organizational role and entitlements through the Admin service (port 6101/7101), following the principle of least privilege.

(c) Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on the new account.

#### Step 2: Communication Tools

(a) Add to Company communication channels (Slack workspace, email distribution lists) as appropriate for the role.

(b) Add to relevant project channels and groups.

#### Step 3: Development and Collaboration Tools

(a) **GitHub:** Invite to the appropriate GitHub repository or repositories with the minimum necessary access level:
  - Read access for review-only roles
  - Write access for active contributors
  - Admin access only for the Managing Member and designated leads

(b) **Tailscale:** Add to the Company's Tailscale network if the contractor requires direct access to Company infrastructure (e.g., for development or operations work). Assign appropriate ACLs.

(c) **IDE and Development Environment:** Provide access to development environment documentation, including:
  - Repository setup instructions
  - Environment variable templates (.env.example)
  - Local development port assignments (6xxx series)
  - Docker configuration

#### Step 4: Platform Services (Role-Dependent)

(a) **Development Roles:** Access to development instances of platform services. NO access to production systems or production data initially.

(b) **Operations Roles:** Access to production monitoring and observability tools. Production write access only after security training completion and Managing Member approval.

(c) **Marketing/Sales Roles (including interns):** Access to CRM, marketing tools, and Company social media accounts (as applicable). NO access to development tools, production systems, or client data.

### 6.2 Access Documentation

(a) All access granted shall be documented in an access log, including:
  - Systems and tools provisioned
  - Access level granted
  - Date of provisioning
  - Provisioned by (Managing Member or delegate)

(b) The access log shall be stored securely and reviewed quarterly.

## 7. Security Training

### 7.1 Required Training

All new contractors and interns shall complete security and compliance training within seven (7) calendar days of access provisioning. Training shall cover:

#### Module 1: Company Policies

(a) Review of the Acceptable Use Policy (OAI-OPS-001).

(b) Review of the Data Privacy and Security Policy (OAI-OPS-002).

(c) Review of the Client Confidentiality Policy (OAI-ETH-001).

(d) Review of the Remote Work Policy (OAI-OPS-004) (if working remotely).

(e) Review of the Social Media Policy (OAI-ETH-003) (if the role involves public communications).

(f) Review of the Anti-Discrimination and Harassment Policy (OAI-ETH-005).

(g) Review of the Whistleblower and Ethics Reporting Policy (OAI-ETH-004).

#### Module 2: Data Security

(a) Client data handling procedures and data classification levels.

(b) Encryption requirements (at rest and in transit).

(c) Password and credential management best practices.

(d) API key security and rotation procedures.

(e) Incident reporting procedures.

#### Module 3: Platform Security

(a) OrchestratorAI platform architecture overview (relevant to the contractor's role).

(b) ExecutionContext and data isolation concepts.

(c) Proper use of development vs. production environments.

(d) Tailscale VPN usage and requirements.

(e) Security logging and monitoring awareness.

#### Module 4: Role-Specific Training

(a) **Developers:** Git workflow, code review requirements, CI/CD pipeline, test requirements, no-client-data-in-repositories rule.

(b) **Marketing/Sales (including interns):** LinkedIn outreach guidelines, client confidentiality on social media, Divinr.ai disclaimer requirements, CRM usage.

(c) **Operations:** Backup procedures, monitoring, incident response roles, production access protocols.

### 7.2 Training Completion

(a) Completion of all required training modules shall be documented, including the date of completion and the modules covered.

(b) Access to client data and production systems shall not be granted until security training is complete.

(c) If training is not completed within the seven (7) day window, system access may be suspended until training is completed.

### 7.3 Ongoing Training

(a) Annual security refresher training is required for all active contractors and interns.

(b) Additional training shall be provided when:
  - Material policy changes are implemented
  - New tools or systems are introduced
  - Security incidents warrant additional awareness

## 8. IP Assignment Verification

### 8.1 Initial Verification

(a) Before any work begins, the Managing Member shall verify that:
  - The IP Assignment Agreement has been fully executed
  - Any pre-existing IP that the contractor intends to use has been identified and documented
  - The contractor understands that work product created during the engagement belongs to the Company

(b) For contractors providing software development services, the Managing Member shall verify that the contractor is not subject to conflicting IP assignment obligations from other engagements.

### 8.2 Ongoing Verification

(a) Code commits shall include standard attribution practices (Git author information).

(b) The Managing Member shall periodically verify that contractor-submitted code does not include:
  - Code copied from other projects without proper licensing
  - Code subject to restrictive open-source licenses (GPL, AGPL) that would be incompatible with the Company's licensing model, unless specifically approved
  - Third-party proprietary code used without authorization

(c) Any concerns about IP ownership or contamination shall be raised immediately with the Managing Member and, if necessary, outside legal counsel.

## 10. Offboarding

### 10.1 Triggering Events

Offboarding procedures shall be initiated upon:

(a) Completion of the contracted scope of work.

(b) Expiration of the engagement agreement without renewal.

(c) Termination by either party for any reason.

(d) Completion of the internship term.

### 10.2 Offboarding Procedures

Upon triggering an offboarding event, the following steps shall be completed within one (1) business day of the effective termination date:

#### Step 1: Access Revocation

(a) Disable the contractor's Auth service account.

(b) Remove GitHub repository access.

(c) Remove Tailscale network access.

(d) Remove from communication channels (Slack, email distribution lists).

(e) Revoke access to any third-party tools provisioned for the engagement.

(f) Rotate any shared credentials or API keys that the contractor had access to.

#### Step 2: Asset Return

(a) Company-provided equipment shall be returned within five (5) business days.

(b) Any Company data on personal devices shall be confirmed deleted (self-attestation from the contractor).

(c) Physical access materials (keys, badges) shall be returned, if applicable.

#### Step 3: Knowledge Transfer

(a) Ensure all work product has been committed to the appropriate repositories or delivered through agreed-upon channels.

(b) Complete documentation of any in-progress work.

(c) Handoff meeting with the Managing Member or successor, as appropriate.

#### Step 4: Final Payment

(a) Process final invoice and payment in accordance with the engagement agreement and the Credit Approval and Collections Policy.

(b) Issue Form 1099-NEC at year end (if applicable).

### 10.3 Offboarding Checklist

- [ ] Auth service account disabled
- [ ] GitHub access revoked
- [ ] Tailscale access revoked
- [ ] Communication channel access revoked
- [ ] Third-party tool access revoked
- [ ] Shared credentials rotated
- [ ] Company equipment returned
- [ ] Personal device data deletion confirmed
- [ ] Work product delivered/committed
- [ ] Knowledge transfer completed
- [ ] Final payment processed
- [ ] Offboarding documented

## 11. Record Retention

(a) Contractor and intern engagement files (agreements, classification analyses, onboarding/offboarding checklists, training records, access logs) shall be retained for a minimum of seven (7) years following the termination of the engagement.

(b) Tax documentation (W-9s, 1099s) shall be retained for a minimum of seven (7) years.

(c) Records shall be stored securely with access restricted to the Managing Member.
