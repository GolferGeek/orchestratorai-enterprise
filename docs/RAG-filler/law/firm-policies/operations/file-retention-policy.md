## Article I: Purpose and Scope

### Section 1.1: Policy Purpose

This policy establishes uniform standards for the retention, storage, and destruction of client files, administrative records, and firm documents at Smith & Associates, PLLC. Proper records management:

1. Ensures compliance with legal and ethical obligations
2. Protects client interests and confidentiality
3. Manages storage costs effectively
4. Provides access to historical records when needed
5. Supports defense against malpractice claims
6. Facilitates orderly file disposition

### Section 1.2: Scope of Application

#### 1.2.1 Covered Records

This policy applies to all firm records, including:

**Client Matter Files:**
- Paper documents
- Electronic files and databases
- Email communications
- Voicemail recordings
- Physical evidence
- Client property

**Administrative Records:**
- Financial records
- Personnel files
- Firm governance documents
- Marketing materials
- Vendor contracts
- Insurance policies

**Work Product:**
- Legal research
- Draft documents
- Internal memoranda
- Strategy notes
- Expert communications

#### 1.2.2 Responsible Personnel

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Records Manager | Policy implementation; storage oversight |
| Practice Group Leaders | Compliance within practice groups |
| Supervising Attorneys | Matter-specific retention decisions |
| Administrative Staff | Day-to-day file management |
| IT Department | Electronic records management |
| All Personnel | Policy compliance |

## Article II: Retention Periods

### Section 2.1: Client Matter Files

#### 2.1.1 Standard Retention Schedule

| Matter Type | Retention Period | From Event |
|-------------|-----------------|------------|
| General civil litigation | 10 years | Matter closed |
| Personal injury | 10 years | Settlement/judgment + minors' majority |
| Medical malpractice | 10 years | Settlement/judgment |
| Employment matters | 7 years | Matter closed |
| Criminal defense | Permanent (digital) | N/A |
| Real estate transactions | 15 years | Transaction closed |
| Estate planning | Permanent (until client death + 10 years) | N/A |
| Probate/Estate admin | 10 years | Estate closed |
| Corporate/Business | 10 years | Entity dissolution or matter closed |
| Bankruptcy | 10 years | Discharge/dismissal |
| Immigration | Permanent (digital) | N/A |
| Family law | 10 years (or until child is 21) | Matter closed |
| Tax matters | 10 years | Matter closed |
| Intellectual property | Life of IP + 10 years | N/A |

#### 2.1.2 Extended Retention Triggers

Extend standard retention when:
- Ongoing litigation or potential claims
- Client specifically requests longer retention
- Matter involves minors (until majority + period)
- Tax implications extend beyond standard period
- Regulatory requirements exceed standard period
- Original documents held for client
- Appeal rights not exhausted

#### 2.1.3 Document-Specific Retention Within Files

| Document Type | Retention | Notes |
|---------------|-----------|-------|
| Engagement letter | Full matter period | Essential |
| Correspondence | Full matter period | Core file |
| Pleadings (final) | Full matter period | Core file |
| Discovery (produced) | Full matter period | May reduce after appeal period |
| Discovery (received) | 5 years | Return to opposing counsel |
| Research memoranda | Full matter period | Work product |
| Draft documents | 2 years | Unless significant |
| Billing records | 10 years | IRS requirement |
| Conflict check records | Permanent | Ethical requirement |
| Settlement documents | Full matter period | Essential |
| Court orders | Full matter period | Essential |

## Article III: File Closing Procedures

### Section 3.1: Matter Closing Checklist

#### 3.1.1 Pre-Closing Steps

Before closing any matter:

- [ ] All substantive work completed
- [ ] Final invoice sent
- [ ] Outstanding invoices collected (or collection process initiated)
- [ ] Client notified of matter conclusion
- [ ] All trust funds disbursed
- [ ] Original documents identified
- [ ] Client property identified
- [ ] File review completed
- [ ] Retention period determined
- [ ] Electronic files organized

#### 3.1.2 File Organization for Storage

**File Contents:**
- [ ] Engagement letter
- [ ] Significant correspondence
- [ ] Key pleadings/documents
- [ ] Court orders/judgments
- [ ] Settlement documents
- [ ] Conflict check record
- [ ] Closing memorandum

**Remove Before Storage:**
- [ ] Duplicates
- [ ] Routine correspondence
- [ ] Superseded drafts
- [ ] Received discovery (unless needed)
- [ ] Research from online services
- [ ] Unused supplies

#### 3.1.3 Closing Memorandum

Document for each closed file:
- Matter summary
- Outcome
- Key documents retained
- Original documents location
- Retention period assigned
- Destruction date
- Special instructions

### Section 3.2: Client Property and Original Documents

#### 3.2.1 Client Property

Client property includes:
- Original documents provided by client
- Physical evidence
- Original wills and estate planning documents
- Stock certificates, deeds, titles
- Funds held in trust

#### 3.2.2 Return of Client Property

**Upon Matter Closing:**
1. Identify all client property
2. Notify client of property in file
3. Offer to return or provide instructions
4. Return within 30 days of instruction
5. Document return in closing memo

**If Client Does Not Respond:**
1. Send second notice (certified mail)
2. Wait 60 additional days
3. If no response, store securely
4. Document non-response
5. Apply longer retention period

#### 3.2.3 Original Documents

| Document | Action | Retention |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Original signed wills | Return to client or retain in safekeeping | Until client death + administration |
| Original trusts | Return to client or trustee | As instructed |
| Deeds/titles | Return to client | 30 days; then destroy |
| Signed contracts | Return original if requested | 10 years (copy) |
| Court-filed originals | Court retains | N/A |

### Section 3.3: Matter Reactivation

If matter reopens:
1. Retrieve file from storage
2. Review for completeness
3. Update conflict check
4. Reset retention clock upon re-closing
5. Document reactivation

## Article IV: Storage and Security

### Section 4.1: Physical Storage

#### 4.1.1 On-Site Storage

**Active Files:**
- Secured filing areas
- Organized by attorney/matter number
- Access controlled
- Fire protection

**Closed Files (Short-term):**
- Secured storage room
- Organized by destruction date
- Environmental controls
- Access logged

#### 4.1.2 Off-Site Storage

**Vendor Requirements:**
- Bonded and insured
- Confidentiality agreement
- Climate-controlled facility
- Fire suppression
- Access controls
- Disaster recovery plan
- Certificate of destruction services

**Storage Box Standards:**
- Standard records boxes
- Clearly labeled (matter name, number, destruction date)
- Box inventory maintained
- Barcode tracking preferred

### Section 4.2: Electronic Storage

#### 4.2.1 Document Management System

All electronic files stored in:
- Firm document management system
- Matter-centric organization
- Version control enabled
- Access permissions applied
- Audit logging active
- Regular backups

#### 4.2.2 Email Archiving

- All email automatically archived
- Journaling to archive system
- Matter tagging encouraged
- Searchable archive
- Legal hold capability
- Retention policies enforced

#### 4.2.3 Security Requirements

- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Access controls by matter
- Multi-factor authentication
- Audit logging
- Disaster recovery
- Intrusion detection

## Article V: File Destruction

### Section 5.1: Destruction Authorization

#### 5.1.1 Destruction Review Process

Before any destruction:

1. **Records Manager**: Identifies files reaching destruction date
2. **Supervising Attorney**: Reviews and approves destruction
3. **Practice Group Leader**: Final approval (files > $50,000 value)
4. **Records Committee**: Annual review of pending destructions
5. **Client Notice**: Not required unless agreed in engagement letter

#### 5.1.2 Destruction Holds

**Mandatory Holds:**
- Active or threatened litigation
- Regulatory investigation
- Audit notification
- Client request
- Malpractice claim or circumstances

**Hold Process:**
1. Legal hold notice issued
2. All destruction suspended for affected matters
3. IT notified to preserve electronic records
4. Hold tracked in records system
5. Hold lifted only upon written authorization

### Section 5.2: Destruction Methods

#### 5.2.1 Paper Documents

**On-Site Destruction:**
- Cross-cut shredding only
- Witnessed destruction preferred
- Destruction log maintained

**Off-Site Destruction:**
- Bonded shredding vendor
- Certificate of destruction required
- Chain of custody documented

#### 5.2.2 Electronic Records

**Deletion Standards:**
- Permanent deletion from all systems
- Backup media included
- Cloud storage cleared
- Certificate of destruction for media

**Media Destruction:**
- Hard drives: Degaussed and physically destroyed
- Optical media: Shredded
- USB drives: Wiped and destroyed
- Tapes: Degaussed and destroyed

### Section 5.3: Destruction Documentation

#### 5.3.1 Required Records

For each destruction:
- List of files destroyed
- Destruction date
- Destruction method
- Authorization signatures
- Certificate of destruction (if applicable)

## Article VI: Special Circumstances

### Section 6.1: Attorney Departure

#### 6.1.1 File Transfer

When attorney departs:
1. Inventory all active matters
2. Determine file disposition (stay/transfer)
3. Client notification and consent
4. Orderly file transfer
5. Retain copy of key documents if needed
6. Update records system

#### 6.1.2 Retention After Departure

Files remaining with firm:
- Full retention period applies
- Closed as directed by successor attorney
- Same destruction procedures

### Section 6.2: Firm Dissolution

If firm dissolves:
1. Notify all clients
2. Transfer active matters per client direction
3. Arrange storage for closed files
4. Maintain destruction schedule
5. Designate responsible party for retention period

### Section 6.3: Disaster Recovery

#### 6.3.1 Physical Records

If physical records damaged:
1. Assess damage immediately
2. Engage disaster recovery vendor
3. Attempt restoration where possible
4. Document lost records
5. Reconstruct from electronic copies
6. Notify affected clients if significant

#### 6.3.2 Electronic Records

Recovery procedures:
1. Activate backup restoration
2. Restore from most recent backup
3. Verify data integrity
4. Document any data loss
5. Notify affected clients if significant
