**Last Revised:** April 1, 2026

**Approved By:** Managing Member, Orchestrator AI LLC

---

## 1. Purpose

This Social Media and Public Communications Policy ("Policy") governs how all personnel of Orchestrator AI LLC ("the Company") engage with social media platforms, public forums, press inquiries, and any other forms of public communication that may relate to or reflect upon the Company, its products (OrchestratorAI, AI Coding Course, Divinr.ai), its clients, or the broader AI industry. The Policy ensures that public communications protect client confidentiality, accurately represent the Company's brand and values, comply with applicable laws and regulations, and support the Company's business development objectives.

Given the Company's active use of social media for business development — particularly LinkedIn outreach for client acquisition — and the presence of a sales/marketing intern engaged in these activities, this Policy provides detailed guidance for balancing effective outreach with the protection of confidential information and professional reputation.

## 3. Definitions

**Official Company Account:** A social media account or online presence that is owned, operated, or managed by the Company, including the Company's LinkedIn page, website blog, and product-specific accounts (OrchestratorAI, Divinr.ai).

**Authorized Spokesperson:** An individual who has been expressly authorized by the Managing Member to communicate publicly on behalf of the Company. By default, only the Managing Member is an authorized spokesperson.

**Client Confidential Information:** Any information relating to a client that has not been publicly disclosed by the client, including the client's identity as a customer of the Company, the nature of services provided, the client's business strategies, and any data processed through the Company's platforms.

**Proprietary Information:** Trade secrets, technical architecture, source code, algorithms, business strategies, financial information, and other non-public information belonging to the Company.

**Outreach Communication:** Proactive communications directed at potential clients, partners, or other business contacts for the purpose of business development, lead generation, or relationship building.

## 4. General Principles

### 4.1 Client Confidentiality on Social Media

(a) **Absolute Prohibition on Client Disclosure:** No Company personnel shall disclose the identity of any client, the nature of services provided to any client, or any details about client engagements on social media or in any public forum without the express written consent of the client.

(b) **No Testimonials Without Consent:** Client testimonials, case studies, or success stories shall not be published without the client's express written approval. The client shall have the opportunity to review and approve the specific content before publication.

(c) **No Implied Client References:** Communications shall not imply a client relationship through context clues (e.g., "Working with a leading Minneapolis law firm on their AI strategy" is prohibited even without naming the firm, if the firm could be reasonably identified).

(d) **Client Data in Demonstrations:** Screenshots, videos, or live demonstrations shared on social media shall use only synthetic data or pre-approved demonstration environments. No actual client data shall appear in any public content.

(e) **Engagement Work Product:** AI outputs, agent workflows, LangGraph designs, or other work product created for clients shall not be shared publicly without client consent, even in anonymized form.

### 4.2 Brand Representation

(a) When representing the Company publicly, personnel shall:
  - Be truthful, accurate, and professional
  - Not make claims about the Company's products, services, or capabilities that cannot be substantiated
  - Not make disparaging comments about competitors
  - Represent the Company's values of innovation, integrity, security, and client service
  - Use approved Company branding, logos, and product names correctly

(b) **Product References:**
  - "OrchestratorAI" refers to the AI orchestration platform (orchestratorai.io)
  - "Divinr" or "Divinr.ai" refers to the market prediction SaaS (divinr.ai)
  - "AI Coding Course" refers to the Company's educational offering
  - Product names shall be used consistently and not abbreviated in confusing ways

(c) **No Investment Advice:** Under no circumstances shall any public communication regarding Divinr.ai constitute, suggest, or imply investment advice. All public references to Divinr.ai's prediction capabilities must include appropriate disclaimers. See Section 6 for required disclaimer language.

### 4.3 Personal vs. Professional

(a) When Company personnel express opinions on their personal social media accounts on topics related to AI, technology, law, finance, or other areas relevant to the Company's business, they should include a disclaimer such as: "Views are my own and do not represent Orchestrator AI LLC."

(b) Personal social media accounts that identify the individual as affiliated with the Company are subject to this Policy to the extent that the content relates to the Company, its products, its clients, or its industry.

(c) Personnel are not required to disclose their affiliation with the Company on personal social media accounts, but if they choose to do so, they accept the obligations of this Policy regarding Company-related content.

## 5. LinkedIn Outreach Guidelines

### 5.1 General LinkedIn Conduct

(a) LinkedIn is the Company's primary platform for business development outreach, particularly for targeting boutique legal firms, marketing agencies, financial firms, and development teams.

(b) All LinkedIn outreach conducted on behalf of the Company shall be professional, respectful, and compliant with LinkedIn's Terms of Service and Professional Community Policies.

(c) Outreach messages shall be personalized and relevant to the recipient. Mass generic messaging that could be perceived as spam is prohibited.

### 5.2 Connection Requests and InMail

(a) **Personalization Required:** Connection requests to potential clients shall include a personalized note explaining the reason for connecting. Generic or blank connection requests are discouraged for business development purposes.

(b) **Honest Representation:** Outreach messages shall honestly represent the individual's role and the Company's offerings. Misleading subject lines, false urgency, or deceptive tactics are prohibited.

(c) **Respect for Boundaries:** If a recipient declines a connection request, does not respond to an InMail, or requests not to be contacted, no further outreach shall be directed to that individual for at least six (6) months.

(d) **Volume Limits:** To avoid being flagged as spam and to maintain professional credibility:
  - Connection requests: Maximum of twenty-five (25) personalized requests per day
  - InMail messages: In accordance with LinkedIn's sending limits for the account type
  - Follow-up messages: No more than two (2) follow-up messages to a non-responsive contact, spaced at least seven (7) days apart

(e) **CAN-SPAM Compliance:** While LinkedIn messages are generally not subject to CAN-SPAM, any email outreach conducted as part of the same campaign shall comply with the CAN-SPAM Act, including accurate identification, opt-out mechanisms, and physical address.

### 5.3 LinkedIn Content Posting

(a) Posts on the Company's LinkedIn page or on personal LinkedIn profiles that relate to the Company's business should be reviewed by the Managing Member before publication, unless the individual has been granted pre-approved content guidelines.

(b) Acceptable content categories:
  - Thought leadership on AI, technology, and industry trends
  - Product updates and feature announcements (approved by Managing Member)
  - Industry event participation and commentary
  - Educational content related to AI orchestration, agent frameworks, or related technologies
  - Team and company milestones (with appropriate confidentiality protections)

(c) Prohibited content:
  - Client identities or engagement details (without written consent)
  - Proprietary technical details about the platform's architecture, algorithms, or security mechanisms
  - Financial information about the Company
  - Disparaging comments about competitors, clients, or industry participants
  - Political, religious, or other divisive content posted through Company accounts

### 5.4 Intern-Specific LinkedIn Guidelines

(a) Interns conducting LinkedIn outreach on behalf of the Company (including Ethan and future marketing/sales interns) shall:
  - Clearly identify themselves by name and role (e.g., "Marketing Intern at Orchestrator AI LLC")
  - Not impersonate the Managing Member or other Company personnel
  - Use pre-approved message templates for initial outreach, customized for each recipient
  - Log all outreach activities in the designated CRM or tracking system
  - Escalate interested prospects to the Managing Member for substantive business discussions

(b) Interns shall receive specific training on:
  - This Policy's requirements
  - Client confidentiality obligations
  - Approved messaging and positioning
  - LinkedIn best practices and Terms of Service compliance
  - Handling common responses and objections professionally

(c) The Managing Member shall review intern LinkedIn activity weekly during the first month of the internship and monthly thereafter.

## 6. Required Disclaimers

### 6.1 Divinr.ai Disclaimers

All public communications referencing Divinr.ai's prediction capabilities must include one of the following disclaimers, as appropriate to the medium:

**Full Disclaimer (for articles, blog posts, press releases):**
> "Divinr.ai provides market prediction analytics powered by artificial intelligence. Predictions generated by Divinr.ai are for informational purposes only and do not constitute investment advice, financial advice, trading advice, or any other type of advice. Past predictions do not guarantee future results. Users should conduct their own research and consult with qualified financial professionals before making investment decisions. Orchestrator AI LLC is not registered as an investment adviser, broker-dealer, or financial planner."

**Short Disclaimer (for social media posts):**
> "Divinr.ai predictions are for informational purposes only, not investment advice."

**Video/Webinar Disclaimer:**
Include the full disclaimer in writing on screen at the beginning and end of any presentation, and state verbally at the beginning: "Divinr.ai provides informational predictions and does not provide investment advice."

### 6.2 General AI Disclaimers

When discussing AI capabilities publicly, communications should accurately represent current capabilities and avoid overpromising. Phrases such as "guaranteed results," "100% accurate," or "perfect predictions" are prohibited.

## 7. Public Speaking and Media Relations

### 7.1 Authorized Spokespersons

(a) The Managing Member is the default authorized spokesperson for the Company.

(b) Other personnel may be authorized to speak publicly on behalf of the Company on specific topics with the Managing Member's prior written approval.

(c) Interns are not authorized to serve as Company spokespersons without specific approval.

### 7.2 Press Inquiries

(a) All media inquiries (from journalists, bloggers, podcasters, or other media) shall be directed to the Managing Member.

(b) No Company personnel shall provide comments, interviews, or statements to media on behalf of the Company without prior authorization from the Managing Member.

(c) Background, off-the-record, and deep-background conversations with media are prohibited without prior Managing Member approval.

### 7.3 Conference Presentations and Panels

(a) Invitations to speak at conferences, webinars, or panels on topics related to the Company's business shall be reported to the Managing Member, who shall determine whether participation is appropriate.

(b) Presentation materials shall be reviewed by the Managing Member before the event.

(c) Presentations shall not disclose client confidential information or proprietary technical details without appropriate approvals.

### 7.4 Publications

(a) Articles, blog posts, white papers, or academic publications authored by Company personnel that relate to the Company's business, products, or technology shall be reviewed by the Managing Member before submission or publication.

(b) Publications shall include appropriate confidentiality protections and disclaimers.

## 8. Open Source and Developer Community Participation

(a) Participation in open-source projects and developer communities (GitHub, Stack Overflow, Discord) is encouraged when it supports the Company's technical reputation and business objectives.

(b) The following restrictions apply to developer community participation:
  - Proprietary source code shall not be shared in public repositories or forums
  - Security vulnerabilities, architectural details, and infrastructure configurations shall not be disclosed
  - Client-specific implementations shall not be discussed
  - Contributions to open-source projects during Company-paid time shall be disclosed to the Managing Member

(c) When participating in developer communities in a capacity related to the Company, personnel should identify their affiliation and be mindful that their conduct reflects on the Company.

## 9. Crisis Communications

### 9.1 Social Media During Incidents

(a) In the event of a security incident, data breach, service outage, or other crisis, all public communications shall be coordinated through the Managing Member.

(b) No Company personnel shall post about the incident on social media, respond to media inquiries, or make public statements about the incident without authorization from the Managing Member.

(c) The Managing Member shall prepare approved messaging for use during and after the incident, consistent with the Business Continuity Plan and Data Privacy and Security Policy.

### 9.2 Responding to Negative Publicity

(a) If the Company receives negative attention on social media or in the press, the Managing Member shall determine the appropriate response strategy.

(b) Personnel shall not engage in public disputes, arguments, or defensive exchanges on social media in response to criticism of the Company.

(c) Legitimate concerns raised publicly shall be acknowledged respectfully and, where appropriate, directed to private channels for resolution.

---

## 10. Monitoring and Compliance

(a) The Company may monitor public social media activity that relates to the Company, its products, or its clients, consistent with applicable law.

(b) The Company shall periodically review LinkedIn outreach activities conducted on its behalf to ensure compliance with this Policy.

(c) Personnel who become aware of social media content that violates this Policy shall report it to the Managing Member.

---

## 11. Enforcement

Violations of this Policy may result in:

**Minor violations** (e.g., failure to include a disclaimer, posting unapproved but non-harmful content):
- Verbal or written guidance
- Required removal or correction of the content
- Refresher training on this Policy

**Significant violations** (e.g., disclosure of client identity without consent, misrepresentation of Company capabilities, unauthorized media statements):
- Written warning
- Temporary suspension of social media responsibilities
- Required Managing Member approval for all future public communications

**Severe violations** (e.g., intentional disclosure of client confidential information, conduct causing reputational harm to the Company or clients):
- Immediate termination of the engagement
- Civil legal action for breach of confidentiality obligations
- Referral to applicable regulatory authorities if warranted
