# Whistleblower and Ethics Reporting Policy

**Policy Number:** OAI-ETH-004
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** April 1, 2026
**Policy Owner:** Managing Member
**Approved By:** Managing Member, Orchestrator AI LLC

---

## 1. Purpose

Orchestrator AI LLC ("the Company") is committed to conducting its business with the highest standards of integrity, ethics, and compliance with applicable laws. This Whistleblower and Ethics Reporting Policy ("Policy") establishes secure, confidential channels for reporting suspected misconduct, fraud, legal violations, and ethical concerns. The Policy provides robust protections against retaliation for good-faith reporters and ensures that all reports are taken seriously, investigated thoroughly, and resolved appropriately.

The Company recognizes that individuals within the organization are often the first to become aware of potential wrongdoing. This Policy is intended to encourage and enable personnel to raise concerns internally so that the Company can address problems promptly and prevent harm to clients, the Company, and the public.

---

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Covered Individuals

This Policy applies to and protects:

(a) The Managing Member of the Company.

(b) All independent contractors (1099) engaged by the Company.

(c) All interns.

(d) Any third-party consultants, vendors, or service providers who become aware of potential misconduct in the course of their dealings with the Company.

(e) Former contractors and interns, with respect to conduct observed during their engagement.

### 2.2 Reportable Conduct

This Policy covers reports of suspected:

(a) **Fraud:** Misappropriation of funds, falsification of financial records, billing fraud, expense report fraud, or deceptive practices directed at clients.

(b) **Legal Violations:** Violations of federal, state, or local laws, including the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act, data privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, FIPA), securities regulations, and employment/contractor classification laws.

(c) **Data Security Violations:** Unauthorized access to or disclosure of client data, failure to follow data security protocols, deliberate circumvention of security controls, and unreported data breaches.

(d) **Client Harm:** Actions that damage client interests, including misuse of client data, breach of client confidentiality, conflicts of interest, and provision of negligent or deliberately harmful AI outputs.

(e) **AI Ethics Violations:** Use of the OrchestratorAI platform or Divinr.ai in ways that violate the AI Ethics and Governance Policy, including generating harmful content, providing unauthorized investment advice through Divinr.ai, or deploying AI models in violation of responsible AI principles.

(f) **Policy Violations:** Material violations of any Company policy, including the Acceptable Use Policy, Data Privacy and Security Policy, Client Confidentiality Policy, or Conflict of Interest Policy.

(g) **Discrimination and Harassment:** Conduct violating the Anti-Discrimination and Harassment Policy, including discrimination, harassment, or retaliation based on protected characteristics.

(h) **Financial Misconduct:** Tax evasion, improper contractor classification (misclassification of employees as 1099 contractors), failure to comply with regulatory requirements, or other financial irregularities.

(i) **Safety Concerns:** Conditions or practices that pose a risk to the health or safety of any person.

## 3. Non-Retaliation Protection

### 3.1 Zero Tolerance for Retaliation

The Company maintains an absolute prohibition against retaliation directed at any individual who, in good faith:

(a) Reports suspected misconduct through any of the channels described in this Policy.

(b) Participates in or cooperates with an investigation of reported misconduct.

(c) Refuses to participate in activity that the individual reasonably believes to be illegal or in violation of Company policy.

(d) Provides information to, or cooperates with, a government agency, law enforcement authority, or regulatory body regarding suspected violations of law.

### 3.2 Prohibited Retaliatory Actions

Retaliation includes, but is not limited to:

(a) Termination or non-renewal of a contractor agreement or internship.

(b) Reduction in work assignments, compensation, or project scope.

(c) Negative or unfavorable references or performance evaluations motivated by the individual's reporting activity.

(d) Exclusion from meetings, communications, or professional opportunities.

(e) Hostile, intimidating, or threatening conduct.

(f) Any other action that would dissuade a reasonable person from making a good-faith report.

### 3.3 Retaliation Reporting

Any individual who believes they have been subjected to retaliation for making a good-faith report under this Policy should immediately report the retaliation through the same channels described in Section 4. Retaliation complaints shall be investigated with the same rigor as the underlying report.

### 3.4 Consequences of Retaliation

Any individual found to have engaged in retaliation against a good-faith reporter shall be subject to disciplinary action up to and including immediate termination of the engagement relationship. The Company may also pursue legal remedies against retaliators.

### 3.5 Legal Protections

This Policy is intended to complement, not limit, the protections available under applicable whistleblower protection laws, including:

(a) The Sarbanes-Oxley Act (to the extent applicable).

(b) The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act (to the extent applicable).

(c) Florida's Whistleblower Act (Fla. Stat. Section 448.101-448.105).

(d) Minnesota's Whistleblower Act (Minn. Stat. Section 181.931-181.935).

(e) Other applicable federal and state whistleblower protection statutes.

## 4. Reporting Channels

### 4.1 Internal Reporting

#### 4.1.1 Direct Report to Managing Member

(a) For most concerns, the preferred initial reporting channel is direct communication with the Managing Member. Reports may be made verbally (in person or by phone) or in writing (email or secure message).

(b) The Managing Member's contact information is provided to all personnel during onboarding and is maintained in the Company's emergency contact directory.

#### 4.1.2 Written Report

(a) Written reports may be submitted via email to a designated ethics reporting email address (ethics@orchestratorai.io).

(b) Written reports should include, to the extent known:
  - A description of the suspected misconduct
  - The identity of the individual(s) involved (if known)
  - The date(s) and location(s) of the suspected misconduct
  - Any witnesses
  - Any documentary evidence
  - The reporter's contact information (unless submitting anonymously per Section 4.2)

#### 4.1.3 When the Concern Involves the Managing Member

Because the Company is a single-member LLC with the Managing Member as the sole owner/operator, special provisions apply when the concern involves the Managing Member:

(a) Reports concerning the Managing Member's conduct may be directed to the Company's outside legal counsel. Contact information for outside legal counsel shall be provided to all personnel during onboarding and maintained in the Company's contractor handbook.

(b) Outside legal counsel shall serve as an independent receiver of such reports and shall engage appropriate external resources for investigation.

(c) If outside legal counsel is not available or the reporter is uncomfortable contacting counsel, the reporter may make a report directly to applicable regulatory authorities (see Section 4.3).

### 4.2 Anonymous Reporting

(a) The Company accepts anonymous reports. Anonymous reports may be submitted via:
  - An anonymous email account
  - A written letter sent to the Company's registered address without identifying the sender
  - A third-party anonymous reporting platform (if the Company implements one in the future)

(b) While anonymity is respected, reporters are encouraged to provide contact information (which will be kept confidential) to facilitate follow-up questions and investigation. Anonymous reports may be more difficult to investigate and resolve.

(c) Anonymous reports shall be investigated to the fullest extent possible given the available information.

### 4.3 External Reporting

(a) Nothing in this Policy prohibits or discourages any individual from reporting suspected legal violations directly to government agencies or law enforcement, including but not limited to:
  - The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)
  - The Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
  - The U.S. Department of Justice (DOJ)
  - The Florida Attorney General's Office
  - The Minnesota Attorney General's Office
  - State and local law enforcement
  - Relevant industry regulatory bodies

(b) The Company shall not require any individual to notify the Company before, during, or after making a report to a government agency.

(c) The Company shall not require any individual to waive whistleblower protections as a condition of any agreement, including Independent Contractor Agreements, NDAs, or settlement agreements.

## 5. Investigation Procedures

### 5.1 Receipt and Acknowledgment

(a) Upon receiving a report, the designated investigator (Managing Member or outside counsel, as applicable) shall acknowledge receipt within forty-eight (48) hours, unless the report is anonymous and no contact information was provided.

(b) The acknowledgment shall confirm that the report has been received and that an initial assessment will be conducted.

### 5.2 Initial Assessment

(a) Within five (5) business days of receiving a report, the designated investigator shall conduct an initial assessment to determine:
  - Whether the report, if true, would constitute a violation of law, regulation, or Company policy
  - The apparent severity of the alleged conduct
  - Whether immediate protective measures are needed (e.g., suspension of access, preservation of evidence)
  - The appropriate scope and methodology of investigation

(b) If the initial assessment determines that the report does not describe conduct covered by this Policy, the reporter shall be informed and, where appropriate, directed to a more suitable resolution process.

### 5.3 Investigation

(a) **Investigator Assignment:** Investigations shall be conducted by the Managing Member for most matters, or by outside legal counsel when the matter involves the Managing Member or when the Managing Member determines that an independent investigation is warranted.

(b) **Investigation Standards:** All investigations shall be:
  - Conducted promptly and thoroughly
  - Fair and impartial
  - Documented in writing
  - Conducted with appropriate confidentiality protections
  - Completed within thirty (30) business days where practicable, or within a reasonable time for complex matters

(c) **Investigation Methods:** Investigations may include, as appropriate:
  - Interviews with the reporter, the subject of the report, and witnesses
  - Review of relevant documents, records, communications, and data
  - Review of system logs, audit trails, and platform activity records
  - Engagement of external experts (forensic accountants, cybersecurity specialists, legal counsel)
  - Preservation and analysis of electronic evidence

(d) **Cooperation:** All Company personnel are expected to cooperate fully with investigations conducted under this Policy. Failure to cooperate, or providing false or misleading information during an investigation, may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of engagement.

(e) **Interim Measures:** Pending the outcome of an investigation, the Managing Member may implement interim measures to protect the reporter, preserve evidence, or prevent further potential harm. Such measures may include temporary reassignment of duties, temporary access restrictions, or enhanced monitoring.

### 5.4 Findings and Resolution

(a) Upon completion of the investigation, the investigator shall prepare a written summary of findings, including:
  - A description of the allegations
  - The investigation methodology
  - Key evidence considered
  - Factual findings
  - Whether a violation occurred
  - Recommended corrective actions

(b) If the investigation substantiates the reported misconduct, the Company shall take appropriate corrective action, which may include:
  - Termination of the offending individual's engagement
  - Implementation of additional controls or policy changes to prevent recurrence
  - Referral to law enforcement or regulatory authorities
  - Notification to affected clients (if client data or interests were involved)
  - Remediation of any harm caused by the misconduct

(c) If the investigation does not substantiate the reported misconduct, the matter shall be closed with appropriate documentation.

### 5.5 Communication with Reporter

(a) The reporter shall be informed of the outcome of the investigation to the extent permissible, while respecting the privacy of the individuals involved.

(b) At minimum, the reporter shall be informed whether the investigation was concluded and whether corrective action was taken (without disclosing the specific nature of any personnel action).

## 6. Confidentiality

(a) Reports made under this Policy shall be treated with the highest degree of confidentiality consistent with the need to conduct an adequate investigation and comply with applicable law.

(b) The identity of the reporter shall be disclosed only to those with a need to know for purposes of the investigation, unless the reporter consents to broader disclosure or disclosure is required by law.

(c) All individuals involved in an investigation (reporter, subject, witnesses, investigators) are expected to maintain confidentiality regarding the investigation to the extent possible, consistent with their rights under applicable law.

(d) Investigation records shall be maintained in a secure, access-restricted location separate from general personnel files.

---

## 7. Good Faith Requirement

(a) This Policy protects individuals who make reports in good faith — that is, reports based on a reasonable belief that the reported conduct may constitute a violation, even if the investigation ultimately determines that no violation occurred.

(b) Reports that are knowingly false, made with malicious intent, or made in reckless disregard of their truth or falsity are not protected by this Policy.

(c) An individual who makes a knowingly false report may be subject to disciplinary action.

(d) The fact that a report is not substantiated does not, by itself, indicate that the report was made in bad faith.

---

## 8. Record Keeping

(a) Records of all reports made under this Policy, investigation files, findings, and corrective actions shall be maintained for a minimum of seven (7) years.

(b) Records shall be stored securely with access restricted to the Managing Member and outside legal counsel (as applicable).

(c) Records shall be maintained in compliance with the Company's File Retention Policy and applicable legal hold requirements.

---

## 9. Training and Awareness

(a) All personnel shall be informed of this Policy during onboarding.

(b) Annual training shall include a review of this Policy, including reporting channels, non-retaliation protections, and examples of reportable conduct.

(c) This Policy shall be accessible to all personnel at all times through the Company's policy repository.

---

## 10. Policy Limitations

(a) This Policy does not create a contractual obligation or alter the at-will nature of any contractor or intern engagement (to the extent such engagement is at-will).

(b) This Policy does not guarantee a specific outcome for any report or investigation.

(c) This Policy supplements, but does not replace, any individual's right to report concerns to government agencies, seek legal counsel, or exercise rights under applicable law.
