**Last Revised:** April 1, 2026

## 1. Purpose

Orchestrator AI LLC ("the Company") is committed to providing a work environment that is free from discrimination, harassment, and retaliation. The Company values diversity and inclusion as essential to innovation, and believes that all individuals deserve to be treated with dignity and respect. This Anti-Discrimination, Harassment, and Equal Opportunity Policy ("Policy") establishes the standards of conduct expected of all personnel, defines prohibited behavior, and provides procedures for reporting, investigating, and resolving complaints.

Although the Company operates with independent contractors (1099) and interns rather than W-2 employees, the Company holds all personnel to the same high standards of professional conduct and extends the protections of this Policy to all individuals in its work environment.

---

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Covered Individuals

This Policy applies to:

(a) The Managing Member.

(b) All independent contractors (1099) engaged by the Company.

(c) All interns, including university interns (such as interns from the University of St. Thomas and other institutions).

(d) Third-party vendors, consultants, and service providers interacting with Company personnel.

(e) Clients, to the extent their conduct affects Company personnel.

### 2.2 Covered Settings

This Policy applies to all conduct that occurs:

(a) During the performance of work for the Company, regardless of location (home offices, co-working spaces, client premises, travel).

(b) At Company-sponsored events, meetings, or social functions.

(c) Through Company communication systems (email, messaging platforms, video conferencing, project management tools).

(d) On social media, when the conduct is directed at Company personnel and relates to their engagement with the Company.

(e) During interactions between Company personnel and clients, vendors, or other third parties in the course of Company business.

## 3. Definitions

**Discrimination:** Treating an individual less favorably in the terms, conditions, or privileges of their engagement with the Company because of a protected characteristic.

**Harassment:** Unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that is sufficiently severe or pervasive to create a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment, or that results in a tangible adverse action (such as termination of engagement, reduction in work assignments, or denial of opportunities).

**Sexual Harassment:** Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal, visual, or physical conduct of a sexual nature when:
  - Submission to such conduct is made, either explicitly or implicitly, a term or condition of the individual's engagement;
  - Submission to or rejection of such conduct is used as the basis for decisions affecting the individual's engagement; or
  - Such conduct has the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with the individual's work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.

**Protected Characteristics:** Race, color, religion, creed, national origin, ancestry, sex (including pregnancy, childbirth, and related medical conditions), gender, gender identity, gender expression, sexual orientation, age, physical or mental disability, medical condition, genetic information, marital status, familial status, veteran or military status, citizenship status, and any other characteristic protected by applicable federal, state, or local law.

**Retaliation:** Any adverse action taken against an individual because the individual has, in good faith, reported a potential violation of this Policy, participated in an investigation, or opposed conduct they reasonably believed to be discriminatory or harassing.

**Hostile Work Environment:** A work environment that a reasonable person would find intimidating, hostile, or abusive due to unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic. Determination is based on the totality of the circumstances, including the frequency, severity, and nature of the conduct.

**Quid Pro Quo Harassment:** A form of harassment in which submission to or rejection of unwelcome conduct is used as the basis for tangible engagement decisions.

**Microaggression:** Subtle, often unintentional, behaviors or comments that communicate derogatory or negative messages to individuals based on their membership in a marginalized group. While individual microaggressions may not constitute harassment, a pattern of microaggressions may contribute to a hostile work environment.

## 4. Policy Statements

### 4.1 Equal Opportunity

(a) The Company provides equal opportunity to all individuals regardless of protected characteristics in all aspects of the engagement relationship, including:
  - Contracting and onboarding
  - Work assignments and project allocation
  - Compensation and payment terms
  - Training and development opportunities
  - Performance evaluation
  - Termination of engagement

(b) Decisions regarding contractor and intern selection, work assignments, and continued engagement shall be based solely on qualifications, performance, business needs, and merit.

(c) The Company will make reasonable accommodations for individuals with disabilities, consistent with applicable law, to enable them to perform the essential functions of their role.

### 4.2 Prohibition of Discrimination

(a) Discrimination against any individual based on any protected characteristic is strictly prohibited.

(b) This prohibition applies to all aspects of the engagement relationship and all interactions occurring within the scope described in Section 2.2.

(c) Both intentional discrimination (disparate treatment) and practices that have an unjustified disparate impact on individuals with protected characteristics are prohibited.

### 4.3 Prohibition of Harassment

(a) Harassment based on any protected characteristic is strictly prohibited.

(b) Prohibited harassment includes, but is not limited to:

  **Verbal Harassment:**
  - Epithets, slurs, or derogatory comments related to a protected characteristic
  - Unwelcome jokes, stories, or comments related to a protected characteristic
  - Threats, intimidation, or hostile statements based on a protected characteristic
  - Persistent unwelcome romantic or sexual attention
  - Comments about an individual's body, appearance, or personal life that are unwelcome

  **Visual Harassment:**
  - Display of offensive images, cartoons, drawings, or symbols related to a protected characteristic
  - Sending or sharing offensive electronic content (emails, messages, images, memes, videos) related to a protected characteristic
  - Offensive screen savers, desktop images, or virtual backgrounds

  **Physical Harassment:**
  - Unwelcome physical contact (touching, patting, hugging, kissing, massaging)
  - Physical intimidation, blocking movement, or invading personal space
  - Assault or threatened assault

  **Online/Digital Harassment:**
  - Sending unwelcome messages through Company communication platforms, social media, or personal channels that relate to a protected characteristic
  - Cyberbullying, including spreading rumors, making derogatory posts, or engaging in coordinated online harassment
  - Unwelcome surveillance or monitoring of an individual's online activity beyond legitimate business purposes

(c) A single incident of harassment may be sufficient to constitute a violation of this Policy if the conduct is sufficiently severe.

(d) Harassing conduct need not be motivated by sexual desire to constitute sexual harassment. Conduct of a sexual nature is evaluated by its impact on the recipient, not the intent of the actor.

### 4.4 Prohibition of Retaliation

(a) Retaliation against any individual who, in good faith, reports a potential violation of this Policy, participates in an investigation, or opposes conduct they reasonably believe to be discriminatory or harassing is strictly prohibited.

(b) Retaliation includes, but is not limited to, the actions described in the Whistleblower and Ethics Reporting Policy, Section 3.2.

(c) The Company will take prompt action against any individual found to have engaged in retaliation.

### 4.5 AI-Specific Considerations

(a) The Company's AI platform shall not be used to generate content that discriminates against, harasses, or demeans any individual or group based on protected characteristics.

(b) AI model outputs shall be reviewed for discriminatory bias before being delivered to clients or used in client-facing contexts, consistent with the AI Ethics and Governance Policy.

(c) The Company's hiring, contracting, and project allocation decisions shall not be delegated to automated AI systems without human oversight to ensure non-discrimination.

## 5. Responsibilities

### 5.1 All Personnel

Every individual covered by this Policy is responsible for:

(a) Treating all individuals with dignity and respect.

(b) Refraining from discriminatory, harassing, or retaliatory conduct.

(c) Being mindful of how their words and actions may affect others, including in digital communications and remote interactions.

(d) Reporting any conduct they believe may violate this Policy, whether they are the target, a witness, or otherwise become aware of the conduct.

(e) Cooperating fully in any investigation conducted under this Policy.

### 5.2 Managing Member

The Managing Member is additionally responsible for:

(a) Modeling respectful, inclusive behavior.

(b) Ensuring that all personnel are aware of this Policy and their obligations under it.

(c) Taking prompt action when potential violations are reported or observed.

(d) Ensuring that investigations are conducted fairly and thoroughly.

(e) Implementing appropriate corrective action when violations are substantiated.

(f) Monitoring the work environment for potential issues and taking proactive steps to prevent discrimination and harassment.

## 6. Complaint Procedures

### 6.1 Reporting a Complaint

(a) Any individual who believes they have been subjected to discrimination, harassment, or retaliation, or who has witnessed such conduct, is encouraged to report the matter promptly.

(b) **Reporting Channels:**

  1. **Direct Communication:** If comfortable doing so, the individual may address the concern directly with the person engaging in the problematic conduct. This step is optional and is not a prerequisite to filing a formal complaint.

  2. **Report to Managing Member:** Reports may be made verbally (in person, by phone, or by video call) or in writing (email) to the Managing Member.

  3. **Report to Outside Legal Counsel:** If the complaint involves the Managing Member, or if the reporter is uncomfortable reporting to the Managing Member, the report may be directed to the Company's outside legal counsel. Contact information is provided during onboarding.

  4. **Ethics Reporting Channel:** Reports may be submitted to the designated ethics email (ethics@orchestratorai.io) as described in the Whistleblower and Ethics Reporting Policy.

  5. **Anonymous Reporting:** Anonymous reports are accepted, though they may be more difficult to investigate. See the Whistleblower and Ethics Reporting Policy for anonymous reporting procedures.

(c) **Timeliness:** Reports should be made as promptly as possible. While there is no fixed deadline for internal reporting, prompt reporting facilitates more effective investigation and resolution.

### 6.2 Contents of a Complaint

To facilitate investigation, complaints should include, to the extent known:

(a) The name(s) of the individual(s) engaging in the alleged conduct.

(b) A description of the conduct, including specific words, actions, or behaviors.

(c) The date(s), time(s), and location(s) (physical or digital) of the conduct.

(d) The names of any witnesses.

(e) Any documentary evidence (emails, messages, screenshots).

(f) The impact of the conduct on the reporter.

(g) Any prior attempts to address the conduct.

## 7. Investigation Process

### 7.1 Investigation Initiation

(a) Upon receiving a complaint, the Company shall initiate an investigation within five (5) business days.

(b) The Managing Member shall serve as the investigator for most complaints. If the complaint involves the Managing Member, or if the Managing Member determines that an independent investigation is warranted, outside legal counsel or an external investigator shall be engaged.

(c) **Interim Measures:** Pending the outcome of the investigation, the Company may implement interim measures to protect the complainant and ensure the integrity of the investigation, including:
  - Separation of the parties (adjusted work assignments, modified communication protocols)
  - Temporary access restrictions
  - Temporary suspension of the respondent's engagement (in cases involving alleged severe conduct)
  - Any other measures reasonably necessary to prevent further potential harm

### 7.2 Investigation Methodology

(a) **Interviews:** The investigator shall interview the complainant, the respondent, and relevant witnesses. All parties shall be given a fair opportunity to present their account of events and provide relevant evidence.

(b) **Evidence Review:** The investigator shall review all relevant evidence, including communications, documents, system logs, and any other materials pertinent to the complaint.

(c) **Standard of Review:** Findings shall be made based on a preponderance of the evidence (more likely than not).

(d) **Timeline:** Investigations shall be completed within thirty (30) business days where practicable. If additional time is needed, the complainant shall be informed of the reason for the delay and the expected completion date.

(e) **Documentation:** The investigation shall be documented in writing, including a summary of the allegations, the evidence reviewed, witness statements, findings of fact, conclusions, and recommended actions.

### 7.3 Investigation Outcomes

(a) **Substantiated Complaint:** If the investigation determines that a violation of this Policy occurred, the Company shall take prompt and appropriate corrective action, which may include:
  - Written warning
  - Required training or counseling
  - Modification of work assignments or communication protocols
  - Termination of the respondent's contractor agreement or internship
  - Report to law enforcement (in cases involving criminal conduct)
  - Implementation of systemic changes to prevent recurrence (policy updates, training, process improvements)

(b) **Unsubstantiated Complaint:** If the investigation does not substantiate the complaint, the matter shall be closed. The complainant shall be informed that the investigation was completed and that the allegations were not substantiated. The complainant shall be assured that making a good-faith complaint will not result in adverse consequences.

(c) **Inconclusive Investigation:** If the investigation is inconclusive, the Company shall document the findings, implement any appropriate preventive measures, and monitor the situation going forward.

### 7.4 Communication of Outcome

(a) Both the complainant and the respondent shall be informed of the outcome of the investigation within five (5) business days of its conclusion.

(b) The level of detail shared shall balance the complainant's interest in knowing the outcome with the respondent's privacy rights and any confidentiality obligations.

## 8. Confidentiality

(a) All complaints and investigations shall be handled with the highest degree of confidentiality consistent with a thorough investigation and compliance with applicable law.

(b) Information regarding complaints and investigations shall be shared only with those who have a need to know for purposes of the investigation or implementation of corrective action.

(c) All parties involved in a complaint or investigation are expected to maintain confidentiality to the extent possible, consistent with their rights under applicable law.

(d) Breach of investigation confidentiality may constitute a separate policy violation.

---

## 9. Third-Party Conduct

(a) If a client, vendor, or other third party engages in conduct that violates this Policy toward Company personnel, the Company shall take appropriate action to address the situation, which may include:
  - Requesting that the client or vendor address the conduct
  - Modifying the working arrangement to protect Company personnel
  - Terminating the client or vendor relationship, if necessary to protect Company personnel

(b) Company personnel are not required to tolerate discrimination or harassment from clients or other third parties as a condition of their engagement.

---

## 10. Training

(a) All personnel shall receive training on this Policy during onboarding, including:
  - Definitions of discrimination, harassment, and retaliation
  - Examples of prohibited conduct
  - Reporting procedures
  - Investigation process
  - Non-retaliation protections

(b) Annual refresher training shall be required for all personnel.

(c) Training records shall be maintained for three (3) years.

---

## 11. Record Keeping

(a) All complaint records, investigation files, findings, and corrective actions shall be maintained securely for a minimum of seven (7) years.

(b) Records shall be maintained separately from general contractor/intern files and shall be accessible only to the Managing Member and outside legal counsel (as applicable).

## 12. Legal Compliance

This Policy is intended to comply with applicable federal, state, and local anti-discrimination and anti-harassment laws, including but not limited to:

(a) Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 (to the extent applicable).

(b) The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) (to the extent applicable).

(c) The Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA) (to the extent applicable).

(d) The Florida Civil Rights Act (Fla. Stat. Chapter 760).

(e) The Minnesota Human Rights Act (Minn. Stat. Chapter 363A).

(f) Applicable local ordinances, including the Minneapolis Civil Rights Ordinance and the St. Paul Human Rights Ordinance.

Nothing in this Policy limits any individual's right to file a charge or complaint with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC), the Florida Commission on Human Relations (FCHR), the Minnesota Department of Human Rights, or any other government agency.
