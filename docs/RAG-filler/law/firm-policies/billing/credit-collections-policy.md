## 1. Purpose

This Credit Approval and Collections Policy ("Policy") establishes the standards, procedures, and timelines governing the extension of credit to clients of Orchestrator AI LLC ("the Company"), the invoicing of services, the collection of outstanding receivables, and the resolution of delinquent accounts. The Company provides AI-powered orchestration services (OrchestratorAI platform), market prediction analytics (Divinr.ai), and AI education services (AI Coding Course) to boutique legal firms, marketing agencies, financial firms, and development teams. Timely collection of receivables is essential to the Company's cash flow and continued operations as a single-member LLC.

This Policy balances the Company's need for reliable cash flow with the maintenance of strong, professional client relationships. It establishes clear expectations for payment terms, predictable escalation procedures for delinquent accounts, and defined thresholds for enforcement actions.

## 3. Definitions

**Client:** Any individual, firm, company, or entity that has entered into a service agreement, subscription agreement, or other contractual arrangement with the Company for the provision of services.

**Credit Terms:** The payment timeline extended to a client, specifying the number of days within which payment is due following the issuance of an invoice.

**Net-30:** Payment is due within thirty (30) calendar days of the invoice date.

**Invoice Date:** The date on which an invoice is issued to the client.

**Due Date:** The date by which payment is required, calculated from the Invoice Date based on the applicable Credit Terms.

**Delinquent Account:** An account with an unpaid balance that has exceeded its Due Date.

**Past Due Balance:** The total amount of unpaid invoices that have exceeded their respective Due Dates.

**Late Fee:** A charge assessed on past due balances as compensation for the cost of delayed payment.

**Write-Off:** The accounting treatment of an uncollectible receivable, removing it from accounts receivable and recognizing it as a bad debt expense.

**Small Claims Court:** A court of limited jurisdiction designed to resolve disputes involving relatively small monetary amounts, without the need for formal legal representation.

## 4. Credit Approval

### 4.1 Standard Credit Terms

(a) The Company's standard credit terms are **Net-30** — payment is due within thirty (30) calendar days of the invoice date.

(b) Standard Net-30 terms are extended to all clients upon execution of a service agreement, subscription agreement, or statement of work, unless modified per Section 4.2 or 4.3.

(c) The Managing Member may, at their discretion, offer alternative payment terms (e.g., Net-15, Net-45, Net-60) based on the client relationship, engagement size, or negotiated contract terms. Any deviation from standard terms must be documented in the applicable agreement.

### 4.2 Credit Evaluation for Large Engagements

For engagements with an expected total value exceeding Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00), the Company shall conduct a credit evaluation before extending Net-30 terms:

(a) **Evaluation Criteria:**
  - Client's business history and reputation
  - Length of the existing client relationship (if applicable)
  - Client's industry and financial stability indicators
  - References from other service providers (if available)
  - Public financial information (for publicly reporting entities)

(b) **Evaluation Outcome:**
  - If the evaluation is satisfactory, standard Net-30 terms shall be extended.
  - If the evaluation raises concerns, the Managing Member may require:
    - Advance payment (50% or 100% upfront)
    - Shortened payment terms (Net-15)
    - A deposit or retainer arrangement
    - Milestone-based billing with payment required before proceeding to the next milestone

(c) Credit evaluations are not required for:
  - Standard SaaS subscriptions paid by credit card
  - AI Coding Course enrollments paid at registration
  - Engagements with existing clients in good standing

### 4.3 Prepayment Requirements

The following services require full prepayment:

(a) **SaaS Subscriptions (Monthly/Annual):** Divinr.ai and OrchestratorAI platform subscriptions billed via automated recurring payment.

(b) **Course Enrollment:** AI Coding Course fees are due at the time of enrollment.

(c) **Hardware Procurement:** If the Company procures hardware on behalf of a client for local deployment, the hardware cost is due in advance of procurement.

### 4.4 Credit Limit

(a) Each client's outstanding credit shall not exceed the greater of:
  - The total value of the current active statement(s) of work; or
  - Twenty-five Thousand Dollars ($25,000.00)

  unless a higher limit is specifically approved in writing by the Managing Member.

(b) If a client's outstanding balance approaches or reaches the credit limit, additional work shall be paused until the balance is reduced to an acceptable level.

## 5. Invoicing Procedures

### 5.1 Invoice Issuance

(a) Invoices shall be issued promptly upon the earlier of:
  - Completion of a defined deliverable or milestone
  - The end of each monthly billing cycle (for ongoing services)
  - The agreed-upon billing schedule specified in the client's agreement

(b) For recurring SaaS subscriptions, invoices are generated automatically by the billing system.

(c) For project-based work, invoices shall be issued within five (5) business days of milestone completion.

### 5.2 Invoice Contents

Each invoice shall include:

(a) Invoice number (unique, sequential).

(b) Invoice date.

(c) Client name and billing address.

(d) Company name, address, and payment information.

(e) Description of services rendered or deliverables provided.

(f) Applicable billing period.

(g) Amount due, itemized by service category.

(h) Payment terms (e.g., "Net-30").

(i) Due date.

(j) Accepted payment methods.

(k) Late fee notice: "Invoices not paid by the due date are subject to a late fee of 1.5% per month (18% per annum) on the outstanding balance."

(l) Company's EIN/tax identification information (as required).

### 5.3 Payment Methods

The Company accepts the following payment methods:

(a) ACH bank transfer (preferred for large invoices).

(b) Wire transfer.

(c) Credit card (Visa, Mastercard, American Express) — a processing surcharge of up to 3% may apply for invoices exceeding $5,000.

(d) Check (made payable to "Orchestrator AI LLC").

(e) Online payment through the Company's invoicing platform.

### 5.4 Invoice Disputes

(a) Clients who dispute an invoice must notify the Company in writing within fifteen (15) calendar days of the invoice date, specifying the nature and amount of the dispute.

(b) Undisputed portions of an invoice remain due and payable by the original due date.

(c) The Company shall respond to invoice disputes within ten (10) business days and shall work in good faith to resolve the dispute promptly.

(d) If the dispute is resolved in the client's favor, a credit memo or adjusted invoice shall be issued within five (5) business days of resolution.

## 6. Late Fees

### 6.1 Late Fee Rate

(a) A late fee of **one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month** (equivalent to 18% per annum) shall be assessed on all past due balances.

(b) Late fees shall accrue beginning on the first day following the Due Date and shall compound monthly.

(c) Late fees shall be calculated on the outstanding principal balance of past due invoices, not on previously accrued late fees (no compound interest on late fees).

### 6.2 Late Fee Application

(a) Late fees shall be automatically calculated and applied to the client's account on the first business day of each month for all invoices past due as of the last day of the preceding month.

(b) Late fees shall appear as separate line items on subsequent invoices or statements.

### 6.3 Late Fee Waiver

(a) The Managing Member may waive late fees at their discretion for clients who:
  - Make payment within seven (7) days of the Due Date (first occurrence)
  - Demonstrate extenuating circumstances
  - Have a long-standing record of timely payment

(b) Late fee waivers must be documented and shall not be construed as a waiver of the right to assess late fees on future delinquent invoices.

## 7. Collections Escalation Timeline

### 7.1 Escalation Schedule

The following escalation timeline shall be followed for delinquent accounts:

#### Day 1 (Due Date +1): Automated Reminder

(a) An automated payment reminder is sent to the client's billing contact via email.

(b) The reminder shall include the invoice number(s), amount(s) due, and instructions for making payment.

(c) Tone: Friendly, professional. "This is a courtesy reminder that the following invoice(s) are now past due."

#### Day 7: First Follow-Up

(a) A personalized email from the Company's billing function to the client's billing contact and primary engagement contact.

(b) The email shall reference the specific invoice(s), total amount due, and accruing late fees.

(c) The email shall offer to discuss any concerns or disputes regarding the invoice(s).

(d) Tone: Professional, direct. "We want to ensure there are no issues with this invoice. Please let us know if you have questions."

#### Day 15: Second Follow-Up (Managing Member)

(a) The Managing Member shall personally contact the client's primary business contact (not just the billing contact) via email and phone.

(b) The communication shall:
  - Reference the specific past due amount, including accrued late fees
  - Inquire about any billing disputes or payment difficulties
  - Propose a payment plan if the client indicates financial hardship
  - Clearly state the consequences of continued non-payment (service suspension at Day 30)

(c) Tone: Firm but constructive. "We value our relationship and want to resolve this. Please contact me directly to discuss."

#### Day 30: Service Suspension Warning

(a) Written notice (email with delivery/read receipt, plus certified mail if the past due balance exceeds $5,000) informing the client that:
  - Total past due balance, including late fees
  - Services will be suspended within seven (7) business days unless payment is received or a payment arrangement is agreed upon
  - Upon suspension, the client's access to the OrchestratorAI platform, Divinr.ai, or other services will be disabled
  - Client data will be preserved in accordance with the File Retention Policy during the suspension period

(b) If the client's local deployment is on client-owned hardware, the suspension shall involve disabling remote support, updates, and license validation, not physical access to the client's own hardware.

(c) Tone: Formal. "Despite our previous attempts to resolve this matter, your account remains past due. We must take the following steps."

#### Day 37: Service Suspension

(a) If no payment or payment arrangement has been received by Day 37, services shall be suspended:
  - SaaS subscriptions (OrchestratorAI cloud, Divinr.ai): Account access disabled
  - Managed local deployments: Remote support and update services suspended
  - Ongoing project work: Work paused; no new deliverables until payment is received

(b) The client shall be notified in writing of the suspension and the steps required to restore service.

(c) Client data shall not be deleted during the suspension period (see Section 8).

#### Day 45: Demand Letter

(a) A formal demand letter shall be sent to the client via certified mail, return receipt requested, demanding full payment within fifteen (15) calendar days.

(b) The demand letter shall include:
  - A complete accounting of all past due invoices, late fees, and total amount owed
  - A statement of the Company's right to pursue legal remedies
  - A statement that failure to pay may result in referral to a collection agency or the initiation of legal proceedings
  - The governing law and jurisdiction for any legal action

(c) The demand letter may be prepared with the assistance of legal counsel.

#### Day 60: Final Collection Action

(a) If the account remains unpaid after the Day 45 demand letter period, the Managing Member shall determine the appropriate final collection action based on the amount owed:

  **For balances of $8,000 or less (Florida) / $15,000 or less (Minnesota):**
  - Filing in small claims court in the appropriate jurisdiction. Florida small claims limit is $8,000 (Fla. Stat. Section 34.01). Minnesota small claims limit is $15,000 (Minn. Stat. Section 491A.01).

  **For balances exceeding small claims limits:**
  - Engagement of a collection attorney to pursue formal legal action
  - Referral to a licensed collection agency
  - Filing a civil lawsuit in the appropriate court

(b) **Cost Recovery:** The Company shall seek recovery of all collection costs, attorney's fees, court costs, and interest to the extent permitted by the client's agreement and applicable law.

(c) **Bad Debt Write-Off:** If the Managing Member determines that a receivable is uncollectible (e.g., the client is bankrupt, has dissolved, or cannot be located), the receivable may be written off as bad debt. Written-off amounts may still be pursued if circumstances change.

## 8. Client Data During Delinquency

### 8.1 Data Preservation

(a) Client data shall not be deleted or destroyed during any stage of the collections process, regardless of the account's delinquent status.

(b) Client data shall be preserved for the period specified in the File Retention Policy and the applicable client agreement.

(c) During service suspension, the client shall not have access to the platform or services, but their data remains stored and protected in accordance with the Data Privacy and Security Policy.

### 8.2 Data Return Upon Termination

(a) If the engagement is ultimately terminated due to non-payment, the client may request a copy of their data.

(b) Data return requests shall be honored within thirty (30) days, subject to payment of any applicable data export fees specified in the client's agreement.

(c) After data return (or after the data return request period expires), client data shall be securely destroyed in accordance with the Data Privacy and Security Policy, unless retention is required by law or the File Retention Policy.

## 9. Payment Plans

### 9.1 Eligibility

(a) The Company may offer payment plans to clients who are unable to pay their full outstanding balance by the Due Date, at the Managing Member's discretion.

(b) Payment plans are generally available to clients who:
  - Communicate their financial difficulty proactively (before Day 30)
  - Have a prior history of timely payment
  - Demonstrate willingness and ability to make regular payments

### 9.2 Payment Plan Terms

(a) Payment plans shall be documented in a written agreement specifying:
  - Total amount owed (including accrued late fees)
  - Payment schedule (amount and frequency of payments)
  - Duration (typically not to exceed three (3) months)
  - Consequence of default (immediate acceleration of the full balance)
  - Whether late fees continue to accrue during the payment plan

(b) A minimum down payment of twenty-five percent (25%) of the total outstanding balance is typically required upon entering a payment plan.

(c) Services may be maintained at the Managing Member's discretion during an active payment plan, provided the client remains current on plan payments.

### 9.3 Payment Plan Default

(a) A payment plan is considered in default if the client misses a scheduled payment by more than five (5) business days.

(b) Upon default, the full remaining balance (including all accrued late fees) becomes immediately due and payable, and the Company shall proceed with the collections escalation at the appropriate stage.

## 11. Accounting and Record Keeping

  - The Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA), to the extent applicable
