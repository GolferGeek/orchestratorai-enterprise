# Litigation Hold Notice Template and Procedures

## Purpose and Scope

This document provides a comprehensive litigation hold template, implementation procedures, and management framework designed for technology companies and the law firms that represent them. It addresses the unique preservation challenges presented by AI platforms, SaaS applications, cloud/local hybrid deployments, continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD) pipelines, and the diverse data types generated by modern technology operations.

A litigation hold is not a suggestion. It is a legal obligation that, when breached, can result in adverse inference instructions, monetary sanctions, issue preclusion, default judgment, or criminal contempt. For technology companies, where data is the product and the evidence, the failure to implement and maintain a proper litigation hold is among the most consequential litigation errors possible.

## 2. Litigation Hold Notice Templates

### 2.1 Template A: General Litigation Hold Notice (Employee/Custodian)

---

**PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL**
**ATTORNEY-CLIENT COMMUNICATION / ATTORNEY WORK PRODUCT**

**LITIGATION HOLD NOTICE**

**Date:** [Date]

**To:** [Custodian Name]

**From:** [Legal Department / Outside Counsel Name]

**Re:** Litigation Hold — [Matter Name / Brief Description]

**Hold Number:** [LH-YYYY-NNN]

---

**THIS IS A LEGAL OBLIGATION. PLEASE READ THIS NOTICE CAREFULLY AND COMPLY IMMEDIATELY.**

**1. Why You Are Receiving This Notice**

[Company Name] is involved in [a pending / anticipated] legal matter: [brief description sufficient for the custodian to understand the scope without disclosing privileged strategy — e.g., "a dispute with [Opposing Party] regarding allegations that certain proprietary technology was used without authorization" or "a regulatory investigation into data handling practices related to [product/service]"].

As a result, we are legally required to preserve all documents, data, and information that may be relevant to this matter. You have been identified as a person who may possess, control, or have access to relevant information.

**2. What You Must Do**

Effective immediately, you must:

**PRESERVE** all documents, data, communications, and information described below that are in your possession, custody, or control, whether stored on company systems, personal devices, cloud accounts, or any other location.

**DO NOT** delete, destroy, discard, modify, overwrite, or alter any potentially relevant information.

**DO NOT** move, transfer, or reorganize files or data in a way that changes metadata or obscures the original location or context.

**SUSPEND** any routine or automatic deletion of data, including emptying trash/recycle bins, deleting email, purging chat histories, or running cleanup scripts.

**3. What Must Be Preserved**

You must preserve all documents and information relating to [description of subject matter — tailored to the specific matter], including but not limited to:

**Communications:**
- [ ] Email messages (sent, received, drafts) related to [subject matter]
- [ ] Text messages (SMS, iMessage, WhatsApp) related to [subject matter]
- [ ] Chat messages (Slack, Microsoft Teams, Discord) related to [subject matter]
- [ ] Video conferencing recordings and chat logs (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet) related to [subject matter]
- [ ] Voicemail messages related to [subject matter]

**Documents and Files:**
- [ ] Word processing documents, spreadsheets, presentations, and PDFs
- [ ] Notes (handwritten, digital, Apple Notes, Notion, Evernote)
- [ ] Calendar entries and meeting invitations
- [ ] Project management records (Jira tickets, Asana tasks, Linear issues, GitHub issues)
- [ ] Contracts, agreements, proposals, and invoices
- [ ] Technical specifications, design documents, and architecture diagrams

**Technical Data:**
- [ ] Source code (all branches, tags, and commit history in Git or other version control)
- [ ] Code reviews (pull requests, merge requests, review comments)
- [ ] Build and deployment records (CI/CD pipeline logs, deployment manifests)
- [ ] Configuration files (Docker, Kubernetes, infrastructure-as-code)
- [ ] Database records, schemas, and migration history
- [ ] API logs, access logs, and audit trails
- [ ] System monitoring data, alerts, and incident reports

**AI and Machine Learning Data (if applicable):**
- [ ] Training data, validation data, and test data
- [ ] Model weights, architectures, and hyperparameter configurations
- [ ] Experiment tracking records (MLflow, Weights & Biases, Neptune)
- [ ] Model evaluation results and performance metrics
- [ ] Inference logs (inputs, outputs, and metadata)
- [ ] Prompt templates, system prompts, and conversation logs
- [ ] Fine-tuning data and transfer learning records

**Financial and Business Data:**
- [ ] Financial records related to [subject matter] (invoices, payments, revenue reports)
- [ ] Customer records and communications related to [subject matter]
- [ ] Marketing materials and sales presentations related to [subject matter]
- [ ] Business plans, strategy documents, and board materials related to [subject matter]

**4. Scope of Preservation**

- **Time period:** [Start date] through the present (and ongoing until you receive written notice that the hold has been released)
- **Locations:** All company systems, personal devices used for company business, cloud storage accounts (Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, iCloud), personal email accounts used for company business, and any other location where relevant information may be stored
- **Format:** Preserve information in its original format. Do not convert, print, or reformat electronic documents

**5. Your Obligations**

- This hold remains in effect until you receive written notice from [Legal Department / Outside Counsel] that it has been released. Do not assume the hold has been lifted because the matter appears to be resolved.
- If you become aware of any additional relevant information or data sources, notify [Legal Contact] immediately.
- If you receive a request to delete, modify, or move any potentially relevant information, notify [Legal Contact] before taking any action.
- If you leave the company while this hold is in effect, you must return all company information and notify [Legal Contact] of any relevant information on personal devices.

**6. Consequences of Non-Compliance**

Failure to comply with this litigation hold may result in:
- Sanctions imposed by the court against the company and/or individuals
- Adverse inferences drawn against the company (the court may instruct the jury to assume the destroyed information was unfavorable)
- Personal liability for individuals who willfully destroy evidence
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment

**7. Acknowledgment**

Please sign and return this acknowledgment within [48 hours / 5 business days] of receipt.

I, [Custodian Name], acknowledge that I have received and read this Litigation Hold Notice. I understand my obligations to preserve all potentially relevant documents and information as described above. I will comply with these obligations immediately and will contact [Legal Contact] with any questions.

Signature: _________________________

Date: _________________________

If you have any questions about this notice or your obligations, contact [Legal Contact Name] at [email/phone] immediately.

### 2.2 Template B: IT/DevOps Litigation Hold Notice (System-Level Preservation)

---

**PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL**
**ATTORNEY-CLIENT COMMUNICATION / ATTORNEY WORK PRODUCT**

**LITIGATION HOLD NOTICE — IT/DEVOPS SYSTEMS PRESERVATION**

**Date:** [Date]

**To:** [IT Director / DevOps Lead / System Administrator Name]

**From:** [Legal Department / Outside Counsel Name]

**Re:** System-Level Preservation — [Matter Name / Brief Description]

**Hold Number:** [LH-YYYY-NNN]

---

**THIS NOTICE REQUIRES IMMEDIATE ACTION TO PRESERVE SYSTEM-LEVEL DATA.**

**1. Background**

[Company Name] has a legal obligation to preserve electronically stored information (ESI) related to [brief matter description]. This notice supplements the individual custodian hold notices and addresses system-level preservation that must be implemented by IT/DevOps.

**2. Immediate Actions Required**

The following system-level preservation measures must be implemented within [24/48] hours of receipt of this notice:

**Email and Communication Systems:**
- [ ] Place legal holds on the following email accounts/mailboxes: [list]
- [ ] Place legal holds on the following Slack channels and DMs: [list or "all channels and DMs for users: (list)"]
- [ ] Disable automatic deletion policies for the identified accounts and channels
- [ ] Preserve all email and Slack data from [start date] to present
- [ ] Export and archive affected data as a backup

**Source Code Repositories:**
- [ ] Create complete mirror clones of the following repositories (including all branches, tags, and complete commit history):
  - [Repository 1]
  - [Repository 2]
  - [etc.]
- [ ] Calculate and record SHA-256 hash values of each cloned repository
- [ ] Store clones in a read-only, access-controlled location: [specify location]
- [ ] Disable force-push, history rewriting, and branch deletion on the production repositories for the relevant branches
- [ ] Preserve all CI/CD pipeline logs for these repositories from [start date] to present

**Databases:**
- [ ] Create full database dumps of the following databases:
  - [Database 1 — e.g., "PostgreSQL/Supabase production database on port 6011"]
  - [Database 2]
  - [etc.]
- [ ] Record the backup timestamp, database version, and the command used
- [ ] Calculate and record SHA-256 hash values of all backup files
- [ ] Store backups in a secure, read-only location
- [ ] Preserve all database audit logs from [start date] to present
- [ ] Disable any automated data purging or archiving processes for the relevant tables/schemas

**AI and Machine Learning Systems (if applicable):**
- [ ] Snapshot and preserve the following model versions:
  - [Model 1 — version identifier, registry location]
  - [Model 2]
  - [etc.]
- [ ] Preserve all training data used for these model versions
- [ ] Preserve all experiment tracking data (MLflow, W&B, etc.) from [start date] to present
- [ ] Preserve all inference logs from [start date] to present
- [ ] Disable any automated model version cleanup or training data purging

**Server and Application Logs:**
- [ ] Extend log retention for the following systems to indefinite (until hold release):
  - [System 1 — e.g., "Application server access logs"]
  - [System 2 — e.g., "API gateway request/response logs"]
  - [System 3 — e.g., "Authentication and authorization logs"]
  - [etc.]
- [ ] Archive existing logs from [start date] to present in a secure location
- [ ] Disable log rotation that would overwrite relevant log files

**Cloud Infrastructure:**
- [ ] Enable and preserve cloud audit logs (CloudTrail, Activity Log, Audit Logs) for the relevant period
- [ ] Preserve IAM records, access policies, and permission change history
- [ ] Preserve relevant cloud storage objects (S3 buckets, Azure Blob, GCS)
- [ ] Snapshot relevant cloud instances and volumes
- [ ] Preserve DNS records and configuration history

**Backup Systems:**
- [ ] Identify and preserve all backup media (tapes, snapshots, cloud backups) from [start date] to present
- [ ] Suspend any backup rotation or overwriting schedules that would destroy relevant backups
- [ ] Document the backup inventory with dates, contents, and storage locations

**Client/On-Premises Deployments (if applicable):**
- [ ] Coordinate with [client name / deployment team] to preserve the following on-premises data:
  - Deployment configurations and version information
  - Local database backups
  - Local log files
  - Any locally stored model data or inference results

**3. Documentation Requirements**

For each preservation action taken, document:
- What was preserved (specific system, data type, scope)
- When the preservation action was taken (date and time)
- Who performed the action (name and role)
- How the preservation was accomplished (method, tool, command)
- Where the preserved data is stored (location, access controls)
- Verification performed (hash values, completeness checks)

**4. Ongoing Obligations**

- Continue preserving new data in the identified categories as it is generated
- Do not implement any system changes that would affect the preserved data without prior approval from [Legal Contact]
- Report any data loss, system failures, or preservation challenges to [Legal Contact] immediately
- Provide periodic status updates on preservation compliance to [Legal Contact] on a [weekly/monthly] basis

**5. Acknowledgment**

I, [IT/DevOps Lead Name], acknowledge that I have received and read this IT/DevOps Litigation Hold Notice. I understand the system-level preservation actions required and will implement them within the specified timeframe. I will document all preservation actions and report any issues to [Legal Contact].

Signature: _________________________

Date: _________________________

Contact [Legal Contact Name] at [email/phone] with any questions or if any preservation action cannot be completed as specified.

## 3. Implementation Procedures

### 3.1 Initial Hold Implementation (First 48 Hours)

**Hour 0-4: Assessment and Planning**
1. Identify the trigger event and assess the scope of the preservation obligation
2. Identify all potentially relevant custodians (individuals and departments)
3. Identify all potentially relevant data sources and systems
4. Draft the litigation hold notice (adapt from templates above)
5. Assign a hold coordinator responsible for implementation and tracking

**Hour 4-24: Notice Distribution and System Holds**
6. Issue individual custodian hold notices (Template A)
7. Issue IT/DevOps system preservation notice (Template B)
8. Suspend relevant automated deletion processes
9. Begin collecting acknowledgments from custodians
10. Begin implementing system-level preservation measures

**Hour 24-48: Verification and Documentation**
11. Verify that all custodians have received and acknowledged the hold notice
12. Verify that system-level preservation measures have been implemented
13. Document all preservation actions taken
14. Identify any gaps or challenges and develop remediation plans
15. Report to counsel on the status of implementation

### 3.2 Custodian Identification

Identify custodians by role and involvement:

**Primary custodians** (directly involved in the events at issue):
- Individuals named in the complaint or anticipated claims
- Individuals who worked directly on the technology at issue
- Individuals who communicated with the opposing party
- Individuals who made decisions related to the dispute

**Secondary custodians** (may possess relevant information):
- Managers and supervisors of primary custodians
- IT administrators with access to relevant systems
- HR personnel (for employment-related disputes)
- Finance personnel (for damages-related information)
- Customer support personnel (for client-related disputes)

**System custodians** (responsible for system-level data):
- IT/DevOps team leads
- Database administrators
- Security operations personnel
- Cloud infrastructure administrators
- AI/ML operations engineers

### 3.3 Data Source Mapping

Create a comprehensive map of all data sources that may contain relevant information:

| Data Source | Custodian/System Owner | Data Type | Retention Policy | Preservation Action |
|-------------|----------------------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Gmail / Exchange | Individual users | Email, calendar | Auto-delete after 2 years | Place legal hold |
| Slack | Engineering team | Messages, files | 90-day retention | Disable retention policy, export |
| GitHub | DevOps team | Source code, PRs, issues | Indefinite | Mirror clone, disable force-push |
| Supabase (PostgreSQL) | DBA | Application data, audit logs | Varies by table | Full backup, suspend purging |
| MLflow | ML team | Experiment tracking | 1-year retention | Extend retention, snapshot |
| AWS CloudTrail | Cloud team | API audit logs | 90-day retention | Extend to indefinite, archive |
| Jira | Product team | Tickets, comments, attachments | Indefinite | Export relevant projects |
| Google Drive | All employees | Documents, spreadsheets | Auto-delete trash after 30 days | Disable auto-delete |
| Zoom | All employees | Meeting recordings | 30-day retention | Disable auto-delete, download |
| Application logs | DevOps | Access logs, error logs | 30-day rotation | Extend rotation, archive |

### 3.4 Special Preservation Challenges for Technology Companies

**Continuous deployment environments:**
Modern software companies deploy code dozens or hundreds of times per day. Each deployment may overwrite the previous version. Preservation in this environment requires:
- Maintaining a complete record of all deployments (version identifiers, deployment timestamps, deployer identity)
- Preserving specific versions of deployed code (tag the relevant versions in the repository and ensure they cannot be deleted)
- Preserving deployment configurations (Docker images, Kubernetes manifests, environment variables) for each relevant deployment

**AI model versioning:**
AI models are retrained and updated regularly. Each training run may produce a different model. Preservation requires:
- Identifying and preserving the specific model versions relevant to the dispute
- Preserving the complete training pipeline: data, preprocessing code, training code, hyperparameters, evaluation metrics
- Preserving the inference environment: the model, its dependencies, and the application code that calls it
- Freezing model training and deployment for the relevant models until counsel authorizes changes

**Ephemeral infrastructure:**
Cloud infrastructure (containers, serverless functions, auto-scaling instances) is designed to be created and destroyed dynamically. Preservation requires:
- Preserving infrastructure definitions (Terraform, CloudFormation, Kubernetes manifests) rather than running instances
- Preserving cloud provider audit logs (CloudTrail, Activity Log) that record the creation and destruction of resources
- Preserving container images in a registry with deletion disabled

**Multi-tenant environments:**
SaaS platforms serve multiple customers from shared infrastructure. Preserving data for one customer without affecting others requires:
- Tenant-specific data extraction and preservation
- Careful scoping to avoid preserving (and potentially disclosing) other customers' data
- Documentation of the extraction methodology to demonstrate completeness without overreach

## 4. Hold Monitoring and Maintenance

### 4.1 Periodic Reminders

Issue reminder notices to all custodians at regular intervals:
- **Quarterly reminders** for long-running holds
- **Event-triggered reminders** when significant case developments occur (new claims, new parties, scope expansion)
- **Reminders upon custodian changes** (new employees joining the team, employees leaving the company, role changes)

### 4.2 Custodian Departure Protocol

When an employee subject to a litigation hold departs the company:

1. **Before departure:**
   - Remind the departing employee of the hold obligations
   - Collect all company devices and ensure forensic imaging before decommissioning
   - Collect access credentials for all company systems
   - Determine whether the employee has relevant information on personal devices
   - If so, arrange for imaging or review of personal devices (with the employee's consent or pursuant to employment agreement provisions)

2. **After departure:**
   - Preserve the employee's email account, Slack messages, and other communications
   - Preserve the employee's home directory, cloud storage, and project files
   - Ensure the employee's system access is revoked but the data is preserved
   - Update the hold log to reflect the custodian's departure and preservation actions taken
   - If the employee joins a competitor or adverse party, notify counsel immediately

### 4.3 Hold Modification

As the case develops, the hold scope may need to be expanded or narrowed:

**Expansion triggers:**
- New claims or theories are asserted
- New parties are added
- Discovery reveals additional relevant custodians or data sources
- The relevant time period is expanded

**Narrowing triggers:**
- Claims are dismissed
- Parties are dismissed
- The court narrows the scope of discovery
- Counsel determines that specific data sources are no longer relevant

**Document all modifications** to the hold scope, including the date, the basis for the modification, the specific changes made, and who authorized the modification.

### 4.4 Compliance Tracking

Maintain a hold tracking log:

## 5. Hold Release Procedures

### 5.1 When to Release

The litigation hold may be released only when:
- The litigation has been fully resolved, including all appeals and post-judgment proceedings
- A final settlement has been executed and all obligations fulfilled
- Counsel has affirmatively determined that no further claims or proceedings are anticipated
- The specific data or custodian is no longer relevant to any active hold

### 5.2 Release Process

1. **Counsel authorization:** Obtain written authorization from counsel (internal or external) to release the hold
2. **Release notice:** Issue a written release notice to all custodians and to IT/DevOps
3. **Restore normal retention:** Re-enable automated deletion processes that were suspended
4. **Document the release:** Record the release date, the authorizing counsel, and the basis for release
5. **Update the hold tracking log**
6. **Do not destroy immediately:** Allow a reasonable buffer period after the release before resuming routine deletion, in case the hold needs to be reinstated

### 5.3 Release Notice Template

---

**PRIVILEGED AND CONFIDENTIAL**

**LITIGATION HOLD RELEASE NOTICE**

**Date:** [Date]

**To:** [Custodian Name / IT/DevOps]

**From:** [Legal Department / Outside Counsel Name]

**Re:** Release of Litigation Hold — [Matter Name] (Hold Number: [LH-YYYY-NNN])

---

The litigation hold issued on [original hold date] regarding [matter description] is hereby **RELEASED** effective [date].

You are no longer required to preserve documents and information specifically for this matter. Normal document retention and destruction policies may resume for the data that was subject to this hold, subject to any other active litigation holds that may apply.

**Important:** If you are subject to any other active litigation holds, continue to comply with those holds. This release applies only to the hold identified above.

If you have questions, contact [Legal Contact Name] at [email/phone].

## 6. Litigation Hold Audit and Compliance Verification

### 6.1 Periodic Audits

Conduct periodic audits to verify hold compliance:

- [ ] All custodians have received and acknowledged the current hold notice
- [ ] All system-level preservation measures remain in effect
- [ ] No automated deletion processes have been inadvertently re-enabled
- [ ] New custodians (new hires, role changes) have been identified and added to the hold
- [ ] Departing custodians have been processed according to the departure protocol
- [ ] The hold tracking log is current and complete
- [ ] No relevant data has been lost, destroyed, or altered

### 6.2 Documentation for Litigation

Maintain a comprehensive hold file for each matter that includes:
- The hold notice (all versions, if modified)
- The list of custodians and their acknowledgments
- The IT/DevOps preservation notice and compliance documentation
- All reminder notices
- All modifications to the hold scope
- Custodian departure records
- Audit results
- The release notice (when issued)

This documentation will be critical if the opposing party alleges spoliation or if the court inquires about preservation efforts. The hold file should demonstrate that the company took reasonable steps to preserve relevant information, identified and notified all relevant custodians, implemented system-level preservation measures, monitored compliance, and addressed any issues promptly.

## 7. Litigation Hold Checklist

**Initiation:**
- [ ] Trigger event identified and documented
- [ ] Scope of preservation obligation assessed
- [ ] Custodians identified (primary, secondary, system)
- [ ] Data sources mapped
- [ ] Hold notice drafted (adapted from templates)
- [ ] Hold coordinator assigned
- [ ] Hold tracking number assigned

**Implementation (First 48 Hours):**
- [ ] Individual custodian notices issued
- [ ] IT/DevOps system preservation notice issued
- [ ] Automated deletion processes suspended
- [ ] Custodian acknowledgments collected
- [ ] System-level preservation measures implemented
- [ ] Preservation actions documented
- [ ] Gaps identified and addressed

**Ongoing Maintenance:**
- [ ] Quarterly (or periodic) reminders issued
- [ ] New custodians identified and notified as they arise
- [ ] Departing custodians processed per protocol
- [ ] Hold scope updated as the case develops
- [ ] Compliance audits conducted
- [ ] Hold tracking log maintained

**Release:**
- [ ] Counsel authorization obtained
- [ ] Release notice issued to all custodians and IT/DevOps
- [ ] Normal retention policies restored
- [ ] Release documented in hold tracking log
- [ ] Hold file archived for future reference
