**Last Revised:** November 22, 2025
**Version:** 1.5

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for internal promotions and transfers at Acme Corporation. We are committed to developing our employees and providing growth opportunities while ensuring fair, transparent processes.

---

## 2. Guiding Principles

- **Internal First:** We prioritize internal candidates for open positions
- **Meritocracy:** Decisions based on skills, performance, and potential
- **Transparency:** Clear criteria and processes
- **Development:** Support employees' career growth
- **Fairness:** Consistent application across the organization

---

## 3. Promotions

### 3.1 Definition

A promotion is advancement to a higher-level position with:
- Increased responsibilities
- Higher job grade/level
- Salary increase
- Typically a new title

### 3.2 Types of Promotions

**Title Promotion (In-Band):**
- Movement within the same job level
- Example: Engineer II to Senior Engineer (same level)
- Recognition of growth and contribution
- Typically 3-5% salary increase

**Level Promotion (Advancement):**
- Movement to a higher job level
- Example: IC3 to IC4, or M1 to M2
- Significant increase in scope and responsibility
- Typically 8-15% salary increase

**Management Promotion:**
- Transition from individual contributor to manager
- Requires leadership competencies
- Additional management training required

### 3.3 Eligibility Criteria

To be considered for promotion, employees should:

**Performance:**
- At least 12 months in current role
- Performance rating of "Meets Expectations" or higher
- Last two reviews: At least one "Exceeds Expectations"
- No active performance improvement plans

**Competencies:**
- Demonstrates competencies of next level
- Consistently performs at higher level
- Shows potential for continued growth

**Behavior:**
- Exemplifies company values
- Strong collaboration and teamwork
- No significant disciplinary issues

### 3.4 Promotion Process

**Step 1: Manager Nomination**
- Manager identifies candidate
- Documents readiness with evidence
- Discusses with skip-level leader

**Step 2: Assessment**
- HR reviews eligibility
- Calibration with peers at same level
- 360 feedback (for management promotions)

**Step 3: Approval**

| Promotion Type | Approvers |
|---------------|-----------|
| Title (In-Band) | Manager + Director |
| Level (IC4 and below) | Director + VP + HR |
| Level (IC5+) | VP + SVP + HR |
| Manager (M1) | VP + HR |
| Senior Manager (M2+) | SVP + CHRO |
| Director+ | C-Suite |

**Step 4: Communication**
- Manager communicates to employee
- HR processes changes in Workday
- Announcement to team (per employee preference)

**Step 5: Onboarding to New Role**
- Updated expectations and goals
- Development plan for new level
- Manager support for transition

### 3.5 Promotion Timing

**Regular Promotion Cycles:**
- Q1 (aligned with annual review cycle)
- Q3 (mid-year, for exceptional cases)

**Off-Cycle Promotions:**
- For urgent business needs
- Retention situations
- Requires VP + HR approval

### 3.6 Compensation Changes

**Salary Increase:**

| Promotion Type | Typical Increase |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Title (In-Band) | 3% - 5% |
| One Level Up | 8% - 12% |
| Two Levels Up (rare) | 12% - 18% |
| Into Management | 10% - 15% |

**Placement in New Range:**
- Typically at or near minimum of new range
- May be higher based on experience and market data
- Must not exceed range maximum

**Effective Date:**
- Regular cycle: March 1 or September 1
- Off-cycle: First of month following approval

### 3.7 Unsuccessful Promotion Requests

If a promotion request is not approved:
- Manager provides clear feedback
- Creates development plan
- Identifies specific gaps
- Reviews again at next cycle

## 4. Internal Transfers

### 4.1 Definition

A transfer is movement to a different role that may be:
- **Lateral:** Same level, different team/function
- **Promotional:** Higher level (follows promotion process)
- **Developmental:** Temporary assignment for growth

### 4.2 Types of Transfers

**Lateral Transfer:**
- Same job level and compensation
- Different team, department, or function
- Provides new experiences and skills

**Promotional Transfer:**
- Movement to higher-level role in different area
- Combines transfer and promotion processes

**Temporary Assignment/Rotation:**
- 3-12 month assignment in different area
- Returns to original role afterward
- For development purposes

**Relocation Transfer:**
- Same or similar role in different location
- May involve relocation assistance

### 4.3 Eligibility

**Minimum Requirements:**
- At least 12 months in current role (6 months for exceptional cases)
- Performance rating of "Meets Expectations" or higher
- No active performance issues
- Manager awareness (see notification process)

**Exceptions:**
- Organizational restructuring
- Position elimination
- Approved by HR and VP

### 4.4 Finding Internal Opportunities

**Internal Job Postings:**
- All open positions posted on careers.acmecorp.com/internal
- Employees see postings before external candidates (5-day head start)
- Apply through Workday

**Networking:**
- Connect with hiring managers
- Discuss interests with your manager
- Leverage your HR Business Partner

**Career Conversations:**
- Discuss transfer interests with your manager
- Include in development plans
- Identify target roles and preparation needed

### 4.5 Transfer Process

**Step 1: Express Interest**
- Apply through internal job posting, or
- Discuss with hiring manager, or
- Work with HR to identify opportunities

**Step 2: Notify Current Manager**
- Required before formal interview process
- Ideally discuss transfer interests in advance
- Manager cannot block transfers, but can request reasonable transition time

**Step 3: Interview Process**
- Similar to external hiring process
- May be abbreviated for internal candidates
- Internal candidates receive priority consideration

**Step 4: Decision**
- Hiring manager makes selection
- HR reviews and approves
- Offer extended through HR

**Step 5: Transition Planning**
- Current and new managers agree on timing
- Standard transition: 2-4 weeks
- May be longer for critical roles (max 6 weeks)
- Knowledge transfer and handoff

**Step 6: Transfer Completion**
- HR processes in Workday
- New role begins
- Support from both teams during transition

### 4.6 Compensation for Transfers

**Lateral Transfers:**
- Generally no salary change
- Geographic differential may apply if relocating
- Sign-on bonus not typically provided

**Promotional Transfers:**
- Follow promotion compensation guidelines
- 8-15% increase typical

**Transfers to Lower Level:**
- May occur for personal reasons or career change
- Salary adjusted to new level range
- Typically 5-15% reduction

### 4.7 Manager's Role

**Current Manager:**
- Cannot unreasonably block transfers
- Provide honest assessment of employee
- Support employee's career goals
- Plan for transition

**New Manager:**
- Evaluate candidate fairly
- Provide clear job expectations
- Support onboarding to new role

## 5. Temporary Assignments and Rotations

### 5.1 Purpose

Temporary assignments provide:
- Exposure to different parts of the business
- Skill development opportunities
- Cross-functional experience
- Leadership development

### 5.2 Types

**Job Rotation:**
- Planned rotation through multiple roles
- Typically 6-12 months per rotation
- Part of leadership development programs

**Temporary Assignment:**
- Fill a temporary need in another area
- 3-6 months typical duration
- Returns to original role

**Project Assignment:**
- Work on cross-functional project
- May be part-time while maintaining current role
- Defined project duration

### 5.3 Eligibility

- 18+ months at Acme
- Strong performance record
- Manager and department approval
- Skills match assignment needs

### 5.4 Compensation

- Base salary remains unchanged
- Eligible for acting pay if significantly higher-level work
- Expenses covered if relocation required

### 5.5 Return from Assignment

- Guaranteed return to original role or equivalent
- May result in permanent transfer by mutual agreement
- Performance reviewed with input from both managers

---

## 6. International Transfers

### 6.1 Overview

Transfers to international locations require additional planning for:
- Immigration/visa requirements
- Tax implications
- Compensation localization
- Benefits and retirement
- Family considerations

### 6.2 Process

1. Discuss interest with manager and HR
2. HR assesses feasibility
3. Immigration review
4. Compensation and benefits analysis
5. Offer and relocation planning
6. Transfer execution

### 6.3 Support Provided

- Immigration assistance
- Relocation package
- Tax consultation
- Cultural training
- Family support services

Contact HR for international transfer inquiries.

---

## 7. Special Situations

### 7.1 Organizational Changes

During reorganizations:
- Employees may be placed in new roles
- Normal eligibility requirements may be waived
- Compensation protection may apply

### 7.2 Return from Leave

Employees returning from leave are entitled to:
- Same or equivalent position
- Same pay and benefits
- Reasonable accommodation if needed

### 7.3 Rehires

Former employees who return:
- Prior tenure may be considered for eligibility
- Not guaranteed previous level or salary
- Subject to normal hiring and promotion processes

## 8. Roles and Responsibilities

### Employees

- Own your career development
- Communicate interests to your manager
- Apply for opportunities that match your skills
- Prepare for next-level roles
- Support smooth transitions

### Current Managers

- Discuss career goals with employees
- Support development and growth
- Provide honest assessments
- Enable reasonable transitions
- Plan for succession

### Hiring Managers

- Consider internal candidates fairly
- Evaluate based on role requirements
- Communicate decisions promptly
- Support successful onboarding

### HR

- Maintain fair and consistent processes
- Facilitate internal mobility
- Review and approve compensation changes
- Provide career resources and guidance
