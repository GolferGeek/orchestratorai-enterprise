## 1. Policy Statement

Acme Corporation is committed to providing equal employment opportunities to qualified individuals with disabilities. We comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and applicable state laws. We provide reasonable accommodations to enable qualified individuals to perform essential job functions, unless doing so would cause undue hardship.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All applicants
- All employees
- All terms, conditions, and privileges of employment

It covers:
- Hiring and onboarding
- Job assignments
- Training
- Promotions
- Compensation
- Benefits
- Termination

## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 Disability

Under the ADA, a disability is:
- A physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities
- A record of such an impairment
- Being regarded as having such an impairment

**Major Life Activities Include:**
- Caring for oneself
- Performing manual tasks
- Seeing, hearing, eating, sleeping
- Walking, standing, sitting, reaching
- Lifting, bending
- Speaking, breathing
- Learning, reading, concentrating
- Thinking, communicating
- Working
- Major bodily functions

### 3.2 Qualified Individual

A qualified individual is someone who:
- Meets the skill, experience, education, and other job-related requirements
- Can perform the essential functions of the job with or without reasonable accommodation

### 3.3 Essential Functions

Essential functions are the fundamental duties of a position. A function may be essential if:
- The position exists to perform that function
- Limited employees are available to perform it
- It requires specialized skills
- Significant time is spent performing it

### 3.4 Reasonable Accommodation

A reasonable accommodation is any modification or adjustment that enables a qualified individual with a disability to:
- Apply for a job
- Perform essential job functions
- Enjoy equal benefits and privileges of employment

### 3.5 Undue Hardship

An undue hardship is an accommodation that would:
- Be unduly costly
- Be disruptive to operations
- Fundamentally alter the nature of the business
- Create a direct threat to safety

## 5. Interactive Process

### 5.1 Overview

The interactive process is a collaborative dialogue between the employee and employer to identify effective accommodations. It is:
- Two-way communication
- Flexible and ongoing
- Good faith effort by both parties
- Focused on finding solutions

### 5.2 Steps in the Interactive Process

**Step 1: Request**
Employee requests accommodation (see Section 6)

**Step 2: Acknowledge**
HR acknowledges request within 5 business days

**Step 3: Gather Information**
- HR meets with employee
- Discuss limitations and needs
- Request medical documentation if needed
- Review job duties and requirements

**Step 4: Identify Options**
- Brainstorm possible accommodations
- Employee input valued
- Research effective accommodations
- Consider JAN resources (Job Accommodation Network)

**Step 5: Select Accommodation**
- Choose effective accommodation
- Employer may select among effective options
- Document decision and rationale

**Step 6: Implement**
- Put accommodation in place
- Communicate with relevant parties
- Ensure employee has what they need

**Step 7: Monitor and Adjust**
- Check effectiveness
- Remain open to modifications
- Reassess if circumstances change

### 5.3 Timeframe

While there is no specific legal deadline, Acme aims to:
- Acknowledge requests: 5 business days
- Initial meeting: Within 10 business days
- Decision: Within 30 days of receiving necessary information
- Implementation: As soon as reasonably possible

Complex situations may take longer. Communication is key.

## 6. Requesting an Accommodation

### 6.1 Who May Request

Accommodations may be requested by:
- The employee with a disability
- A family member on behalf of the employee
- A healthcare provider on behalf of the employee
- A supervisor who observes a need (with employee's consent)

### 6.2 How to Request

**No Magic Words Required**

You do not need to say "ADA" or "reasonable accommodation." Simply communicate that you need a change because of a medical condition.

**Examples of Requests:**
- "I'm having trouble getting to work at 8 AM because of my medication. Can I start at 9?"
- "My back condition makes sitting all day painful. Is there something else I can use?"
- "I need time off for my chemotherapy treatments."

**Formal Request Options:**
- Speak to your manager
- Contact HR at hr@acmecorp.com or ext. 4000
- Complete Accommodation Request Form (HR portal)

### 6.3 What to Include

When requesting, provide:
- Description of the limitation
- How it affects your work
- Ideas for accommodations (if you have them)
- Willingness to engage in interactive process

You do NOT need to disclose your diagnosis initially.

## 7. Medical Documentation

### 7.1 When Required

Acme may request medical documentation when:
- Disability is not obvious
- Need for accommodation is not clear
- Documentation is needed to identify effective accommodations

### 7.2 What May Be Requested

Documentation may include:
- Description of impairment
- How it limits work functions
- Duration of condition
- Recommended accommodations
- Healthcare provider credentials

Acme will NOT ask for:
- Complete medical records
- Diagnosis (in most cases)
- Information about unrelated conditions

### 7.3 Who Provides Documentation

Documentation should come from:
- Treating physician
- Mental health provider
- Other appropriate healthcare professional

### 7.4 Employer's Medical Consultation

Acme may consult with a healthcare provider to:
- Clarify documentation
- Understand limitations
- Explore accommodation options

Any examination is at Acme's expense.

### 7.5 Confidentiality

Medical information is:
- Kept separate from personnel file
- Shared only on need-to-know basis
- Protected under GINA and ADA

Supervisors may be informed of:
- Necessary restrictions
- Necessary accommodations
- Emergency procedures if needed

## 8. Evaluating Accommodation Requests

### 8.1 Factors Considered

When evaluating requests, Acme considers:
- Nature of the limitation
- Essential functions of the job
- How the limitation affects job performance
- Possible accommodations
- Effectiveness of accommodations
- Preferences of the employee
- Cost and resources
- Impact on operations

### 8.2 Selecting Accommodations

The employer may choose among effective accommodations:
- Employee preference considered
- Cost-effectiveness considered
- Operational impact considered
- Must be effective for the employee's needs

### 8.3 Undue Hardship Analysis

An accommodation may be denied if it creates undue hardship:
- Significant difficulty or expense
- Considering organization's size and resources
- Nature and cost of accommodation
- Impact on operations

Each request is evaluated individually.

### 8.4 Direct Threat

An accommodation may be denied if the employee poses a direct threat:
- Significant risk of substantial harm
- To self or others
- That cannot be eliminated by accommodation
- Based on objective, medical evidence

## 9. Types of Situations

### 9.1 New Hires

- Do not ask about disabilities before job offer
- After conditional offer, may ask about need for accommodations
- Provide accommodations for onboarding
- Ensure training is accessible

### 9.2 Current Employees

- May request accommodations at any time
- When disability develops or changes
- When limitations change
- When job duties change

### 9.3 Returning from Leave

- Employees returning from medical leave may need accommodations
- Engage in interactive process
- Consider temporary or permanent accommodations
- May need reassignment if cannot perform essential functions

### 9.4 Pregnancy

- Pregnancy-related conditions may qualify as disabilities
- Reasonable accommodations required
- Similar treatment to other temporarily disabled employees
- See also Pregnancy Accommodation Policy

### 9.5 Mental Health Conditions

Mental health conditions may qualify as disabilities:
- Anxiety, depression, PTSD, bipolar disorder, etc.
- Common accommodations:
  - Flexible scheduling
  - Quiet workspace
  - Regular breaks
  - Work from home
  - Modified supervision
  - Leave for treatment

---

## 10. Reassignment

### 10.1 When Considered

Reassignment to a vacant position may be an accommodation when:
- Employee cannot perform current job with any accommodation
- No effective accommodation exists for current position
- An equivalent vacant position exists

### 10.2 Reassignment Process

- Employee must be qualified for the position
- No demotion required, but may accept
- Employer must make reasonable efforts to identify positions
- No "bumping" other employees
- Not required to create a position

## 11. Documentation and Records

### 11.1 What to Document

- Accommodation request

- Accommodations considered
- Decision and rationale

## 12. Roles and Responsibilities

### 12.1 Employees

- Request accommodations when needed
- Participate in interactive process
- Provide medical documentation if requested
- Try accommodations in good faith
- Communicate about effectiveness

### 12.2 Managers

- Recognize potential accommodation requests
- Forward requests to HR
- Do not deny requests independently
- Implement approved accommodations
- Maintain confidentiality
- Monitor effectiveness

### 12.3 Human Resources

- Facilitate interactive process
- Request and maintain medical documentation
- Research accommodation options
- Make accommodation decisions
- Document the process
- Ensure confidentiality
- Monitor compliance

### 12.4 Leadership

- Support accommodation process
- Provide necessary resources
- Model inclusive behavior

## 13. Non-Discrimination and Retaliation

### 13.1 Non-Discrimination

Acme Corporation does not discriminate against qualified individuals with disabilities in any aspect of employment.

### 13.2 Non-Retaliation

Retaliation is prohibited against anyone who:
- Requests an accommodation
- Participates in the accommodation process
- Files a disability discrimination complaint
- Assists in an investigation

Report retaliation to HR or the Ethics Hotline.
