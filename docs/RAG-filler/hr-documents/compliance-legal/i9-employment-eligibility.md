## 1. Purpose

This policy ensures Acme Corporation's compliance with federal law requiring verification of employment eligibility for all employees. The Immigration Reform and Control Act (IRCA) of 1986 requires employers to verify the identity and employment authorization of all individuals hired.

---

## 2. Legal Requirements

### 2.1 What Is Form I-9?

Form I-9 is a federal form used to verify the identity and employment eligibility of individuals hired for employment in the United States. All U.S. employers must complete Form I-9 for every employee hired after November 6, 1986.

### 2.2 Compliance Requirements

Acme Corporation must:
- Complete Form I-9 for every employee
- Meet strict timing requirements
- Accept only valid documents from the Lists of Acceptable Documents
- Retain forms for required periods
- Make forms available for government inspection
- Not discriminate based on citizenship or national origin

### 2.3 Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Violation | Penalty Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Paperwork violations (first offense) | $252 - $2,507 per form |
| Paperwork violations (subsequent) | $252 - $2,507 per form |
| Knowingly hiring unauthorized worker (first) | $627 - $5,016 per worker |
| Knowingly hiring unauthorized worker (second) | $5,016 - $12,537 per worker |
| Knowingly hiring unauthorized worker (third+) | $7,523 - $25,076 per worker |
| Document fraud | Criminal penalties possible |

## 3. Form I-9 Process

### 3.1 Timing Requirements

| Action | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| Section 1 (Employee) | No later than first day of work |
| Section 2 (Employer) | Within 3 business days of first day of work |
| Rehires (within 3 years) | Section 3 completion |
| Reverification | Before authorization expiration |

**Example Timeline:**
- Employee starts Monday, July 7
- Section 1: Must be completed by Monday, July 7
- Section 2: Must be completed by Thursday, July 10

### 3.2 Section 1: Employee Information

**Employee Responsibilities:**
- Complete Section 1 on or before the first day of employment
- Provide full legal name
- Provide date of birth
- Provide address
- Attest to citizenship/immigration status
- Sign and date the form
- Provide Social Security number (voluntary, unless using E-Verify)

**Preparer/Translator:**
- If an employee uses a preparer or translator, that person must complete the Preparer/Translator Certification

### 3.3 Section 2: Employer Review and Verification

**HR Responsibilities:**
- Review original documents in person
- Ensure documents appear genuine
- Ensure documents relate to the employee
- Record document information accurately
- Sign and date the certification
- Retain copies (if desired, consistently)

**Document Examination:**
- Must physically examine original documents
- Cannot specify which documents to present
- Employee chooses from List A, or List B + List C
- Cannot request more documents than required

### 3.4 Section 3: Reverification and Rehires

**Reverification Required When:**
- Employment authorization expires
- Document used for Section 2 expires (if it affects work authorization)

**Rehires:**
- If rehired within 3 years of original I-9
- May complete Section 3 instead of new I-9
- Original I-9 must still be on file

## 4. Acceptable Documents

### List A: Documents That Establish Both Identity and Employment Authorization

Employees may present ONE document from List A:

1. U.S. Passport or U.S. Passport Card
2. Permanent Resident Card or Alien Registration Receipt Card (Form I-551)
3. Foreign passport with temporary I-551 stamp
4. Employment Authorization Document (Form I-766)
5. Foreign passport with Form I-94/I-94A with refugee or asylee status
6. Passport from Federated States of Micronesia or Marshall Islands with Form I-94/I-94A

### List B: Documents That Establish Identity

Employees must present ONE document from List B AND ONE from List C:

**For individuals 18 or older:**
1. Driver's license or state ID card with photo
2. ID card issued by federal, state, or local government with photo
3. School ID card with photo
4. Voter registration card
5. U.S. military card or draft record
6. Military dependent's ID card
7. U.S. Coast Guard Merchant Mariner Document
8. Native American tribal document
9. Canadian driver's license (for Canadian citizens)

**For individuals under 18:**
- School record or report card
- Clinic, doctor, or hospital record
- Day-care or nursery school record

### List C: Documents That Establish Employment Authorization

1. Social Security card (unrestricted)
2. Certification of Birth Abroad (Form FS-545)
3. Certification of Report of Birth (Form DS-1350)
4. Original or certified copy of U.S. birth certificate
5. Native American tribal document
6. U.S. Citizen ID Card (Form I-197)
7. ID Card for Resident Citizen of the United States (Form I-179)
8. Employment authorization document issued by DHS

## 5. E-Verify

### 5.1 What Is E-Verify?

E-Verify is an internet-based system that compares Form I-9 information to government databases to verify employment eligibility.

### 5.2 Acme's E-Verify Requirements

As a federal contractor, Acme Corporation is required to use E-Verify for all new hires.

### 5.3 E-Verify Process

1. Complete Form I-9 normally
2. Enter employee information into E-Verify within 3 business days
3. Receive initial case result:
   - **Employment Authorized:** Process complete
   - **Tentative Nonconfirmation (TNC):** Employee must contest or not contest
   - **Case in Continuance:** Await further result
4. Take action based on result

### 5.4 Tentative Nonconfirmation (TNC)

If E-Verify returns a TNC:
- Notify employee privately
- Employee has 8 federal business days to contest
- If contested, refer case to SSA or DHS
- Cannot take adverse action while case is pending
- If employee doesn't contest, may terminate

### 5.5 E-Verify Timing

| Step | Deadline |
|------|----------|
| Create E-Verify case | Within 3 business days of Section 2 completion |
| Employee notification of TNC | As soon as possible after receiving |
| Employee decision to contest | 8 federal business days |

## 6. Anti-Discrimination Requirements

### 6.1 Prohibited Practices

The following are prohibited:
- Requesting specific documents
- Rejecting valid documents
- Requiring more documents than necessary
- Requiring documents before hire
- Treating employees differently based on citizenship status
- Requiring U.S. citizens to present more or different documents
- Citizenship status discrimination in hiring/firing
- National origin discrimination
- Document abuse
- Retaliation

### 6.2 Best Practices

**Do:**
- Accept any valid, unexpired document from the list
- Apply consistent procedures to all employees
- Train staff on proper I-9 procedures
- Use neutral language in job postings

**Don't:**
- Ask "Are you a U.S. citizen?" (until I-9 time)
- Request specific documents
- Reject documents that reasonably appear genuine
- Refuse to accept receipts when permitted
- Reverify documents unnecessarily

### 6.3 Citizenship Status Discrimination

It is illegal to discriminate based on:
- Citizenship status (citizen vs. non-citizen)
- National origin
- Immigration status (for authorized workers)

Exception: Jobs requiring U.S. citizenship by law (certain federal positions).

## 7. Receipts

### 7.1 When Receipts Are Acceptable

Employees may present receipts for:
- Lost, stolen, or damaged documents (90 days)
- Pending renewal applications
- Refugees with Form I-94 showing refugee status

### 7.2 Receipt Rules

- Receipt valid for 90 days
- Employee must present actual document within 90 days
- Record receipt initially, then update with document information
- Receipts not acceptable for certain documents

---

## 8. Reverification

### 8.1 When Required

Reverification is required when:
- Work authorization expires
- Employment Authorization Document (EAD) expires

### 8.2 When NOT Required

Do not reverify:
- U.S. citizens
- Lawful permanent residents (green card holders)
- List B documents (identity documents)
- Documents that don't expire

### 8.3 Reverification Process

1. Before expiration, remind employee to provide updated documentation
2. Employee presents unexpired document showing continued work authorization
3. Complete Section 3 of Form I-9
4. May use current version of I-9 or continue on original form

### 8.4 Timing

- Begin process 90-120 days before expiration
- Complete Section 3 no later than the expiration date
- If delayed, document the reason

## 9. Record Retention

### 9.1 Retention Requirements

I-9 forms must be retained for:
- **While employed:** Throughout employment
- **After termination:** Later of:
  - 3 years from date of hire, OR
  - 1 year after termination

**Example:**
- Hired: January 1, 2023
- Terminated: June 1, 2025
- 3 years from hire: January 1, 2026
- 1 year after termination: June 1, 2026
- Retention until: June 1, 2026 (later date)

### 9.2 Storage

- May be stored electronically or on paper
- Must be available for inspection within 3 days
- Stored securely with limited access
- May be stored separately from personnel files

### 9.3 Destruction

After retention period:
- Securely destroy forms
- Document destruction date
- Follow records retention policy

## 10. Audits and Inspections

### 10.1 Government Inspections

ICE or other agencies may inspect I-9 records:
- Notice of Inspection (NOI) provides 3 business days to produce records
- May request more time if needed
- Ensure records are organized and accessible

### 10.2 What to Do During an Inspection

1. Verify credentials of inspecting officers
2. Contact Legal and HR immediately
3. Request copy of NOI
4. Gather requested documents within 3 days
5. Cooperate professionally
6. Document the process
7. Do not alter or destroy any records

### 10.3 Internal Audits

HR conducts internal I-9 audits:
- Annually for compliance review
- Upon acquisition or merger
- Before anticipated inspection

**Audit Procedures:**
- Review all forms for completeness
- Check timing compliance
- Verify retention periods
- Correct technical errors
- Document findings and corrections

## 12. Special Situations

### 12.1 Remote Hires

For remote employees:
- Authorized representative must physically examine documents
- May use remote verification with live video (where permitted)
- Or use authorized agent at employee's location
- Acme uses [designated process for remote verification]

### 12.2 Minors

Minors may present:
- Same List A documents as adults, or
- List B documents specifically for minors (school records, medical records)
- Parent/guardian may complete Section 1 if minor cannot

### 12.3 Name Changes

When an employee changes their name:
- Not required to update I-9
- May choose to update with documentation
- New documents should be retained with I-9

### 12.4 Acquired Companies

When acquiring another company:
- Review their I-9 compliance
- May continue their I-9 process or complete new I-9s
- Consult legal counsel
