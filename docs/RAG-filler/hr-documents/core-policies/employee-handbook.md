## Welcome Message

This handbook is designed to provide you with essential information about our company policies, procedures, and benefits. Please read it carefully and keep it accessible for future reference. If you have any questions, your manager or the HR department is always available to help.

Our mission is to deliver innovative technology solutions that transform how businesses operate. We achieve this through our core values:

- **Innovation** - We embrace creative thinking and continuous improvement
- **Integrity** - We conduct business ethically and transparently
- **Collaboration** - We work together to achieve shared goals
- **Excellence** - We strive for the highest quality in everything we do
- **Respect** - We value diversity and treat everyone with dignity

## Section 1: Employment Fundamentals

### 1.1 At-Will Employment

Employment at Acme Corporation is "at-will," meaning that either you or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice. Nothing in this handbook creates an express or implied contract of employment.

### 1.2 Equal Employment Opportunity

Acme Corporation is committed to providing equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other characteristic protected by applicable federal, state, or local law.

### 1.3 Employment Classifications

**Full-Time Employees:** Work 40 hours or more per week and are eligible for all company benefits.

**Part-Time Employees:** Work fewer than 30 hours per week. Part-time employees working 20+ hours are eligible for limited benefits.

**Temporary Employees:** Hired for a specific period or project. Temporary employees are not eligible for company benefits unless required by law.

**Interns:** Students or recent graduates participating in structured learning programs. Internships may be paid or unpaid in accordance with applicable law.

### 1.4 Employment Eligibility Verification

All employees must complete Form I-9 and provide acceptable documentation proving identity and work authorization within three business days of their start date, as required by federal law.

## Section 2: Workplace Conduct

### 2.1 Professional Standards

Employees are expected to conduct themselves professionally at all times. This includes:

- Treating colleagues, clients, and vendors with respect
- Maintaining a positive and collaborative attitude
- Meeting performance expectations and deadlines
- Following all company policies and procedures
- Representing Acme Corporation positively in all interactions

### 2.2 Attendance and Punctuality

Regular attendance is essential to our operations. Employees are expected to:

- Report to work as scheduled
- Notify their supervisor at least one hour before their shift if they will be late or absent
- Provide documentation for absences exceeding three consecutive days
- Not exceed 6 unexcused absences in any rolling 12-month period

Excessive absenteeism or tardiness may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 2.3 Dress Code

Acme Corporation maintains a business casual dress code. Employees should dress in a manner appropriate to their role and any client interactions. Specific guidelines:

- **Business Casual (Standard):** Collared shirts, blouses, slacks, khakis, skirts, closed-toe shoes
- **Business Professional (Client Meetings):** Suits, ties, professional dresses
- **Casual Fridays:** Jeans permitted with appropriate tops; no athletic wear

Department managers may establish additional guidelines as needed.

### 2.4 Workplace Relationships

Acme Corporation does not prohibit consensual romantic relationships between employees. However, relationships between supervisors and their direct or indirect reports must be disclosed to HR within 30 days. HR will work with the parties to ensure appropriate reporting structures and prevent conflicts of interest.

## Section 3: Compensation

### 3.1 Pay Periods

Employees are paid bi-weekly on alternating Fridays. If a payday falls on a holiday, payment will be issued on the preceding business day. The 2025 pay schedule is available on the HR portal.

### 3.2 Direct Deposit

Direct deposit is mandatory for all employees. Employees may split deposits across up to three accounts. Changes to banking information must be submitted through the HR portal at least 5 business days before the next pay date.

### 3.3 Overtime

Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5 times their regular hourly rate for hours worked in excess of 40 per workweek. All overtime must be pre-approved by the employee's manager. Unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action.

### 3.4 Time Reporting

Non-exempt employees must accurately record all time worked using the company's time tracking system (currently Workday). Time entries must be submitted by 5:00 PM on the Monday following the end of each pay period.

Falsification of time records is grounds for immediate termination.

## Section 4: Benefits Overview

### 4.1 Eligibility

Full-time employees are eligible for benefits on the first day of the month following 30 days of employment. Detailed information about each benefit is available in the Benefits Guide and on the HR portal.

### 4.2 Health Insurance

Acme Corporation offers three medical plan options through Blue Cross Blue Shield:

| Plan | Employee Monthly Cost | Employee + Spouse | Employee + Family |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Bronze PPO | $125 | $310 | $485 |
| Silver PPO | $185 | $420 | $625 |
| Gold PPO | $245 | $530 | $780 |

Dental and vision coverage is also available. See the Benefits Guide for complete details.

### 4.3 Retirement

Employees are eligible to participate in the 401(k) plan immediately upon hire. Acme Corporation matches 100% of contributions up to 4% of salary and 50% of contributions between 4% and 6% of salary. The company match vests over four years at 25% per year.

### 4.4 Paid Time Off

Full-time employees accrue PTO based on years of service:

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Accrual |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| 0-2 years | 15 days (120 hours) |
| 3-5 years | 20 days (160 hours) |
| 6-10 years | 25 days (200 hours) |
| 11+ years | 30 days (240 hours) |

PTO may be carried over up to a maximum of 40 hours. Unused PTO beyond 40 hours will be forfeited on January 1 of each year.

### 4.5 Holidays

Acme Corporation observes the following paid holidays in 2025:

- New Year's Day - January 1
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day - January 20
- Presidents' Day - February 17
- Memorial Day - May 26
- Independence Day - July 4
- Labor Day - September 1
- Thanksgiving Day - November 27
- Day After Thanksgiving - November 28
- Christmas Eve - December 24
- Christmas Day - December 25

Additionally, employees receive two floating holidays per year to use at their discretion.

## Section 5: Leave Policies

### 5.1 Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

Eligible employees may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year for qualifying family and medical reasons. To be eligible, employees must have worked for Acme Corporation for at least 12 months and at least 1,250 hours in the preceding 12 months.

### 5.2 Parental Leave

Acme Corporation provides 12 weeks of paid parental leave to all full-time employees following the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child. This leave runs concurrently with FMLA where applicable.

### 5.3 Bereavement Leave

Employees may take paid bereavement leave as follows:

- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 days
- Extended family (grandparent, grandchild, in-law): 3 days
- Close friend or colleague: 1 day

### 5.4 Jury Duty

Employees summoned for jury duty will receive their regular pay for up to 10 days per calendar year. Employees must provide a copy of the jury summons to their manager and HR.

### 5.5 Military Leave

Employees who are members of the uniformed services are entitled to leave in accordance with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA).

## Section 6: Workplace Safety and Security

### 6.1 Safety Commitment

The safety of our employees is our highest priority. All employees are responsible for maintaining a safe work environment and must immediately report any unsafe conditions, accidents, or injuries to their supervisor or the Safety Committee.

### 6.2 Emergency Procedures

Emergency evacuation maps are posted throughout all facilities. Fire drills are conducted quarterly. In case of emergency:

1. Remain calm
2. Follow evacuation routes to designated assembly areas
3. Do not use elevators
4. Account for all team members
5. Do not re-enter the building until authorized

### 6.3 Workplace Violence Prevention

Acme Corporation has zero tolerance for workplace violence. This includes threats, intimidation, physical assault, and possession of weapons on company property. Any employee who witnesses or experiences workplace violence should report it immediately to HR or the confidential hotline at 1-800-555-SAFE.

### 6.4 Building Access

All employees must wear their ID badge visibly while on company premises. Badges should never be shared or loaned. Lost badges must be reported to Security immediately at extension 5555. A replacement fee of $25 will be charged for lost badges.

## Section 7: Technology and Communication

### 7.1 Acceptable Use

Company technology resources, including computers, phones, email, and internet access, are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work duties or violate any company policies.

### 7.2 Email and Communication Standards

All business communications should be professional and appropriate. Employees should assume that any electronic communication may be monitored, accessed, or disclosed. Never use company systems for:

- Harassment or discrimination
- Sharing confidential information without authorization
- Personal commercial activities
- Accessing inappropriate content
- Distributing chain letters or spam

### 7.3 Data Security

Employees are responsible for protecting company and client data. Requirements include:

- Using strong passwords and changing them every 90 days
- Locking computers when away from desks
- Not sharing login credentials
- Reporting suspected security incidents to IT immediately
- Following data classification and handling procedures

### 7.4 Social Media

Employees should use good judgment when using social media. When posting about work-related topics:

- Clearly state that views are your own, not the company's
- Do not disclose confidential or proprietary information
- Do not disparage colleagues, clients, or competitors
- Respect copyright and intellectual property

## Section 8: Performance Management

### 8.1 Performance Reviews

Formal performance reviews are conducted annually in January. Mid-year check-ins occur in July. Reviews assess performance against established goals, core competencies, and company values.

### 8.2 Goal Setting

Employees work with their managers to establish SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) at the beginning of each year. Goals should align with departmental and company objectives.

### 8.3 Feedback and Development

Acme Corporation encourages ongoing feedback between managers and employees. Resources for professional development include:

- LinkedIn Learning access for all employees
- Annual training budget of $2,500 per employee
- Tuition reimbursement program (see separate policy)
- Internal mentorship program
- Leadership development programs

## Section 9: Separation from Employment

### 9.1 Voluntary Resignation

Employees who wish to resign should provide at least two weeks' written notice to their manager. Executives and managers are requested to provide four weeks' notice.

### 9.2 Involuntary Termination

The company may terminate employment at any time for any lawful reason. Common reasons for termination include, but are not limited to:

- Performance deficiencies
- Policy violations
- Misconduct
- Position elimination
- Business restructuring

### 9.3 Exit Process

Departing employees must:

- Return all company property (laptop, phone, badge, keys, etc.)
- Complete an exit interview with HR
- Provide status of ongoing projects
- Update account access and credentials

### 9.4 Final Pay

Final paychecks will be issued on the next regular payday or as required by state law. Accrued, unused PTO will be paid out in accordance with state law.
