## 1. Policy Statement

Protecting confidential information is critical to Acme Corporation's success and competitive advantage. This policy establishes requirements for handling confidential and proprietary information, as well as personal data of employees, clients, and other individuals.

All employees must safeguard confidential information and comply with applicable privacy laws and regulations. Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination, as well as civil and criminal liability.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary workers
- All confidential and proprietary information regardless of format
- All personal data collected, processed, or stored by Acme Corporation
- Information belonging to Acme, its clients, vendors, and partners

---

## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 Confidential Information

Confidential information includes any non-public information that has value to Acme Corporation or could harm the company if disclosed. Categories include:

**Business Information:**
- Financial data, budgets, and forecasts
- Strategic plans and business initiatives
- Pricing and cost information
- Sales data and projections
- Client lists and relationships
- Vendor contracts and terms
- Merger, acquisition, or investment plans

**Technical Information:**
- Source code and software
- Algorithms and formulas
- System architecture and designs
- Security configurations
- Research and development data
- Patents and patent applications
- Technical specifications

**Personnel Information:**
- Employee personal information
- Compensation and benefits data
- Performance evaluations
- Medical information
- Investigation files

**Client Information:**
- Client data and files
- Project details and deliverables
- Contractual terms
- Client communications

### 3.2 Personal Data

Personal data (also called personally identifiable information or PII) means any information that identifies or could identify an individual, including:
- Name, address, phone number, email
- Social Security number, driver's license number
- Financial account information
- Date of birth
- Medical information
- Biometric data
- Online identifiers (IP address, cookies)

### 3.3 Sensitive Personal Data

Certain personal data requires additional protection:
- Social Security numbers
- Financial account numbers
- Health information
- Biometric data
- Racial or ethnic origin
- Political opinions
- Religious beliefs
- Sexual orientation
- Criminal history

## 4. Information Classification

### 4.1 Classification Levels

| Classification | Definition | Examples |
|---------------|------------|----------|
| **Public** | Information intended for public disclosure | Press releases, marketing materials, public filings |
| **Internal** | General business information for internal use | Organization charts, internal newsletters, general policies |
| **Confidential** | Sensitive business information requiring protection | Client data, financial reports, HR records, contracts |
| **Restricted** | Highly sensitive information with limited access | Trade secrets, source code, M&A plans, security data |

### 4.2 Classification Responsibilities

**Information Owners:** Determine classification level and approve access

**All Employees:** Handle information according to its classification

**IT Security:** Implement technical controls for each classification level

### 4.3 Handling Requirements by Classification

| Requirement | Public | Internal | Confidential | Restricted |
|------------|--------|----------|--------------|------------|
| Storage | Any | Company systems | Encrypted/secure | Encrypted, access-logged |
| Transmission | Any | Company systems | Encrypted | Encrypted, verified recipient |
| Printing | Allowed | Allowed | Retrieve promptly | Approval required |
| Disposal | Regular trash | Recycle/shred | Shred/secure delete | Certified destruction |
| Labeling | Optional | Optional | Required | Required |

## 5. Handling Confidential Information

### 5.1 General Requirements

All employees must:
- Access confidential information only when needed for job duties
- Not share confidential information with unauthorized persons
- Not discuss confidential matters in public places
- Protect physical and electronic documents
- Report suspected breaches immediately

### 5.2 Electronic Information

When handling electronic confidential information:
- Use only company-approved systems and devices
- Encrypt sensitive files and emails
- Use secure file sharing (not personal email or cloud storage)
- Lock computers when away from desk
- Use strong passwords and multi-factor authentication
- Do not forward confidential emails without authorization

### 5.3 Physical Documents

When handling physical documents:
- Store in locked cabinets or secure areas
- Do not leave unattended in common areas
- Retrieve from printers immediately
- Shred when no longer needed
- Do not remove from premises without authorization
- Return all documents when leaving employment

### 5.4 Verbal Communications

Protect verbal information by:
- Not discussing confidential matters in public
- Using private offices or conference rooms
- Being aware of surroundings on calls
- Not using speakerphone in open areas
- Being cautious in elevators and lobbies

### 5.5 Working Remotely

Additional requirements for remote work:
- Use VPN for all company access
- Ensure home workspace is secure
- Position screens away from others' view
- Do not use public Wi-Fi without VPN
- Store documents securely
- Dispose of documents properly (shred)

## 6. Personal Data Privacy

### 6.1 Privacy Principles

Acme Corporation follows these data privacy principles:

**Lawfulness:** Collect and process data only with legal basis

**Purpose Limitation:** Use data only for specified, legitimate purposes

**Data Minimization:** Collect only necessary data

**Accuracy:** Keep data accurate and up to date

**Storage Limitation:** Retain data only as long as needed

**Security:** Protect data with appropriate measures

**Accountability:** Document compliance and be able to demonstrate it

### 6.2 Legal Bases for Processing

Personal data may be processed when:
- Required for employment relationship
- Required to fulfill a contract
- Required by law
- Individual has given consent
- Necessary for legitimate business interests (balanced against individual rights)

### 6.3 Employee Data

Acme Corporation collects employee data for:
- Payroll and benefits administration
- Performance management
- Compliance with legal requirements
- Workplace safety and security
- Business operations

Employee data is protected by:
- Access controls limiting who can view records
- Encryption of sensitive data
- Secure storage and transmission
- Retention policies and secure disposal

### 6.4 Client Data

When handling client data:
- Follow contractual requirements
- Implement required security measures
- Limit access to authorized personnel
- Report incidents to clients as required
- Return or destroy data at contract end

### 6.5 Individual Rights

Individuals may have rights regarding their personal data:
- Right to know what data is collected
- Right to access their data
- Right to correct inaccurate data
- Right to delete data (in some circumstances)
- Right to restrict processing
- Right to data portability
- Right to object to processing

Employees should direct individual requests to the Privacy Office.

## 7. Data Breach Response

### 7.1 What Is a Data Breach?

A data breach is any unauthorized access, disclosure, acquisition, or loss of personal data or confidential information. Examples include:
- Lost or stolen laptop or phone
- Unauthorized access to systems
- Accidental email to wrong recipient
- Ransomware or malware attack
- Physical theft of documents
- Employee snooping

### 7.2 Reporting Requirements

**Immediate Reporting:** All suspected breaches must be reported immediately to:
- IT Security: security@acmecorp.com, ext. 5100
- Privacy Office: privacy@acmecorp.com
- Your manager

Do not attempt to investigate on your own.

### 7.3 Response Process

Upon receiving a breach report:
1. **Contain:** Stop ongoing breach, preserve evidence
2. **Assess:** Determine scope, data affected, cause
3. **Notify:** Alert affected individuals and regulators as required
4. **Remediate:** Fix vulnerabilities, improve controls
5. **Document:** Complete incident report, lessons learned

### 7.4 Legal Notifications

Depending on the breach, notifications may be required to:
- Affected individuals
- State attorneys general
- Federal regulators
- Clients (per contract requirements)

The Legal Department manages notification requirements.

## 8. Third-Party Information

### 8.1 Receiving Third-Party Information

When receiving confidential information from third parties:
- Only accept through authorized channels
- Ensure proper agreements are in place (NDA, contract)
- Handle according to agreed-upon requirements
- Limit access to those who need to know
- Do not use for unauthorized purposes

### 8.2 Sharing Information with Third Parties

Before sharing confidential information:
- Obtain appropriate approvals
- Ensure NDA or contract is in place
- Share only what is necessary
- Use secure transmission methods
- Document what was shared

### 8.3 Non-Disclosure Agreements

NDAs are required before sharing or receiving confidential information with:
- Potential clients (pre-contract)
- Vendors and contractors
- Potential partners or investors
- Any party outside Acme Corporation

Legal Department maintains NDA templates and reviews agreements.

## 9. Intellectual Property

### 9.1 Company Ownership

All intellectual property created during employment belongs to Acme Corporation:
- Inventions and discoveries
- Software and code
- Documentation and manuals
- Processes and methods
- Creative works

### 9.2 Prior Inventions

Employees must disclose prior inventions at time of hire. Disclosed inventions remain employee property.

### 9.3 Protection Requirements

Employees must:
- Promptly disclose inventions to the company
- Assist in obtaining patents and protections
- Not disclose IP to unauthorized parties
- Not use company IP for personal purposes

## 10. After Employment Ends

### 10.1 Continuing Obligations

Confidentiality obligations continue after employment ends:
- Never disclose trade secrets
- Return all confidential materials
- Delete company data from personal devices
- Comply with any non-compete or non-solicitation agreements

### 10.2 Exit Process

Upon termination, employees must:
- Return all company property
- Return or destroy all confidential documents
- Confirm deletion of company data from personal devices
- Certify compliance with confidentiality obligations

### 10.3 Reminders to Departing Employees

HR provides departing employees with:
- Copy of confidentiality agreements
- Reminder of continuing obligations
- Information about restricted activities
- Contact for questions

## 11. Specific Data Types

### 11.1 Social Security Numbers

Special protections for SSNs:
- Collect only when legally required
- Never include in emails unless encrypted
- Limit access to authorized personnel only
- Never post or display
- Use alternative identifiers when possible

### 11.2 Financial Information

Credit card and bank account data must be:
- Protected in accordance with PCI DSS (for credit cards)
- Encrypted in transit and at rest
- Accessed only by authorized personnel
- Retained only as long as necessary

### 11.3 Health Information

Medical and health data (PHI) must be:
- Protected in accordance with HIPAA (where applicable)
- Stored separately from general personnel files
- Accessed only for legitimate purposes
- Shared only with authorization

### 11.4 Background Check Data

Background check information must be:
- Used only for employment decisions
- Stored securely and separately
- Retained per legal requirements
- Disposed of securely
