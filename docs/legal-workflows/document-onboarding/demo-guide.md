# Demo Guide: Document Onboarding

## Video goal

Show that Document Onboarding turns raw legal documents into a reviewable first-pass legal assessment. The video should make the workflow feel practical, not theoretical.

## Suggested video length

Four to six minutes.

## Opening talk track

“This workflow is for the moment a legal team receives a document and needs to understand it quickly. Instead of starting with a blank page, we upload the document, let the system classify it, run specialist review, and then pause for attorney approval before finalizing the report.”

## Screen-by-screen outline

### 1. Workflow landing page

- Point out the benefits brief.
- Explain the upload area and that the workflow accepts individual documents or small packets.
- Say that this is a first-pass intake workflow, not the final legal judgment.

### 2. Upload a high-risk SaaS agreement

- Use `test-documents/scenario-01-saas-msa-high-risk.md`.
- Explain that the document looks like a commercial contract but has privacy, IP, security, and indemnity issues.
- Mention that the workflow should route to multiple specialists, not just a contract reviewer.

### 3. Watch stage progression

- Call out the stages:
  - reading metadata,
  - routing to specialists,
  - specialist review,
  - synthesis,
  - attorney review,
  - final report.
- Keep this section short; the point is observability and confidence.

### 4. Attorney review gate

- Explain that this is the control point.
- Show that the reviewer can approve, reject, or modify findings.
- Say explicitly: “The system is producing a structured review surface. The lawyer still decides what becomes work product.”

### 5. Final report

- Look for the executive summary, risk matrix, key issues, and recommended next actions.
- Explain what a partner or client could use immediately.
- Point out which downstream workflow might come next, such as contract redlining or compliance audit.

## What to emphasize

- Fast first-pass understanding
- Cross-domain issue spotting
- Structured output instead of raw notes
- Attorney control before finalization
- Downstream routing into deeper legal workflows

## What not to overclaim

- Do not present this as legal advice by itself.
- Do not claim it replaces attorney review.
- Do not claim every issue is guaranteed to be found.
- Do not imply the synthetic test documents are real client documents.

## Closing talk track

“The value here is not that the system magically finishes the legal matter. The value is that the team gets from unknown document to structured legal judgment much faster, with the right specialists involved and a human review gate before final output.”
