## Office and Light Assembly Lease

Landlord: Harbor Park Properties LLC  
Tenant: Greenline Robotics, Inc.  
Premises: Suite 400 and attached light assembly bay, 1180 Harbor Park Drive, Portland, Oregon  
Rentable area: approximately 18,500 square feet  
Commencement date: June 1, 2026  
Initial term: seven years  
Governing law: Oregon

## 1. Use

Tenant may use the premises for executive offices, software development, robotics testing, limited light assembly, storage of components, and related lawful uses. Tenant may not use the premises for hazardous manufacturing, chemical processing, battery cell production, or any use that increases Landlord’s insurance premiums unless Landlord gives prior written consent.

## 2. Base rent

Base rent will be $42.00 per rentable square foot per year, payable monthly in advance. Base rent will increase by 3.5% on each anniversary of the commencement date.

## 3. Operating expenses and CAM

Tenant will pay its proportionate share of taxes, insurance, utilities, repairs, maintenance, management fees, security, landscaping, parking areas, roof maintenance, HVAC maintenance, capital improvements required by law, and other operating expenses for the project. Landlord will provide an annual reconciliation. Tenant may audit Landlord’s books once per year if Tenant gives written notice within 30 days after receiving the reconciliation.

## 4. Maintenance and repairs

Tenant is responsible for routine maintenance and repairs inside the premises, including plumbing serving the premises, lighting, fixtures, dock equipment, floor coverings, interior walls, and supplemental HVAC serving the assembly bay. Landlord is responsible for structural elements, the roof, exterior walls, and common utility systems, except to the extent damage is caused by Tenant or Tenant’s invitees.

## 5. Environmental matters

Tenant will comply with all environmental laws and will not use, store, handle, release, or dispose of hazardous materials except ordinary office and robotics testing materials used in compliance with law. Tenant will indemnify Landlord from all claims, cleanup costs, fines, damages, and expenses arising from hazardous materials brought onto the project by Tenant or Tenant’s invitees. This indemnity survives lease expiration or termination.

## 6. Insurance

Tenant will maintain commercial general liability insurance of at least $2,000,000 per occurrence and $5,000,000 aggregate, property insurance covering Tenant’s property and improvements, workers’ compensation insurance, cyber insurance if Tenant connects equipment to building systems, and umbrella liability insurance of at least $5,000,000. Policies must name Landlord and its lender as additional insureds where applicable.

## 7. Assignment and subletting

Tenant may not assign the lease or sublet all or part of the premises without Landlord’s prior written consent, which may not be unreasonably withheld. A transfer of more than 35% of Tenant’s voting equity, merger, reorganization, or sale of substantially all assets is deemed an assignment requiring consent. Landlord may recapture the premises if Tenant requests consent to assign or sublet more than 40% of the premises.

## 8. Alterations

Tenant may not make alterations without Landlord’s consent. Tenant must remove specialty improvements, cabling, lab benches, robotics testing fixtures, and supplemental HVAC equipment at the end of the term if Landlord requires removal. Tenant must repair damage caused by removal.

## 9. Casualty

If the premises are damaged by fire or other casualty, Landlord will repair the premises if repair can reasonably be completed within 240 days. If repair will take longer, either party may terminate the lease. Rent abates only for the portion of the premises that Tenant cannot reasonably use.

## 10. Default

Failure to pay rent within five days after notice is an event of default. Failure to perform non-monetary obligations within 20 days after notice is an event of default, unless the obligation cannot reasonably be cured in that time and Tenant begins cure promptly. Upon default, Landlord may terminate the lease, recover possession, accelerate rent, recover reletting costs, and pursue other remedies.

## 11. Renewal option

Tenant has one five-year renewal option if Tenant is not in default and gives written notice at least 12 months before the initial term expires. Renewal rent will equal the fair market rental value as determined by Landlord, subject to Tenant’s right to object within 15 days.
