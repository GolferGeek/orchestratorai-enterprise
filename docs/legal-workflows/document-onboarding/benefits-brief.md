# Benefits Brief: Document Onboarding

## Executive summary

Document Onboarding gives a legal team a dependable first read of unfamiliar documents. Instead of asking a lawyer to manually triage every new contract, policy, lease, employment document, diligence packet, or litigation document from a blank page, the workflow classifies the material, extracts key metadata, routes issues to legal specialists, synthesizes the results, and pauses for attorney review before creating final work product.

The immediate value is speed, consistency, and better issue spotting. The deeper value is that the firm gets a repeatable intake layer for legal work: every document starts with a structured record of what it is, who it concerns, what risks it contains, and what should happen next.

## Who it helps

- **Law firm partners** get a faster way to understand what arrived and what needs attention.
- **Associates and paralegals** get an organized first-pass memo instead of starting from raw documents.
- **In-house legal teams** get consistent intake across commercial, employment, privacy, compliance, litigation, and real-estate matters.
- **Business stakeholders** get clearer next steps without needing to interpret legal source material themselves.

## Problems it solves

- Incoming documents are often misrouted, under-scoped, or reviewed too narrowly.
- Early review often depends on whoever happens to see the document first.
- Cross-domain issues are easy to miss when a document looks like only one legal category.
- Partners and clients need concise summaries before the detailed review is complete.
- Teams waste time turning unstructured documents into structured work queues.

## What the workflow does

1. **Reads the document packet.** Extracts document type, parties, dates, signature status, governing law, and other key metadata.
2. **Classifies the material.** Determines whether the document is primarily commercial, corporate, employment, IP, privacy, compliance, litigation, real estate, or mixed.
3. **Routes to specialists.** Runs parallel legal specialist review across the relevant domains.
4. **Synthesizes findings.** Combines specialist observations into one coherent assessment rather than a pile of disconnected notes.
5. **Pauses for attorney review.** Lets a professional approve, reject, or modify the analysis before final output.
6. **Produces a final report.** Creates an executive summary, risk matrix, key issues, recommendations, and suggested downstream actions.

## Why it is valuable

### It shortens time to first judgment

The workflow does not need to finish the entire legal matter to be useful. Its job is to answer the first critical questions quickly: What is this? Who is involved? What risks are obvious? What should we do next?

### It improves cross-domain issue spotting

A SaaS agreement may contain privacy, IP, indemnity, data security, and compliance issues. A separation agreement may implicate employment, confidentiality, litigation, tax, and restrictive covenant concerns. The workflow is designed to surface that mixed risk profile early.

### It creates a consistent intake record

Every document gets the same core treatment: classification, metadata, specialist analysis, synthesis, human review, and a final report. That consistency matters for quality control, delegation, and knowledge management.

### It keeps lawyers in control

The workflow has a human review gate before final work product is generated. That makes it appropriate for professional legal use: the system accelerates review, but the lawyer remains responsible for judgment.

## Best-fit use cases

- New client document intake
- First-pass commercial contract review
- Matter-opening packets
- Mixed legal document packets
- Lease, policy, employment, and compliance document triage
- Preparing a partner, client, or business stakeholder briefing
- Deciding whether a document should move into a deeper workflow such as contract redlining, compliance audit, discovery review, or due diligence

## Demo promise

In a short demo, a user should see the workflow take an unfamiliar document, explain what it is, show which specialists reviewed it, pause for human review, and produce a practical memo that tells a legal professional where to focus.

## Positioning line

Document Onboarding is the legal team’s structured first read: fast enough for intake, broad enough for issue spotting, and controlled enough for professional review.
