# LinkedIn Post — March 23, 2026

Every enterprise I talk to is hitting the same wall with AI agents and workflows.

**SaaS** looks great until you realize it's designed for the middle. You pay full price, use a fraction, and you're locked into their stack and their LLM decisions. It can't deliver what your organization specifically needs.

**Off-the-shelf products** have the same problem — just deployed differently. They built it for a market, not your situation. And AI is moving so fast their tech stack is outdated before you've finished onboarding.

**Building from scratch** is the right instinct. But where do you find the expertise? Consulting companies move slow, charge a lot, and you spend months on infrastructure before your first agent even runs.

After years of building enterprise software, I kept seeing the same pattern: every organization reinvents the same authentication, observability, and orchestration layer before they can do anything interesting.

So we built a starter kit.

Drop the repository. Get the database running. Authentication works. Agents work. Now shape it into the nuanced solution your organization actually needs — not a generic product forced to fit.

Table stakes agents are a database row. Complex workflows are structured pipelines. Your data stays on your infrastructure. And with the way AI-assisted coding is evolving, your team can genuinely build and evaluate agents in days.

We're calling it OrchestratorAI. It's not SaaS. It's not a finished product. It's the starting point for the build you were going to do anyway.
