# LinkedIn Post — Geeks Love Complexity

**Author:** GolferGeek
**Date:** 2026-03-25
**Platform:** LinkedIn

---

## Post

Geeks love complexity. Or at least, we don't mind it.

For a year and a half, I've been building iterations of OrchestratorAI. Each version added more capability. Simple agents with a LangGraph backend and a prompt-and-response frontend became a full composable framework where you could build agents in a dozen different ways.

And every single time I showed it to someone, they looked lost.

Not because they weren't smart. Because I was solving for capability when I should have been solving for clarity.

Here's the thing nobody told me early enough: when I started building this, most people didn't know what AI agents were. They still barely do. I knew that, and I kept building anyway. I told myself the answer was to harden the platform, add more functionality, keep pace with every new avenue AI opened up. More features. More power. More options.

More complexity.

Every demo I gave was too much. Every explanation required another explanation. I couldn't simplify it enough for the people I was talking to, and that wasn't their failure. That was mine.

Two things finally cracked it open for me. A marketing person who kept pushing me: "What is the actual business problem you're solving?" And a sharp new intern who just... looked lost while I was explaining things. Smart kid. Clearly engaged. Completely lost.

That's when it hit me.

This isn't a finished product you buy off the shelf. This is a starting platform for companies that are stuck — the ones where SaaS won't work for their specific idea and building from scratch is too expensive. You get a launchpad. You build from there. We're not selling a destination. We're selling a head start.

When I finally said it that simply, the intern looked at me and said:

"Well, how long did that take you to figure out?"

I laughed and told him, "It's been a year and a half-- and what makes you think I've actually figured anything out?"  I didn't have the heart to tell him that I just finished up and app to help clients "play" with A2A security protocols.  No way would I have been able to explain that to him.

His point has been well-taken though.  I've super-simplified the message on the landing page of orchestratorai.io and we are hiding all of the complexity from users when they first come in.  We are consistently presenting a unified message-- this is a starter platform.  We can get you started in a day.  You can't get to the advanced stuff without wanting to.

If you're building something and nobody understands what you're doing — the problem probably isn't the people you're talking to.
